<h2>Art and Literature Art Lesson Plans</h2>	<p class="postmeta">
Posted <span class="metadate">on January 9, 2010</span> <span class="metaauthor">by <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.deepspacesparkle.com/author/patty/" title="">Patty Palmer</a></span><span class="metacomment"> / <a href="http://www.deepspacesparkle.com/2010/01/09/art-and-literature-art-lessons-for-kids/#comments" title="Comment on Art and Literature Art Lesson Plans">19 comment</a></span>	</p>
<div class="postcontent">
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11057" alt="Art-and-Literature-1" src="http://www.deepspacesparkle.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/550x716xArt-and-Literature-1.jpg.pagespeed.ic._aJ4KfZTy6.jpg" width="550" height="716"/></p>
<p><em>Picture books and art projects go hand-in-hand. These four art lessons inspired by popular children&#8217;s books, contains full-color tutorials, prep and set-up tips, drawing handouts, templates and student galleries.</em></p>
<p><em><strong><span style="color: #7716e8;">The lessons include:</span></strong></em></p>
<p><strong><span style="color: #c10000;">&#8220;Pie in the Sky&#8221; by Lois Ehlert:</span></strong>Lower grade students create painted paper and use templates to create stunning birds. Great lesson for color, composition and cutting skills.</p>
<p><strong><span style="color: #7716e8;">&#8220;Splat the Cat&#8221; by Rob Scotton:</span></strong> Perfect for first or second grade, children will love reading this delightful book and creating their own &#8220;Splat&#8221; using tempera paint and chalk pastel. Scraps of paper turn into Splat&#8217;s bookbag and school supplies.</p>
<p><strong><span style="color: #c10000;">&#8220;Do You Have a Hat&#8221; by Eileen Spinelli:</span></strong>Use one of the six templates (sombrero, wool hat, beach, top hat, sun hat, crown and cowboy) to draw a favorite hat. Watercolor paints and crafts unite in this creative art lessons for little ones.</p>
<p><strong><span style="color: #7716e8;">&#8220;Saturday Night at the Dinosaur Stomp&#8221; by Carol Diggory Shields and Illustrated by Scott Nash:</span></strong>Use the two drawing handouts to guide the children through a series of steps of drawing a dancing dino and then explore cool watercolor techniques to finish the project.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11058" alt="ArtandLit1preview" src="http://www.deepspacesparkle.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/ArtandLit1preview.jpg" width="600" height="540"/></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p style="text-align: center;"><em>How to Draw a Dancing Dino #1</em></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>How to Draw a Dancing Dino #2</em></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Birds in a Cherry Tree</em></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><strong><span style="color: #7716e8;">Templates Included:</span></strong></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Bird wings, body, head and tail</em></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Woolen Hat</em></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Book for Splat</em></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Magician&#8217;s Hat</em></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Beach, Sun or Garden Hat</em></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Sombrero</em></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Crown</em></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Cowboy</em></p>
