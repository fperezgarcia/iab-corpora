					  <p>In fact, arts  and entertainment are as much a part of the Island&rsquo;s <a href="/history/" class="biegedarker">history</a> as its port and  Victorian architecture. From theater, music and dance, in historic and traditional  settings, to reggae, rock, country and the oldies in the city&rsquo;s nightspots,  Galveston sparkles at night.</p>

					  <p><a href="../postofficestreettour/" class="biegedarker">Postoffice Street Arts &amp; Entertainment District</a> is nestled in the famous <a href="../downtowntour/" class="biegedarker">Strand National Historic Landmark District</a>, only three short blocks from The Strand. This district is home to quaint <a href="../artgalleries/" class="biegedarker">galleries</a> that feature various types of art from local, national and internationally recognized artists. </p><p>Every six weeks, the art galleries welcome Island visitors and Galvestonians alike into their galleries for ArtWalk; an event that provides visitors with wine and cheese while leading them through the historic streets and into boutiques and galleries.</p>
					<p><a href="../thegrand/" class="biegedarker">The Grand 1894 Opera House</a> presents  a year-round performing arts schedule featuring international stars of stage  and screen, Broadway musicals, dance, music and comedy productions. Called &ldquo;one  of the finest theaters in the world&rdquo; by veteran actor Hal Holbrook, The Grand  is listed on the National Register of Historic Places and was designated the  &ldquo;official opera house of the State of Texas&rdquo; by the 73rd legislature. Past  performers have included Bill Cosby, Gregory Peck, James Earl Jones, Bernadette  Peters, and Willie Nelson.
