        <h1>
            <span id="dnn_ctr379_dnnTITLE_lblTitle" class="TitleHead">Where do I get an Arizona business license to operate my business?</span>



        </h1>
    </div>
    <div class="clear_float"></div>
</div>
<div class="c_content">
    <div id="dnn_ctr379_ContentPane" class="Normal c_contentpane">
    <!-- Start_Module_379 --><div id="dnn_ctr379_ModuleContent" class="ModDNNHTMLC">
	<div id="dnn_ctr379_HtmlModule_lblContent" class="Normal">
	In Arizona, not all businesses are required to have a license and some businesses are required to have more than one license. There is not a "generic" state business license.&nbsp;&nbsp; The basic types of licensing in Arizona are described in our <A href="/Business/LicensingGuide.aspx">Licensing Guide</A>.

            <span id="dnn_ctr825_dnnTITLE_lblTitle" class="TitleHead">Business Basics in Arizona</span>



        </h1>
    </div>

<div class="c_content">
    <div id="dnn_ctr825_ContentPane" class="Normal c_contentpane">
    <!-- Start_Module_825 --><div id="dnn_ctr825_ModuleContent" class="ModDNNHTMLC">
	<div id="dnn_ctr825_HtmlModule_lblContent" class="Normal">
	<p><a target="_blank" href="/LinkClick.aspx?fileticket=BPeFIxQ09vY%3d&amp;tabid=60">A Guide to Taxes for Arizona Businesses</a> is designed to help Arizona businesses comply with the state’s basic tax and licensing requirements.         <h1>
            <span id="dnn_ctr811_dnnTITLE_lblTitle" class="TitleHead">Transaction Privilege Tax (TPT)/Licensing</span>



        </h1>
    </div>
    <div class="clear_float"></div>
</div>
<div class="c_content">
    <div id="dnn_ctr811_ContentPane" class="Normal c_contentpane">
    <!-- Start_Module_811 --><div id="dnn_ctr811_ModuleContent" class="ModDNNHTMLC">
	<div id="dnn_ctr811_HtmlModule_lblContent" class="Normal">
	<p>If you are selling a product or engaging in a service subject to transaction privilege tax, you will most likely need to obtain the state transaction privilege tax (TPT) license from the Arizona Department of Revenue (commonly referred to as a sales tax, resale, wholesale, vendor or tax license) and a transaction privilege tax or business/occupational license from the city(ies) in which you are based and/or operate.&#160; Details on TPT Licensing can be found on our <a href="/Business/TransactionPrivilegeTax.aspx">TPT page</a>.</p>
<ul>
    <li><a href="/Business/TransactionPrivilegeTax/TPTRates.aspx">Transaction Privilege (Sales) Tax Rate Tables</a> contain rates for Transaction Privilege and other taxes imposed by the State of Arizona, the counties, and most Arizona cities.</li>
    <li><span id="dnn_ctr826_dnnTITLE_lblTitle" class="TitleHead LabelEditOverClass"><a href="/Business/TransactionPrivilegeTax/TaxFactoring.aspx">Transaction Privilege (Sales) Tax Factoring</a> - If you do not seperately state the transaction privilege tax on your customer's invoice or contract, you may <strong>FACTOR </strong>the tax for purposes of reporting the tax to the state and cities.&#160; See the <a href="/Business/TransactionPrivilegeTax/TaxFactoring.aspx">Tax Factor page</a> for details or download an <a target="_blank" href="/LinkClick.aspx?fileticket=jiPsacmY6h4%3d&amp;tabid=60">Automatic Factoring Worksheet</a> (Excel).</span></li>
    <li><span class="TitleHead LabelEditOverClass"><span id="dnn_ctr826_dnnTITLE_lblTitle" class="TitleHead LabelEditOverClass">TPT Due Dates - <a target="_blank" href="/LinkClick.aspx?fileticket=_FdQMJPSeSw%3d&amp;tabid=60">Download Calendar</a></span></span></li>
</ul>
</div>

</div><!-- End_Module_811 --></div>
