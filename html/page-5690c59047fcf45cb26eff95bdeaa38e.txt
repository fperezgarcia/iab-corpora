        	<span class="paragraph-0">
				<p>DOWNTOWN LOS ANGELES -When it comes to enjoying a night at the <a href="http://www.ladowntownnews.com/opinion/downtown-s-modern-movie-era/article_67ba4b22-cd24-11e1-90bb-001a4bcf887a.html" target="_blank">movies in Downtown</a> Los Angeles, people dont have to put up with the confines of four walls, a roof and paying $6 for a small popcorn.</p>
			</span>
        	<span class="paragraph-1">
				<p>This summer, Downtown movie fans can take advantage of four outdoor movie series. The events offer everything from classics to comedies to family friendly fare.</p>
			</span>
                    <p>Some even throw live music and food trucks into the mix, and one series is free. Here is a rundown of the al fresco film action.</p>
                
                    <p align="center"><strong>Friday Flicks</strong></p>
                
                    <p>The granddaddy of the Downtown outdoor film scene is at <a href="http://www.laparks.org/pershingsquare/friday-night-flicks.html" target="_blank">Pershing Square</a>, where the free Friday Night Flicks series has been going on since 2008. This years lineup launched May 3 and continues through October with more than 25 films.</p>
                
                    <p>So far only the lineup through June 28 has been announced. The month of May features 1980s flashback movies. On May 17 the 1985 comedy-drama <em>Desperately Seeking Susan</em> will run. Screening May 24 is John Hughes 1986 comedy <em>Pretty in Pink</em>, starring a young Molly Ringwald. It also features Jon Cryer, Andrew McCarthy, James Spader and even Andrew Dice Clay.</p>
                
                    <p>The following week is a further flashback, all the way to 1950s, thanks to Michael J. Fox and the time-traveling DeLorean in <em>Back to the Future</em>.</p>
                
                    <p>The vibe changes in June with what park officials tout as camp films. Thats wacky camp, not summer camp. There is also plenty of music in movies including <em>The Adventures of</em> <em>Priscilla, Queen of the Desert</em>, about two drag queens and a transsexual who get a gig in the desert. It screens June 14. The John Travolta-Olivia Newton John classic <em>Grease</em> is on June 21.</p>
                
                    <p>Films at Pershing Square are shown on a 40-by-20-foot inflatable screen and Downtowners are invited to bring a blanket, lawn chair and picnic baskets .</p>
                
                    <p>Movies start at 8 p.m. and dogs are welcome, as long as they are on a leash and dont sing too loud during Youre the One That I Want.</p>
                
                    <p> <em>Pershing Square is at 533 S. Olive St., (213) 847-4970 or <a href="http://laparks.org/pershingsquare">laparks.org/pershingsquare</a>.</em></p>
                
                    <p align="center"><strong>Park and View</strong></p>
                
                    <p>It seemed the credits were set to roll last summer when Morgan Night announced he would shut down the Devils Night Drive-In, which had taken place for seven years on a roof at Fourth Street and Broadway<strong>.</strong></p>
                
                    <p>The series did end, but like all those zombies that the kids love these days, it was soon <a href="http://www.ladowntownnews.com/news/new-drive-in-opens-oct/article_b68f54f8-1717-11e2-a0ea-001a4bcf887a.html" target="_blank">reborn</a> after being purchased. It was renamed the Electric Dusk Drive-In.</p>
                
                    <p>Then, unlike the zombies, it was forced to move to the Fashion District. Now <a href="http://www.electricduskdrivein.com/#!films/cilf" target="_blank">Electric Dusk</a> runs at City Market of Los Angeles, an old produce warehouse complex.</p>
                
                    <p>Its great, were really liking it, said drive-in owner Eric Heusinger of their new, bigger location, where they can hold 110 cars, up from the previous 80.</p>
                
                    <p>The summer season is sporadic, though films are usually shown on the weekends through Aug. 31. They range from classics to action to cult hits and mostly run at 8 p.m. The screen is 24-by-18 foot.</p>
                
                    <p>The next installment is May 26 with the 1950 <em>All About Eve</em>, which stars Bette Davis and won six Oscars. Things are completely different June 9 with the 1995 comedy <em>To Wong Foo, Thanks for Everything! Julie Newmar</em>. It features Wesley Snipes, John Leguizamo and Patrick Swayze as drag queens traveling across the country. They end up stranded in a small town and wackiness ensues.</p>
                
                    <p>A good time to take the family to the drive-in will be July 13, when <em>The Princess Bride</em> is on screen, and July 27 when <em>E.T.</em> finds his way to Downtown. The closing film, on Aug. 31, is Alfred Hitchcocks <em>Vertigo</em>.</p>
                
                    <p>Although the Electric Dusk Drive-In is a drive-in, a car is not a requirement. An Astroturf area has room for about 225 people. There is also a snack shack selling hot dogs, burgers and drinks.</p>
                
                    <p> <em>At 1000 San Julian St., (818) 653-8591 or <a href="http://electricduskdrivein.com">electricduskdrivein.com</a>.</em></p>
                
                    <p align="center"><strong>Street Style</strong></p>
                
                    <p>The Street Food Cinema combines three things most people love: food trucks, music and movies. The venue-hopping series returns for its second year, running May 25-Sept. 21.</p>
                
                    <p>The majority of the weekly films take place at Exposition Park. Other Downtown locations include Grand Hope Park and Los Angeles State Historic Park.</p>
                
                    <p>Exposition Park is kind of our home base, but we love Downtown, said Heather Hope-Allison, one of the event producers. Well be at Grand Hope Park for Family Night.</p>
                
                    <p>Doors open at 5:30 p.m. for every event. There is lawn seating for nearly 1,800 people (bring your own lawn chairs and blankets). There are also usually more than a dozen food trucks. A live band performs at 6:30 p.m. and the movie starts at 8 p.m.</p>
                
                    <p>The season begins May 25 with the coming of age hit <em>Stand by Me</em> at Exposition Park. For fans of Corey Feldman (stop laughing), the night will be a double treat, since the actor is also the opening act and will be performing with his band.</p>
                
                    <p>On June 1, Tom Cruise dances in his underwear in 1983s <em>Risky Business</em>. It also screens at Exposition Park.</p>
                
                    <p>As Hope-Allison noted, its family time at Grand Hope Park in South Park on June 8, with the animated <em>Escape From Planet Earth</em>.</p>
                
                    <p><strong>Update</strong>: From Aug. 3 to Sept. 14 Street Food Cinema will also host free family-friendly films every Saturday at Grand Park in the Civic Center.</p>
                
                    <p>The series shifts to Los Angeles State Historic Park on July 27 with the absurd comedy <em>Monty Python &amp; The Holy Grail</em>. Another highlight is <em>Skyfall</em>, with the blond Bond back at Exposition Park Aug. 17.The Street Food Cinema is also dog friendly.</p>
                
                    <p> <em>Exposition Park is at 700 Exposition Park Drive. Los Angeles State Historic Park is at 1245 N. Spring St. Grand Hope Park is at 919 S. Grand Ave. At <a href="http://streetfoodcinema.com">streetfoodcinema.com</a>.</em></p>
                
                    <p align="center"><strong>Hear It, See It, Eat It</strong></p>
                
                    <p>The directions are pretty clear at the festival sponsored by cable channel Showtime. The <a href="http://eatseehear.com" target="_blank">Eat See Hear</a> series runs May 11-Sept. 14.</p>
                
                    <p>Eight of the films in the Saturday series will be in Downtown at either Los Angeles State Historic Park, where people can sit on the grass, or in a drive-in on the rooftop of a parking lot at the Los Angeles Trade Tech College at Flower Street just south of Washington Boulevard.</p>
                
                    <p>We want to marry the three quintessential L.A. things, said Sharon Sperber, co-owner and co-producer of the series, referring to the mix of movies, food and music.</p>
                
                    <p>The movies are projected on a three-and-a-half story tall, 52-foot-wide screen. Events start at 5:30 p.m. with food trucks, the bands play at 7 p.m. and the movies start at 8:30 p.m.</p>
                
                    <p>Coming to Trade Tech May 18 is Seth MacFarlanes comedy <em>Ted</em>. Although the movie is about a cute talking teddy bear, this is not for kids. Consider yourself warned.</p>
                
                    <p>The next Downtown event is June 15 at the State Park with the bloody Brad Pitt action thriller <em>Fight Club</em>. <em>Risky Business</em> gets another local screening on June 29 at Trade Tech and Bill Murrays <em>Stripes</em> plays a week later at the State Park. Downtown is also home to the final event, with Molly Ringwald (again!) as part of the Brat Pack cast of <em>The Breakfast Club</em> at the park on Sept. 14.</p>
                
                    <p> <em>L.A. State Historic Park is at 1245 N. Spring St. L.A. Trade Tech is at Flower and W. 22nd streets or <a href="http://eatseehear.com">eatseehear.com</a>.</em></p>
