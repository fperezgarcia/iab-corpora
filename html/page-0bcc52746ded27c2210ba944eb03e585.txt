    <h1>Diesels</h1>

</div>



                      <div class="clear">&nbsp;</div>
                       
                      
<p><strong>Diesel-Powered Vehicles at the 2013 Chicago Auto Show<br />
</strong></p>
<p><img width="350" height="250" align="right" src="/assets/1/Page/2013_Audi_Q7_Diesel_Vehicles.jpg" alt="2013 Audi Q7 Diesel" style="padding:5px;" />Say the word &quot;diesel&quot; and people immediately think of dirty, stinky and loud over-the-road trucks. That&rsquo;s just not true any more. Today&rsquo;s modern diesel engines are quiet, powerful and efficient. Best of all they don&rsquo;t smell any more!</p>
<p>Diesel engines are inherently more efficient than their gasoline counterparts. Take, for example, the new Audi Q7. With the six-cylinder gas engine the Q7 is EPA rated at 16 mpg city and 22 mpg highway. The diesel counterpart carries a rating of 19 mpg city and 28 mpg highway. That&rsquo;s roughly a 25% improvement. Better yet, the diesel will save you more than $300 per year in fuel costs.</p>
<p>An the other end of the spectrum, there&rsquo;s the tiny little Audi A3 wagon. The gas engine rates a respectable 21 mpg city and 28 mpg highway. Opt for the diesel and you&rsquo;re cruising to the tune of 30 mpg city and 42 mpg highway -- those are hybrid numbers.</p>
<p>Another advantage of diesel engines is the amount of low-speed grunt they offer. Exhibit A, Chevy&rsquo;s Duramax diesel pumps out 765 lb/ft of torque.&nbsp; Exhibit B, Ford&rsquo;s Powerstroke in the Super Duty produces 800 pound-feet of torque.  Exhibit C (and the closing argument) Ram&rsquo;s Cummins Turbo makes 850 lb-ft of torque.  So if you&rsquo;re pulling a boat out of the water or stump out of the ground, there&rsquo;s not better choice than a diesel.</p>
<p>You can find the following diesel-powered vehicles on display at the 2013 Chicago Auto Show or at the <a href="http://www.chicagoautoshow.com/vehicles_on_display/default.aspx">Vehicles on Display</a> section of the auto show Web site.</p>
