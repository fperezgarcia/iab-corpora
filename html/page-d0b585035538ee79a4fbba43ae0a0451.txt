<p><b>What Is It?</b> </p><p>Nissan says its <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/section/versa&template=newcar&make=Nissan">Versa </a>hatchback is the biggest seller in the segment and has been since 2008, a highly competitive slice of the market that includes the relatively new <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/section/sonic&template=newcar&make=chevrolet">Sonic</a>, <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/section/accent&template=newcar&make=Hyundai">Accent/Rio</a>, <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/section/fiesta&template=newcar&make=Ford">Fiesta/Mazda2 </a>and the popularly peppy <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/section/fit&template=newcar&make=Honda">Honda Fit</a>. For the 2014 model year Nissan brings the Versa Note hatchback onto the same platform as the Versa sedan, which lops off six inches of overhang front and rear but maintains the same 102.3-inch wheelbase. Nissan points out that rear legroom stays the segment-leading same at 38 inches. </p><p></p><p>Cd is down to an impressive 0.298 thanks to aerodynamic tweaks all over and under the car. Low rolling-resistance tires, 300 pounds less weight thanks to the new platform, a more-efficient CVT powertrain and dual fuel injectors in each of the 1.6 liter four's cylinders bumps gas mileage to 31 city/40 highway and 35 combined, a 17 percent increase over the previous hatchback.</p><p></p><p><b>What's It Like To Drive?</b> </p><p>Our first drive of the new Versa Note was in an SV trim level with the CVT transmission. CVTs have come a long way since the first ones ventured onto the market and this one shows a higher degree of efficiency. It has a sub planetary gear -- an industry first -- which helps widen the gear ratio from 6.0:1 to 7.3:1.</p><p>Behind the wheel the CVT feels tighter than older units and in fact accounts for 53 percent of that 17-percent improvement in fuel economy. But a CVT still saps some of the fun out of driving anything and the Note takes a hit in that department. However, a manual transmission is also available, which Nissan thinks will account for 10 percent of sales. </p><p>The manual was much more fun, as you'd expect. Push it hard and it plows like a John Deere tractor, as you'd also expect. Many competitive hatchbacks top the Versa Note if you're looking for fun behind the wheel: the Sonic, Fiesta, Accent and their twins Mazda2 and Kia Rio are a little more tossable. </p><p>We even tried some 0-60 launches in a manual transmission Note and were a little surprised to find it barely broke the mythical 11-second barrier, clocking in at 10.8 seconds. It felt a lot faster than that. Or at least we felt a lot faster than that. We didn't get a chance to try the CVT transmission for a 0-60 time but usually you can knock at least a second off the manual trans clocking.</p><p>Nissan also touted the noise control in the new car, but we found it pretty loud from behind the wheel...louder, in fact, than the NV Cargo van we drove the same day. Most of the noise sounded like it was coming from the tires and suspension </p><p>.</p><p><b>Do I Want It?</b> </p><p>Buyers in this segment have been buying more Versa hatchbacks than anything else. Most just want an inexpensive family hauler and the Note handles all the tasks families throw at it well. There is plenty of room inside and you can get NissanConnect with Navigation in the Tech Package, which includes Pandora, Google connectivity for nav as well as Around View parking views displayed on a 5.8-inch touch screen.</p><p>Prices start at $14,780 for an S model with the manual transmission and top out just under $20k. Depending on how you juggle features around -- and that's a big depending -- the Nissan could be the best value in the bunch.</p><p>If you're looking for something fun to drive and practical, be sure to check out the competition before you buy. But if value and practicality top your list, this could be the car for you, your spouse and your 1.7 kids.</p><p></p><p><b>2014 Nissan Versa Note</p><p>On Sale:</b> Now</p><p><b>Base Price:</b> $14,780</p><p><b>Powertrain:</b> 109-hp, 107-lb-ft 1.6-liter four; five-speed manual, fwd</p><p><b>0-60:</b> 10.8 seconds (AW)</p><p><b>EPA Mileage:</b> 31 city/40 highway/35 combined
