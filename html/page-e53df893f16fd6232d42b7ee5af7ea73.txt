<h1><font face="Verdana">Haynes Repair Manual</font></font></h1>
 <div>
  <div class="floatright">
   <font face="Verdana">
   <a class="image" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Haynes-Logo.png">
   <img height="125" alt="Haynes-Logo.png" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/7/7e/Haynes-Logo.png/125px-Haynes-Logo.png" width="125" align="right"></a></font></div>
  <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">The <b>Haynes Owner's Workshop Manuals</b> 
  (commonly known as simply <b>Haynes Manuals</b>) are a series of 
  practical manuals from the
  <a class="new" title="Haynes Publishing Group (page does not exist)" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Haynes_Publishing_Group&action=edit&redlink=1">
  Haynes Publishing Group</a> aimed at both
  <a title="Do it yourself" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Do_it_yourself">
  DIY</a> enthusiasts and professional
  <a title="Automobile repair shop" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Automobile_repair_shop">
  garage</a> mechanics. The series primarily focuses upon the
  <a title="Car maintenance" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Car_maintenance">
  maintenance</a> and repair of automotive vehicles, covering a wide range 
  of makes and models (300 models of car and 130 models of motorcycle), 
  but it also includes manuals in the same style for
  <a class="mw-redirect" title="Domestic appliance" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domestic_appliance">
  domestic appliances</a> and
  <a title="Personal computer" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personal_computer">
  personal computers</a>, digital photography and
  <a class="mw-redirect" title="Model railway" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Model_railway">
  model railways</a>,
  <a title="Man" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Man">men</a> and
  <a class="mw-redirect" title="Baby" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baby">
  babies</a>, <a title="Sex" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sex">sex</a>, 
  and <a title="Woman" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Woman">women</a>. 
  The last four were made slightly
  <a class="mw-redirect" title="Tongue in cheek" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tongue_in_cheek">
  tongue in cheek</a>, but have proved very popular.</font></p>
  <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">A Haynes manual based on the various
  <a title="Starship Enterprise" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Starship_Enterprise">
  Starships <i>Enterprise</i></a> from the various <i>
  <a title="Star Trek" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_Trek">Star 
  Trek</a></i> television series and motion pictures is in development for 
  release in September of 2010. </font></p>
  <font face="Verdana" size="2">
<SCRIPT type=text/javascript>

  </font>
  <h2><font face="Verdana" size="3" color="#FF0000"><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></font></h2>
  <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">The Haynes manuals are named after
  <a class="new" title="John Harold Haynes (page does not exist)" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=John_Harold_Haynes&action=edit&redlink=1">
  John Haynes</a>,
  <a title="Order of the British Empire" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire">
  OBE</a>. In 1956, when he was at school, he wrote and published a book 
  on building a '<a class="mw-redirect" title="Homebuilt car" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homebuilt_car">special</a>' 
  based on the
  <a title="Austin 7" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austin_7">Austin 
  7</a>, and wrote two further books while performing
  <a title="National service" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_service">
  national service</a> in the
  <a title="Royal Air Force" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Air_Force">
  Royal Air Force</a>. Haynes Publishing was founded in 1960 and the first 
  manual actually entitled &quot;Haynes Owners Workshop Manual&quot;, for the
  <a class="mw-redirect" title="Austin Healey Sprite" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austin_Healey_Sprite">
  Austin Healey Sprite</a>, was published in 1965.</font></p>
  <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">Many Haynes Manuals bear a cover 
  illustration of a stripped down technical drawing of the vehicle, 
  hand-drawn by Terry Davey, and they bear his signature.</font></p>
  <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">The automotive vehicle manuals are based 
  upon taking apart (stripping down) and then putting together 
  (rebuilding) a vehicle. A logo on the manual says &quot;based on a complete 
  strip down and rebuild&quot;. Each section has step-by-step instructions with 
  diagrams and photographs of an actual strip down or rebuild.</font></p>
  <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">Manuals for garage professionals include 
  books such as the <i>Automotive Diesel Engine Service Guide</i>, the <i>
  Automotive Air Conditioning TechBook</i>, <i>Citron and Peugeot Engine 
  Management Systems</i>, and two <i>Engine Management and Fuel Injection 
  Systems Pin Tables and Wiring Diagrams TechBook</i> volumes.</font></p>
  <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">Haynes manuals are published in 15 
  languages:
  <a title="English language" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_language">
  English</a> (including
  <a class="mw-redirect" title="English English" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_English">
  British</a>,
  <a title="American English" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_English">
  American</a> and
  <a title="Australian English" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australian_English">
  Australian</a> variants),
  <a title="French language" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language">
  French</a>,
  <a title="Swedish language" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swedish_language">
  Swedish</a>,
  <a title="Chinese language" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language">
  Chinese</a>,
  <a title="Japanese language" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_language">
  Japanese</a>,
  <a title="German language" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_language">
  German</a>,
  <a title="Czech language" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Czech_language">
  Czech</a>,
  <a title="Finnish language" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finnish_language">
  Finnish</a>,
  <a title="Polish language" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polish_language">
  Polish</a>,
  <a title="Bulgarian language" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgarian_language">
  Bulgarian</a>,
  <a title="Hebrew language" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hebrew_language">
  Hebrew</a>,
  <a title="Greek language" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_language">
  Greek</a>,
  <a title="Danish language" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danish_language">
  Danish</a>,
  <a title="Spanish language" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_language">
  Spanish</a> (including
  <a title="American Spanish" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Spanish">
  American Spanish</a> versions), and
  <a title="Russian language" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_language">
  Russian</a>.The company is based in
  <a title="Sparkford" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sparkford">
  Sparkford</a>, a village near
  <a title="Yeovil" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yeovil">Yeovil</a> 
  in <a title="Somerset" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Somerset">
  Somerset</a>, England. The
  <a title="Haynes International Motor Museum" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haynes_International_Motor_Museum">
  Haynes International Motor Museum</a>, the largest motor museum in the 
  UK, is also in Yeovil, and it is home to a large collection of both 
  classic and modern cars, and many rarities.</font></p>
  <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">Haynes manuals are written by a pair of 
  authors, and take between 20 and 30 man-weeks. A car or motorcycle is 
  bought at the beginning of the project and sold at the end. Although the 
  workshop phase of the project usually lasts for roughly four weeks, the 
  vehicle is usually retained for a couple of months to ensure it is 
  functioning correctly.Sometimes special tools are needed to make 
  repairs. Haynes manuals generally suggest an alternative that will work 
  as a one-off job rather than buying or making a special tool. The 
  tooling often is only available to authorised dealers; indeed this is 
  part of the reason people go to them.<sup> </sup>&nbsp;Haynes manuals 
  never recommend
  <a title="Percussive maintenance" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percussive_maintenance">
  percussive maintenance</a>.</font></p>
  <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">Haynes generally has the co-operation of 
  the manufacturers to provide technical information such as mechanical 
  tolerances and wiring diagrams. This may seem against their own 
  interests since servicing can cost 40% of the cost of the vehicle over 
  its lifetime, but manufacturers get loyalty by providing customers with 
  information that lets them fix their vehicle, to their ability, or at 
  least diagnose the fault before taking it to a garage. Over the lifetime 
  of a vehicle, the
  <a title="Aftermarket (automotive)" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aftermarket_(automotive)">
  aftermarket</a> is worth perhaps as much as the new cost price of the 
  vehicle, so the manufacturer still gets a significant revenue from the 
  spare parts, and the longer a vehicle lasts, the more so.</font></p>
  <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><b>The &quot;History&quot; section is taken in its 
  entirety from Wikipedia.com</b></font></div>
