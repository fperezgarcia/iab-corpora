At the New York<a title=" auto show" href=" http://www.motortrend.com/auto_shows/"> auto show</a>, the company confirmed that the upcoming 328d four-cylinder diesel will debut in rear- and all-wheel-drive forms, and as a sedan and wagon, with mileage as high as 45 mpg, though EPA testing has yet to begin. If BMW can keep the 328d&#8217;s price down, it may just become a genuine 328i alternative. &#8212; <strong>Jason Udy</strong></p>
<p>The <a target="_blank" title="BMW 3 Series" href=" BMW 3-Series http://www.motortrend.com/new_cars/04/bmw/3_series/">BMW 3 Series</a> lineup is expanding yet again in the U.S. &#8212; the automaker has just confirmed that the 2014 BMW 328d will debut at the New York auto show. Appearing alongside the new 3 Series GT, the diesel-powered 328d will get its first U.S. showing on March 27.</p>
<p><a href="http://wot.motortrend.com/2014-bmw-328d-diesel-making-us-debut-at-new-york-auto-show-341981.html/2014-bmw-328d-diesel-engine" rel="attachment wp-att-348275"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-348275" src="http://stwot.motortrend.com/files/2013/03/2014-BMW-328d-Diesel-engine-300x187.jpg" alt="2014 BMW 328d Diesel engine 300x187 image" width="300" height="187" title="2014 BMW 328d Diesel engine 300x187 photo" /></a>BMW&#8217;s announcement simply confirms <a href="http://wot.motortrend.com/bmw-brings-back-the-oil-burner-confirms-2-0-liter-i-4-diesel-for-u-s-market-232013.html?ti=v2#axzz2NFaYmBQb">what we already knew</a> &#8212; that a diesel four-cylinder was coming to the U.S. in the 3 Series. <em>Motor Trend</em> enjoyed a 2009 BMW 335d for 23 months, but although that car had 265 hp and 425 lb-ft of torque, the diesel I-6 remained a lower-volume variant. With the 328d, BMW appears to be targeting higher volume by focusing more on fuel economy than power and torque. BMW has confirmed that the 328d will be available in rear- and all-wheel drive, and we&#8217;ve heard <a href="http://wot.motortrend.com/we-hear-bmw-3-series-diesel-awd-wagon-coming-to-u-s-340481.html#axzz2NWwTjygF">even in wagon form</a>. The Euro-spec BMW 320d xDrive Touring is shown in white below; the red 328d from the New York auto show is above and at right.</p>
<p>As we expected, the new BMW 328d will use a turbodiesel 2.0-liter I-4 with 180 hp and 280 lb-ft of torque, and the automaker says that mileage &#8220;could well exceed 40 mpg,&#8221; though it&#8217;s unclear how the car will be rated by the EPA. <a target="_blank" href="http://www.motortrend.com/roadtests/oneyear/1012_2009_bmw_335d_verdict/viewall.html">Our 2009 335d</a> &#8212; a previous-generation 3 Series &#8212; was rated 23/36 mpg while an automatic-transmission 2013 328i is listed at 23/33 mpg.</p>
<p><a href="http://wot.motortrend.com/2014-bmw-328d-diesel-making-us-debut-at-new-york-auto-show-341981.html/2014-bmw-328d-diesel-interior" rel="attachment wp-att-348281"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-348281" src="http://stwot.motortrend.com/files/2013/03/2014-BMW-328d-Diesel-interior-300x187.jpg" alt="2014 BMW 328d Diesel interior 300x187 image" width="300" height="187" title="2014 BMW 328d Diesel interior 300x187 photo" /></a>The new BMW 320i also has 180 hp, but only 200 lb-ft from its gas-powered turbo 2.0-liter I-4. From 0-60 mph, the $33,445 320i will accelerate from 0-60 mph in 7.1 seconds, while the automaker&#8217;s claimed time for the 328d is 7.2 seconds. For comparison, our 2009 335d did the sprint in 5.8 seconds. Unlike that car, however, we expect the 328d to have a base price under $40,000.</p>
<p>The 328d will do battle with the Audi A4, which is expected to get a diesel variant in the upcoming redesign that arrives in the 2014 calendar year.</p>
