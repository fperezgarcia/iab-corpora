					<h2>What is a Streetfighter Motorcycle?</h2>
					<p class="title-meta"><span class="comments-head"><span>Comments Off</span></span> <span class="date">14 October 2010</span></p>
					                    
                    					<div class="entry">
						<a title="What is a Streetfighter Motorcycle?" href="http://www.mrautomotive.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/what-is-a-streetfighter.jpg" rel="lightbox"><img src="http://www.mrautomotive.net/wp-content/themes/dailyedition/thumb.php?src=wp-content/uploads/2010/10/what-is-a-streetfighter.jpg&amp;w=250&amp;h=180&amp;zc=1&amp;q=90" alt="What is a Streetfighter Motorcycle?" class="woo-image thumbnail alignright"  width="250"  height="180"  /></a>						<p>Basically, a streetfighter is a stripped-down sport bike. Often the fairings are removed, the clip-ons replaced with motocross bars, and a single or dual round headlights mounted in place of the OEM unit. They have an aggressive look inspired by the cafe racers of the 1950&#8242;s and 1960&#8242;s, yet the basic platform is most typically a Japanese supersport such as a Suzuki GSX-R or Honda CBR.</p>
<p>In similar fashion to the brakeless fixed-gear bicycles of urban messenger culture, streetfighters developed out of necessity. Whereas messengers adopted brakeless bikes to prevent theft, UK motorcyclists began tearing down their sport bikes when they could not afford to replace expensive fairings damaged from drops, falls, or accidents events often related to two-wheeled stunting and hooliganism.</p>
<p>Before long, the manufacturers took notice, and many production streetfighters are or have been available direct from the factory. Examples include the Ducati Streetfighter, Triumph Speed Triple, and Kawasaki K1000.</p>

<p class="syndicated-attribution">For more information, read this article: <strong><a href="http://www.associatedcontent.com/article/5894434/the_kawasaki_z1000_the_best_factory.html?cat=27">http://www.associatedcontent.com/article/5894434/the_kawasaki_z1000_the_best_factory.html?cat=27</a></strong>.</p>
