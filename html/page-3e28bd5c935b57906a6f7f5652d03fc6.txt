<p><font color="#000000" face="Comic Sans MS"><font size="+3">ART &amp; 
ARCHITECTURE</font></font></p>
<p><font color="#000000" face="Arial,Helvetica"><font size="+0"><a href="http://www.artsmia.org/modernism/e_AM.html">Early 
  modernism in art, design, and architecture</a>, which began at the turn of the 
  century, continued through to 1940 and the war.&nbsp;In cities, <a href="http://brainwaves.corante.com/archives/2003/08/15/electricity_steel_and_skyscrapers_1870_1920.php">Skyscrapers </a>(first in 1870s) were erected 
  and hundreds of architects competed for the work. The first successful design 
  was the <a href="http://users.commkey.net/daniel/wool.htm">Woolworth Building</a>  in New York.&nbsp;&nbsp; In Chicago, the <a href="http://www.aviewoncities.com/chicago/wrigley.htm">Wrigley 
  building</a> was designed by <a href="http://www.gapw.com/">Graham, Anderson, Probst, and White</a> while the <a href="http://www.gommage.com/tribune.html">Chicago 
  Tribune Tower</a> was designed by Howells and Hood.&nbsp; The <a href="http://www.brynmawr.edu/cities/archx/05-600/proj/p2/npk/historydeco.htm">Art 
  Deco design</a> was exemplified by the <a href="http://www.imperialclub.com/Yr/1926/building/Cap.htm">Chrysler 
  </a>and <a href="http://www.artifice.com/cgi-bin/gbc-building.cgi/Empire_State_Building.html">Empire 
  State Building</a>s (depression projects - the Empire State Building completed 
  early 1931.) <a href="http://lcweb.loc.gov/exhibits/flw/flw.html">Frank Lloyd 
  Wright</a> was prolific during this period, designing homes in California and 
  in Japan.&nbsp; The term <a href="http://www.adsw.org/">Art Deco </a>(1925-1950) 
  is derived from the <a href="http://www.nga.gov/resources/expositions.shtm">International 
  Art Exposition in Paris</a> in 1925.&nbsp; In the 20s and 30s art of that style 
  was referred to as modern.&nbsp; Designers included <a href="http://www.uam.ucsb.edu/Pages/weber_brochure_1.html">Karl 
  (Kem) Weber</a> and <a href="http://www.scandinaviandesign.com/elielSaarinen/">Eliel 
  Saarinen</a>.</font></font> </p>
<p><font color="#000000" face="Arial,Helvetica">Art movements included the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modernism">modernist 
  movement</a> [<a href="http://www.artcyclopedia.com/artists/luks_george.html">George 
  Luks</a>, <a href="http://www.juliehellergallery.com/Julie%20Heller%20Gallery/Artist%20Images/Charles%20Hawthorne/Julie%20Heller%20Gallery%20%20Charles%20Hawthorne%20Bio.htm">Charles 
  W. Hawthorne</a>], <a href="http://wwar.com/artists/period/abstract_expressionism.html">abstract 
  expressionism</a> [<a href="http://www.artcyclopedia.com/artists/de_kooning_willem.html">Willem 
  de Kooning</a>],<a href="http://www.artcyclopedia.com/history/surrealism.html"> 
  surrealism,</a> 
  and 
  <a href="http://www.huntfor.com/arthistory/C20th/dadaism.htm">dadaism</a> [<a href="http://www.okeeffemuseum.org/home.aspx">Georgia 
  O'Keeffe</a>, <a href="http://www.askart.com/AskART/interest/Modernism_3.aspx?id=76">Morgan 
  Russell</a>, <a href="http://www.manray-photo.com/catalog/index.php">Man Ray</a>],&nbsp; 
  <a href="http://wwar.com/masters/movements/magic_realism.html">realism</a> [ 
  <a href="http://www.artcyclopedia.com/artists/benton_thomas_hart.html">Thomas

<font color="#000000" face="Arial,Helvetica"><img src="images/algonquin_round_table.jpg" width="504" height="307" hspace="12" vspace="5" align="left">Following 
WWI (the war to end all wars), talented young authors, some expatriates in France, 
wrote about their feelings of disillusionment and alienation. A sense of rebellion 
developed and the victorian idea of decency was considered hypocritical.&nbsp; Writers began to write frankly about sexuality. Three important groups during 
this period were:</font> 
<p><font color="#000000">&nbsp;<font face="Arial,Helvetica"><a href="http://www.npg.si.edu/exh/caricatures/table.htm">The 
  Algonquin Round Table</a>, also called THE ROUND TABLE, informal group of American 
  literary men and women who met daily for lunch on weekdays at a large round 
  table in the <a href="http://www.algonquinhotel.com/nav_the_algonquin.html">Algonquin 
  Hotel in New York City</a> during the 1920s and '30s. Many of the best-known 
  writers, journalists, and artists in New York City were in this group. Among 
  them&nbsp;&nbsp; were <a href="http://www.dorothyparker.com/">Dorothy Parker</a>, 
  <a href="http://www.dorothyparker.com/dot26.htm">Alexander 
  Woollcott </a>(author of the quote "All the things I really like are immoral, 
  illegal, or fattening", <a href="http://www.nps.gov/archive/elro/glossary/broun-heywood.htm">Heywood 
  Broun</a>, <a href="http://www.robertbenchley.org/sob/index.htm">Robert Benchley</a>,<a href="http://www.pbs.org/wnet/americanmasters/database/algonquin_round_table.html">Robert 
  Sherwood</a>,<a href="http://www.georgeskaufman.com/"> George 
  S. Kaufman</a>, <a href="http://www.mgilleland.com/fpabio.htm">Franklin P. Adams</a>, <a href="http://www.ibdb.com/person.asp?ID=7491">Marc 
  Connelly</a>, <a href="http://xroads.virginia.edu/%7EUG02/NewYorker/rosshome2.html">Harold Ross</a>, <a href="http://www.harpomarx.net/">Harpo Marx</a>, 
  and <a href="http://books.google.com/books?hl=en&id=mPWHpfTLJgsC&dq=Russell+Crouse.&printsec=frontcover&source=web&ots=G_HVYjwOl2&sig=7lcx-nPPpnhFz-sKc-BL8SJ5E1A&sa=X&oi=book_result&resnum=3&ct=result#PPP1,M1">Russell Crouse.</a></font></font> </p>

<font color="#000000" face="Arial,Helvetica"><a href="http://www.42explore2.com/harlem.htm">Harlem 
Renaissance</a> is considered the first important movement of<a href="http://www.helium.com/items/875098-how-the-harlem-renaissance-inspired-a-national-community-of-black-writers">

NY,</a> and other urban areas during the 1920s, black writers published more than

received their first serious critical appraisal.&nbsp; This group included<a href="http://www.lonestar.edu/library/kin_TheirEyesWereWatchingGod.htm"> 
Zora Neale Hurston</a>, <a href="http://www.webdubois.org/" target="_blank">W.E.B.

and <a href="http://www.founders.howard.edu/Locke.htm">Alain Locke</a> 
, who was considered the chief interpreter for the <a href="http://www.pbs.org/wnet/americannovel/timeline/harlemrenaissance.html">Harlem 
movement</a>.</font>

<font color="#000000" face="Arial,Helvetica">The <a href="http://www.montgomerycollege.edu/departments/hpolscrv/jbolhofer.html">Lost 
Generation, </a>the self-exiled expatriates who lived and wrote in Paris between 
the wars.&nbsp; These writers, looking for freedom of thought and action, changed 
the face of modern writing.&nbsp; Realistic and rebellious, they wrote what they 
wanted and fought censorship for profanity and sexuality.&nbsp; They incorporated 
Freudian ideas into their characters and styles.&nbsp; This group included <a href="http://www.literarytraveler.com/hemingway/pamplona.htm">Ernest 
Hemingway</a>, <a href="http://www.infoplease.com/spot/bhmharlem1.html">Gertrude 
Stein</a>, <a href="http://www.harvardsquarelibrary.org/poets/passos.php">John 
Dos Passos</a>, <a href="http://www.levity.com/corduroy/millerh.htm">Henry 
Miller,</a> <a href="http://www.sc.edu/fitzgerald/">F. Scott Fitzgerald.</a></font>

<font color="#000000" face="Arial,Helvetica">Others who were important during

Renaissance</a>,&nbsp;<a href="http://www.english.uiuc.edu/maps/poets/m_r/millay/millay.htm">

from <a href="http://www.pbs.org/wnet/americanmasters/database/greenwich_village.html">Greenwich 
Village</a>, and <a href="http://www.imagi-nation.com/moonstruck/clsc34.html">Eugene 
O'Neill</a> drew attention to a serious American stage.&nbsp; AND, we can't leave 
out the beginning of the <a href="http://www.lonestar.edu/library/kin_Moonstone.htm">Golden Age of Mysteries. </a> and introducing America's own contribution to the mystery novel, the <a href="http://www.vintagelibrary.com/hardboil.cfm">hard-boiled, 
with writers such </a>as <a href="http://www.dcs.gla.ac.uk/%7Egnik/crime-fiction/chandler/chandler.html">Raymond

and paving the way for the future.</font><center>

<p><font color="#000000" face="Arial Black">Costumes / Fashion</font></p>
</div>
<p><font color="#000000"><img src="images/friend20.jpg" alt="My mom on the left - with a friend." align="left" height="311" hspace="15" vspace="15" width="157"> 
    <font face="Arial,Helvetica">Men: Clothing for men became a bit more conservative 
  in the <a href="http://www.murrayonhawaii.com/nolan/fashionhistory_1920mens.html">1920s</a>.&nbsp; Trousers widened to as wide as 24 inches at the bottomes.&nbsp; <a href="http://www.oldmagazinearticles.com/articles.php?cid=7">Knickers</a> grew in width and length and were called 'plus fours'.&nbsp; <a href="http://www.antiquedress.com/gallery.htm">White 
  linen</a> was popular during the summer.&nbsp; And during the winter, an outstanding 
  American coat was popular - <a href="http://www.oldmagazinearticles.com/Well-Dressed-Man-Raccoon_Coat_1920s">the racoon coat</a>.&nbsp; These were very popular with 
  the college men. The slouch hat was made of felt and could be rolled up and 
  packed into a suitcase. A wool suit was only $15.85. Garters were 40 cents. 
  All this and a 12" long cigarette holder. Cigarettes were 10 cents a pack.</font></font></p>
<p><font color="#000000" face="Arial,Helvetica"><a href="http://www.fashion-era.com/1920s/index.htm">Women:&nbsp;</a> By 1921 the longer skirt was back - some long and uneven at the bottom.&nbsp; 
  The short skirt was popular by 1925.&nbsp; This period was called the <a href="http://faculty.pittstate.edu/%7Eknichols/jazzage.html">Flapper 
  Age</a>.&nbsp; No bosom, no waistline, and hair nearly hidden under a cloche 
  hat.&nbsp; This decade began the present hey-dey for the <a href="http://www.bitzofglitz.com/compactsinfo.htm">manufacturing 
  of cosmetics</a>.&nbsp; Powder, lipstick, rouge, eyebrow pencil, eye shadow, 
  colored nails.&nbsp; They had it all!&nbsp; AND pearls.</font> </p>
<p><font color="#000000" face="Arial,Helvetica">This period marked the spread 
  of <a href="http://costume.osu.edu/exhibitions/fas/">ready-to-wear fashion</a>.&nbsp; 
  More women were wage earners and did not want to spent time on fittings.&nbsp; 
  The status symbol aspect of fashion was losing its importants as class distinctions 
  were becoming blurred.&nbsp; Inexpensive fashion became available.&nbsp; America 
  moved ahead of other countries&nbsp; mass production of contemporary style clothing 
  for women.&nbsp; America even produced several designers of this fashion including 
  Jane Derby.</font> </p>

  <h2 align="left"> <font color="#000000"><a name="events"></a><font face="Comic Sans MS"><font size="+2">HISTORIC 
    EVENTS AND PEOPLE</font></font></font></h2>
</center>
<font color="#000000" face="Arial,Helvetica">Thanks to <a href="http://www.hfmgv.org/exhibits/hf/">Henry 
Ford</a> and mass production, one could buy a ford for $290.&nbsp; The <a href="http://www.historycentral.com/documents/Volstead.html">Volstead 
Act</a> became effective Jan 16, 1920 and made the sale of a drink <img src="images/milk20.jpg" align="left" height="180" hspace="15" vspace="15" width="353">containing 
as much as one half-ounce of alcohol unlawful. This one unsuccessful act brought 
about much of the flavor of the Jazz Age or Roaring Twenties as we know them. 
This was a period of&nbsp; <a href="http://www.cedro-uva.org/lib/levine.alcohol.html">prohibition 
and intolerance</a>, speakeasies, flappers, <a href="http://www.historylearningsite.co.uk/prohibition_and_the_gangsters.htm">gangsters</a>, 
and crime. Hootch was supplied by <a href="http://www.rotten.com/library/bio/crime/mafia/dutch-schultz/">Dutch 
Schultz</a> and <a href="http://www.chicagohs.org/history/capone.html">Al 
Capone</a>.&nbsp; The <a href="http://www.ourdocuments.gov/doc.php?flash=true&doc=63">Nineteenth 
Amendment</a> had passed the previous year allowing women the right to vote in 
national elections.&nbsp; At the beginning of the decade the US was paralyzed 
by the grip of the <a href="http://www.law.umkc.edu/faculty/projects/ftrials/SaccoV/redscare.html">red 
scare</a> . Racial tensions were high and quotas were set for <a href="http://www.ellisisland.org/">immigrants</a> 
coming into America. The<a href="http://www.assumption.edu/ahc/1920s/Eugenics/Klan.html"> 
Ku Klux Klan</a> was very active during this period. The decade was a wonderful one for 
all of the arts and literature in America. <a href="http://www.thepeoplehistory.com/1920s.html">Technology 
grew</a> - the country shrunk - as popularity of automobiles, radios, and movies 
exploded. Buying on credit or <a href="http://etext.virginia.edu/journals/EH/EH37/Murphy.html">installments 
</a>was an outcome of the industrial age. In the fall of 1929, the <a href="http://www.nyse.com/home.html">New 
York Stock Exchange</a> was more active than it had ever been. Economists predicted 
a permanent high plateau. By October 24, 1929, <a href="http://www.mutual-funds-advisor.com/mutual-fund-history/market-crashes-the-black-thursday-1929.html">Black 
Thursday, </a>the <a href="http://history1900s.about.com/homework/history1900s/msub12.htm?iam=mt&amp;terms=%2Bstock+%2Bmarket+%2Bcrash+%2B1920s">stock 
market crashed</a> and panic broke out. Banks closed. The nation stayed in this<a href="http://www.gusmorino.com/pag3/greatdepression/"> 
depression</a> through the end of the twenties and most of the thirties.&nbsp; Check out the <a href="http://www.jstor.org/pss/2676191">Regulatory environment 
of the 1920s</a></font>.

  <h2> <font color="#000000"><a name="film"></a><font face="Comic Sans MS"><font size="+3">THEATER, 
    FILM RADIO</font></font></font></h2>
</center>
<font color="#000000"> <font face="Arial,Helvetica">The <a href="http://www.cs.monash.edu.au/%7Epringle/silent/ssotm/">Silent 
Screen stars</a> included the chic <a href="http://www.rudolph-valentino.com/">Rudolph 
Valentino</a> , sexy&nbsp; <a href="http://www.clarabow.net/">Clara Bow</a>.&nbsp;&nbsp; 
<a href="http://www.parabrisas.com/d_valleer.html">Rudy Vallee</a>&nbsp; sang 
through his megaphone.&nbsp; The first talking picture,<a href="http://www.filmsite.org/20sintro3.html"> 
Don Juan, starring John Barrymore </a>premiered on Broadway in 1926.&nbsp; This 
made movies big business.&nbsp; The first <a href="http://www.oscar.com/games/knowyouroscarhistory/">Oscars</a> were given in 1927.&nbsp; First <a href="http://www.oscars.org/awards/academyawards/about/history.html">Oscar 
movie</a> was a <a href="http://www.seeing-stars.com/Studios/ParamountStudios.shtml">Paramount 
Picture, Wings</a>.&nbsp; Emil Jennings and Janet Gaynor won best acting awards.</font></font> 
<p><font color="#000000" face="Arial,Helvetica"><font size="+0"><a href="http://www.mapsites.net/gotham01/webpages/alisonhannah/broad1920s.html">Broadway 
  </a>reached an all time peak. <a href="http://www.gershwin.com/">Gershwin</a> was hot with&nbsp; <a href="http://www.1920sera.com/arts/music/george-gershwins-an-american-in-paris/">An American in 
  Paris</a>,&nbsp; <a href="http://www.answers.com/topic/jerome-kern">Jerome Kern</a> 
  and <a href="http://www.rnh.com/index1.html">Oscar </a><a href="http://www.answers.com/topic/oscar-hammerstein-i">Hammerstein</a> 
  created <a href="http://www.theatrehistory.com/american/musical005.html">Show 
  Boat </a>starring Helen Morgan. <a href="http://www.themave.com/Astaire/">Fred 
  and Adele Astaire</a> opened in Funny Face.&nbsp; There were 268 plays offered 
  in New York City in the year 1927.&nbsp; This compaired with 50-60 in the 1970s.</font></font></p>
<p><font color="#000000" face="Arial,Helvetica">Radio networks began during this 
  decade:&nbsp; <a href="http://www.time.com/time/time100/builder/profile/sarnoff.html">David 
  Sarnoff's NBC </a>and <a href="http://www.hbs.edu/leadership/database/leaders/681/">William 
  Paley's</a> CBS both went on the air.&nbsp;<a href="http://www.billboard.com/"> 
  Billboard Magazine </a>published its first charts in 1928.&nbsp;<a href="http://www.who2.com/bingcrosby.html"> 
  Bing Crosby</a> and other crooner singing stars aided their sales with their 
  live and recorded radio performances.</font> </p>
