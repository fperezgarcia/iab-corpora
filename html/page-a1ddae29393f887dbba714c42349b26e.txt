<p>Chrysler is recalling 248,437 of its vehicles due to a potential ignition glitch that can cause the engine to turn to off, according to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration.</p>
<p>The recall affects 2010 models of the Chrysler Town &amp; Country minivan, Dodge Caravan minivan and all-wheel-drive versions of the Dodge Journey crossover.</p>
<p>According to the recall filing, some vehicles may experience &#8220;inadvertent ignition key displacement&#8221; from the &#8220;run&#8221; to the &#8220;accessory&#8221; positions while driving. This, in turn, would stop the engine, which could lead to a crash.</p>
<p>To remedy the situation, dealers will for free replace the affected modules. Recall notices to owners are slated to be sent out in April. If you have additional questions or concerns, you can contact Chrysler at 800-853-1403.</p>
