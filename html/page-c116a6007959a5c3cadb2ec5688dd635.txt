		<p><a href="http://indianautosblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/Chevrolet_Cruze_hatchback.jpg" data-slb-active="1" data-slb-internal="0"><img class="size-medium wp-image-16987 alignright" style="margin: 2px 1px;" title="Chevrolet_Cruze_hatchback" src="http://indianautosblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/Chevrolet_Cruze_hatchback-300x225.jpg" alt="Chevrolet_Cruze_hatchback" width="189" height="141" /></a>Drawings of the upcoming Chevrolet Cruze hatchback convey one thing for certain &#8211; it is going to be a looker!</p>
<p>Details of the hatchback <a href="http://indianautosblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/Chevrolet_Cruze_hatchback_1.jpg" data-slb-active="1" data-slb-internal="0"><img class="size-medium wp-image-16986 alignleft" style="margin: 2px 1px;" title="Chevrolet_Cruze_hatchback_1" src="http://indianautosblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/Chevrolet_Cruze_hatchback_1-300x160.jpg" alt="Chevrolet_Cruze_hatchback_1" width="206" height="113" /></a>are not in the public domain, but it is safe to expect the same level of equipment and engines. The Cruze hatchback isn&#8217;t for the Indian customer who isn&#8217;t willing to pay a premium for something without a boot.</p>
<p>The SRV hatchback, which was based on the Optra, was not exactly a hit. The Honda Jazz is a case in point, with its hefty price tag, people moved towards the City sedan.</p>
<p>The timeline of <a href="http://indianautosblog.com/category/chevrolet-cruze" target="_blank">Chevrolet Cruze</a> variants has already begun. The hatchback and <a href="http://indianautosblog.com/2009/10/chevrolet-cruze-station-wagon" target="_blank">station wagon</a> are expected in the next two years.</p>
