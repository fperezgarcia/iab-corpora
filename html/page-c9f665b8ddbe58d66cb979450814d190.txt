<a name="2290196"></a><div class="comments" id="c2290196">I don't know anything about US auto clubs, but you should be aware that many auto clubs lobby against public transit and policies designed to support people who are not currently driving (in the supposed interest of supporting drivers' interests). If that's of concern to you, then you might look into what your various options do with their money.<br><span class="smallcopy">posted by <a href="http://www.metafilter.com/user/84306" target="_self">If only I had a penguin...</a> at <a href="/159676/What-is-the-best-Roadside-assistance-club#2290196" target="_self">9:40 AM</a>  on July 16, 2010 [<a href="/favorited/4/2290196" style="font-weight:normal;" title="1 user marked this as favorite">1 favorite</a>] </span></div><br><br>

<a name="2290236"></a><div class="comments" id="c2290236">Okay, i've never heard of Better World until now, and I'm too young for AARP.  I have, however, been a member of AAA for many years.  I've barely ever had to use the service -- little enough that I occasionally thought about canceling it.<br>
<br>
Last Sunday I got a flat while driving in the fast lane of a highway in a fairly rural area.  I had my son with me.  Night was falling.  And...AAA was there 30 minutes later.   The spare was on 10 minutes after that and I was on my way.   The technician was polite and reassuring.   And the phone operator who I originally spoke with via my cell was very helpful, given the situation that I didn't know exactly where I was (I was 400+ miles from home, and in an area where there are many miles between freeway exits).<br>
<br>
That satisfied basically my entire criteria in judging a roadside service company.  So, yeah, I'm recommending AAA.<br><span class="smallcopy">posted by <a href="http://www.metafilter.com/user/99024" target="_self">BlahLaLa</a> at <a href="/159676/What-is-the-best-Roadside-assistance-club#2290236" target="_self">9:56 AM</a>  on July 16, 2010 </span></div><br><br>

<a name="2290243"></a><div class="comments" id="c2290243">I'm an AAA member, and their roadside service is really good, but I'd love to switch because of the lobbying issues others have raised.<br><span class="smallcopy">posted by <a href="http://www.metafilter.com/user/19326" target="_self">lukemeister</a> at <a href="/159676/What-is-the-best-Roadside-assistance-club#2290243" target="_self">10:01 AM</a>  on July 16, 2010 </span></div><br><br>

<a name="2290270"></a><div class="comments" id="c2290270">Is roadside assistance the <i>only</i> feature you're looking for?  You might look into whether your cell phone provider offers a roadside assistance programâ€”I dropped my AAA membership a few years ago and replaced it with Verizon roadside assistance, which is cheaper ($3/mo. vs. ~$5/mo from AAA) and includes a longer free towing distance (10 mi. vs. 5 mi.)<br>
<br>
I haven't had to use Verizon roadside assistance, so I can't comment on the quality, but my understanding is that both contract out to local shops and in many cases the same shop would be responding either way.  One other note: I do have to have my Verizon phone <i>with me</i> to use their roadside assistance, but I don't have to call <i>from</i> my Verizon phone, so if I can't get cell coverage, or my phone battery is dead, I could still call from a landline and take advantage of it as long as I have the phone physically with me.  But if I had forgotten my cell phone and left it at home, I'd be screwed.  I don't know whether other cell phone carriers' policies are similar.<br><span class="smallcopy">posted by <a href="http://www.metafilter.com/user/12409" target="_self">DevilsAdvocate</a> at <a href="/159676/What-is-the-best-Roadside-assistance-club#2290270" target="_self">10:23 AM</a>  on July 16, 2010 </span></div><br><br>

<a name="2290942"></a><div class="comments" id="c2290942">Seconding maglikker's comment: AAA membership is for a person, not a car. However you can buy associate memberships for each member of your family (at a lower rate) once you're a member.<br><span class="smallcopy">posted by <a href="http://www.metafilter.com/user/17876" target="_self">DanSachs</a> at <a href="/159676/What-is-the-best-Roadside-assistance-club#2290942" target="_self">6:17 PM</a>  on July 16, 2010 </span></div><br><br>
