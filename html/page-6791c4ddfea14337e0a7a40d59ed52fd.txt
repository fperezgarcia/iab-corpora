<p>The 2014 BMW M6 Gran Coupe and 4-series coupe concept made their public debuts at the 2013 <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/section/detroit">Detroit auto show</a>. BMW introduced the two vehicles alongside the <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/article/20130114/DETROIT/130119919">2013 320i sedan</a>, which appeared for the first time in America.
</p>
<br/><br/><h2>2014 BMW M6 Gran Coupe</h2>
<p>
<div style="float: left; margin-right: 15px"><link rel="stylesheet" type="text/css" href="/css/articleMainPicture.css?ver=20110322" />
<a href="http://autoweek.com/storyimage/CW/20130114/DETROIT/130119916/V2/0/2014-BMW-M6-Gran-Coupe-Detroit-auto-show-debut.jpg" rel="lightbox"><img src="http://autoweek.com/storyimage/CW/20130114/DETROIT/130119916/V2/0/2014-BMW-M6-Gran-Coupe-Detroit-auto-show-debut.jpg&maxW=630" alt="2014 BMW M6 Gran Coupe Detroit auto show debut" title="2014 BMW M6 Gran Coupe Detroit auto show debut"  width="630" height="378" style="padding:3px; border:solid 1px #CCC;" /></a>
<b style="font-size:11px; color:#666;">BMW</b>
<br /><span style="font-size:10px; color:#999; line-height:14px;">The 2014 BMW M6 Gran Coupe debuted at the 2013 Detroit auto show.</span>
<br /><br /></div>
<p>The <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/article/20121212/detroit/121219965</p><p>">2014 M6 Gran Coupe</a> will join a <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/section/6_series&template=newcar&make=BMW">6-series</a>-derived lineup that already includes the two-door <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/article/20120629/CARREVIEWS/120629811">M6 coupe and convertible</a>. A unique front fascia, widened wheel arches, a carbon fiber-reinforced roof panel and -- of course -- a handful of M badges set the M6 Gran Coupe apart from its tamer 6-series counterpart.  </p><p>Like the two-door and the drop-top, the M6 Gran Coupe will be powered by a twin-turbocharged 4.4-liter V8 with an output of 560 hp and 500 lb-ft of torque. The gearbox is a seven-speed dual-clutch unit also found in the BMW M5 and existing models in the M6 range. </p><p>BMW claims that the M6 Gran Coupe will accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 4.2 seconds, the same time as the two-door M6. The base model's top speed of 155 mph can be upped to 189 if the buyer opts for the driver's package. </p><p>There's no word on how much that performance-enhancing driver's package will add to the M6 Gran Coupe's estimated $110,000 base price.
</p>
<br/><br/><h2>BMW 4-series coupe concept</h2>
<p>
<div style="float: left; margin-right: 15px"><link rel="stylesheet" type="text/css" href="/css/articleMainPicture.css?ver=20110322" />
<a href="http://autoweek.com/storyimage/CW/20130114/DETROIT/130119916/V3/0/4-series-coupe-concept-Detroit-auto-show-debut.jpg" rel="lightbox"><img src="http://autoweek.com/storyimage/CW/20130114/DETROIT/130119916/V3/0/4-series-coupe-concept-Detroit-auto-show-debut.jpg&maxW=630" alt="4-series coupe concept Detroit auto show debut" title="4-series coupe concept Detroit auto show debut"  width="630" height="378" style="padding:3px; border:solid 1px #CCC;" /></a>
<b style="font-size:11px; color:#666;">BMW</b>
<br /><span style="font-size:10px; color:#999; line-height:14px;">Presented as a design study, the 4-series coupe concept debuted at the 2013 Detroit auto show.</span>
<br /><br /></div>
<p>While BMW confirmed performance specifications of the production M6 Gran Coupe, the automaker presented the new <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/article/20121205/detroit/121209943</p><p>">4-series coupe</a> as a design study rather than a retail-ready vehicle. Still, we expect the final version -- set for sale in late 2013 as a 2014 model -- to closely resemble the concept shown in Detroit. </p><p>The design of the 4-series coupe draws much of its design inspiration from current 3-series two-door. That's hardly an accident; according to BMW, the longer, wider 4-series coupe will replace the 3-series coupe outright. </p><p>The 4-series coupe will then form the basis of a 4-series lineup that may grow include a convertible, a four-door Gran Coupe and a performance-oriented M4. </p><p>BMW has not released a full set of specifications or expected performance figures for the 4-series coupe, but the automaker has suggested that its xDrive all-wheel drive system may find its way on to some versions of the two-door.
</p>
<br/><br/><h2>About the Detroit auto show</h2>
<p>
<p>The annual North American International Auto Show, better known as the Detroit auto show, has maintained its place as a barometer for the entire industry. For 2013, NAIAS takes place Jan. 14-27 and will host concept cars, green cars and supercars from all the major automakers, including what's expected to be a highlight this year: the new <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/section/corvette">2014 Chevrolet Corvette</a>. Check out Autoweek's <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/section/detroit">complete coverage of the Detroit auto show here</a>.
