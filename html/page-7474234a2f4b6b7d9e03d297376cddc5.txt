<span class="blackshowlinks">Charles,<br />
<br />Just some observations to bolster my belief that car MPG will hover around 40-50 MPG for a long time:<br />
<br />1.&nbsp; It is interesting to compare the MPG of the Prius (51/48 city/hwy) to 2 cars that have IDENTICAL engine + drivetrains: the Prius V (44/40) and Lexus CT200h (43/40).&nbsp; Note that even though the engine is the same ultra-efficient one - MPG is much lower.&nbsp; You are seeing the effect of less optimum aero drag and higher weight.<br />
<br />2.&nbsp; The advantage of hybrid technology is strongest for city driving, but helps a bit for hwy&nbsp;too.&nbsp; Chevy Cruze Eco (28/42 MPG), Hyundai Elantra (30/40).&nbsp; Note that hwy MPG is about the SAME as the latter 2 hybrids...but of course city MPG is much lower.<br />
<br />3.&nbsp; Even after all the years the Prius has been out....no other manufacturer has even matched the MPG figures for it.&nbsp; Also, Toyota claims to be working on an engine that will be ~18% more efficient....adding only about another 8-9 MPG.&nbsp; <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2011/04/24/toyota-targets-45-thermal-efficiency-for-engines-in-next-gen-hy/">http://green.autoblog.com/2011/04/24/toyota-targets-45-thermal-efficiency-for-engines-in-next-gen-hy/</a>&nbsp;&nbsp; This means they would approach high 50's MPG (not 60).<br />
<br />So....it seems reasonable to say that ~55 MPG, maybe 60 MPG is the ASYMPTOTE for MPG of small Prius-like cars, and unless a big aero drag reduction is added, won't change anytime soon.&nbsp; Then....the CAFE standards (including larger vehicles) will probably have many cars in the 40-50 MPG range....no higher.&nbsp;<br />
<br />However, if 85% of all cars were in the 40 - 50 MPG range, that would be a really great mid-range goal for the USA.<br />
<br />Kevin<br /><br /></span></div>
