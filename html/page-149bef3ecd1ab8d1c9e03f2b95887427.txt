        	<span class="paragraph-0">
				<p>Now here is what reality TV is supposed to be: a smartly executed idea that begins on a high note and just nosedives into a downward spiral of doom.</p>
			</span>
        	<span class="paragraph-1">
				<p>Host Nathan Fielder goes through his shares of ups and downs, but its not that when he fails the show fails along with him. Its when the spiraling happens where the biggest laughs kick in.</p>
			</span><div class="p402_hide"><div id="in-story">
    
	<div id="tncms-region-ads-in-story" class="tncms-region-ads"></div>
    
</div>
</div>
                    <p>Similar to how Gordon Ramsey visits restaurants and kick-starts their popularity in "Ramsays Kitchen Nightmares," Canadian comedian Nathan Fielder also goes on rescue sprees on failing businesses and attempts to jump-start their successes by offering innovative and unorthodox marketing suggestions to bump up traffic in their businesses.</p>
                
                    <p>Among these ideas are a 8-minute delivery guarantee for a Pizza parlor when it takes 10 minutes to cook the pizza and a feces-flavored ice cream at a frozen yogurt shop.</p>
                
                    <p>Yes, both of these ideas sound stupid. But there is genius in Fielders plots, and viewers never see it until later on in the episode. The elaboration on Fielders schemes are sheer brilliance in "Nathan For You," and even though they at first seem like bombshells of ideas, they expand to something far brilliant and funnier than expected.</p>
                
                    <p>Thats not to say that his ideas arent prone to failure. It just means that its shocking and exciting when his plans work and quirky and hilarious when they dont. Thats the genius of it: viewers can enjoy the show no matter the turnout.</p>
                
                    <p>Lets take a moment also, to elaborate on Nathans job as the show host. Hes more intuitive and qualified than you would predict. Before he became a comedian, he actually studied school in Canada and got a business degree. Both of his professions are shown wonderfully in this inverted, hilarious show.</p>
                
                    <p>As a businessman, Nathan is creative and insightful, reacting to what hes been taught and trying to apply his lessons to the businesses hes trying to help. As a comedian and host, however, Fielder is masterful.</p>
                
                    <p>Hes the kinda guy that would enter a job interview with a turtle named Ted giving him voice directions through a secret line.</p>
                
                    <p>Yes, you read that right. A turtle. Named Ted.</p>
                
                    <p>No, the turtle isnt fake. No, his applicants didnt know he was repeating what the turtle said. Yes, he got the job.</p>
                
                    <p>There are two shows premiering on the Thursday slot on the Comedy Channel: "Nathan For You" and "The Ben Show." Go and watch "Nathan For You."</p>
                
                    <p>Its a funny show, the ideas are outlandishly innovative and Fielder is likable and fun as the shows host.</p>

// rework section list
    function updateSectionListFooter() {         
        var allSectionsFooter = '';
        var allTypesFooter = '';
        $('#searchSectionsFooter :checked').each(function(){
            if ($(this).val() == 'all'){
                $('#sectionCollectionFooter').removeAttr('name');
                $('#typeCollectionFooter').removeAttr('name');    
            }
            else if($(this).attr('class') == 'section-box regular'){
                $('#sectionCollectionFooter').attr('name','c[]');
                if (allSectionsFooter == ''){
                    allSectionsFooter = $(this).val();
                }
                else{
                    allSectionsFooter += (',' + $(this).val());
                };
            }
            else if($(this).attr('class') == 'type-box regular'){
                $('#typeCollectionFooter').attr('name','t');
                if (allTypesFooter == ''){
                    allTypesFooter = $(this).val();
                }
                else{
                    allTypesFooter += (',' + $(this).val());
                };
            };
        });
        $('#sectionCollectionFooter').val(allSectionsFooter)
        $('#typeCollectionFooter').val(allTypesFooter)
    }
    $(function() {
        $('#searchSectionsFooter input').click(updateSectionListFooter);
