        <div class="fb-like" data-href="http://www.examiner.com/article/live-from-nyias-subaru-xv-crosstrek-hybrid-debuts-at-2013-new-york-auto-show" data-layout="button_count" data-send="false" data-show-faces="false" data-width="90"></div>        
  <div property="schema:articleBody content:encoded" class="article__body">
    <p>An all-wheel-drive system is bound to sap fuel economy, and nearly every Subaru product is offered with AWD standard. To boost gas mileage, the automaker unveiled its first hybrid today at the 2013 New York International Auto Show ‚Äì a gas-electric version of the XV Crosstrek, which is a version of the Impreza compact hatchback with a raised suspension and minor styling tweaks.</p>
<p>Unfortunately, the hybrid isn't doing much for fuel economy ‚Äì ratings of 28 miles per gallon in the city and 34 on the highway are nothing special, especially for a small vehicle, in today's market even for gas-only vehicles. And it's only a slight improvement over the regular XV. It's notable that Subaru didn't even mention the car's mileage during today's press conference.</p>
<p>The hybrid technology adds 300 pounds to the XV's weight, and eats up a bit of its cargo space.</p>
<p>The standard XV Crosstrek starts at $21,995 with a 2.0-liter 148-horsepower engine and a choice of a five-speed manual or continuously variable automatic transmission. Fuel economy ratings are 23 miles per gallon in the city and 30 mpg on the highway with the manual and 25 mpg city / 33 mpg highway with the CVT. Pricing for the hybrid hasn't yet been announced.</p>
<p>The XV Crosstrek's 13-horsepower electric motor will be able to power the car on its own up to 25 miles per hour, and help the gas engine achieve better performance and fuel economy at higher speeds. The improved acceleration, if significant, could provide another reason to pay extra for the XV hybrid. </p>
<p>Visual distinctions between the gas and hybrid XV versions include hybrid-exclusive green paint, unique alloy wheels, and clear taillights instead of red. </p>
<p>Subaru also promises better capability than other crossover hybrids; think of the XV as more as a compact car with improved off-road ability, though, than a true competitor to a Honda CR-V or Toyota RAV4.</p>
<p>The 2014 XV Crosstrek Hybrid will go on sale in late 2013. Subaru says it is the automaker's first step toward further electrification, but with any luck its future efforts will achieve a better fuel economy improvement that the automaker will be proud enough to mention. </p>
