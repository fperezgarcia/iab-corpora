                <!-- article start --><h3 class="byline">By Jonathan Welsh</h3><div class="mceTemp" style="text-align: left;">
<dl class="wp-caption alignleft caption-alignleft " style="width: 359px;">
<dt class="wp-caption-dt"><img class="size-full wp-image-5" src="http://s.wsj.net/public/resources/images/OB-JV464_cruze2_E_20100903140457.jpg" alt="" width="359" height="239" /></dt>
<dd class="wp-caption-dd wp-cite-dd" style="text-align: right;">General Motors Corp.</dd>
<dd class="wp-caption-dd" style="text-align: left;">Hatchback styling gives the Chevy Cruze a flowing shape some drivers may find more appealing than the sedan version.</dd>
</dl>
</div>
<div><span>Chevrolet will begin selling its Cruze compact sedan in the U.S. later this month, but there&#8217;s another, arguably more versatile version of the Cruze that won&#8217;t be available to buyers here.</span> </div>
<p>Today the car maker released photos of a new 5-door hatchback model it plans to unveil at the Paris auto show later  this month. The new version is designed for other markets like Europe, where, the company says, hatchbacks account for about 65% of the popular compact-car market. Chevy says it will not sell the car in the U.S., which is no surprise because the popular wisdom in the industry is that Americans don&#8217;t like hatchbacks. Indeed U.S. automotive history is littered with the marketing wreckage of hatchbacks that didn&#8217;t make it.</p>
<p>But am I the only one who thinks the Cruze hatchback looks good enough to sell here?</p>
<p>But those feelings are changing, especially among younger drivers who increasingly expect versatility in their vehicles. Generally you can carry a lot more in a hatch than in a sedan, and other car companies including Subaru, Mazda and Volkswagen have had success selling hatches here. Rival Ford Motor Co. is banking on Euro-style hatchback versions of its new Fiesta and Focus models to help re-establish it in the small-car market.</p>
<p>In the end a lot of people buy cars for their looks, and I think the Cruze hatch looks a lot better than the sedan. It&#8217;s the difference between dowdy and sexy. The new Cruze 5-door hatchback will go on sale in Europe mid 2011 and later in other markets. Despite what Chevy officials say now, I have a feeling the hatch will reach our shores, too, within a couple of years.</p>
