	  <h1 id="article-title" itemprop="headline">Chrysler to kill the Town and Country minivan in 2014</h1>
	  
	  <div class="article-info">	  
		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2012-05-29T15:26-04:00">Published May 29, 2012</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FoxNews.com</div></div>

				<div class="article-text" itemprop="articleBody">			
		  	<p>Chrysler is killing the Town and Country minivan in 2014, according to a report in <a href="http://www.autonews.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20120528/OEM03/305289959/1251/marchionne-no-compass-1-minivan">Automotive News</a>.</p>

<p>Chrysler CEO Sergio Marchionne told the paper that the automaker will consolidate its minivan lineup under one nameplate, the Dodge Grand Caravan, and replace the Town and Country with a unique three-row crossover. With 90 percent of Chrysler Group dealers now offering cars from all of the company’s brands, it is the latest effort to remove model redundancy from their showrooms.</p>

<p>But where have we heard this story before?</p>

<p>In October of last year, Marchionne told the same paper that he was planning to do the complete opposite and <a href="http://www.foxnews.com/leisure/2011/10/10/dodge-to-kill-its-minivan-in-2013/">kill the Grand Caravan</a> at the expense of the Town and Country.</p>

<p>Apparently, things change quickly in today’s automotive industry.</p>

<p>Chrysler’s minivans have combined to be the best-sellers in the segment since the automaker created it in 1984, although each has been outsold individually at various times in recent years by the Toyota Sienna and Honda Odyssey.</p>

<p>As of the end of April, the Grand Caravan was outselling the Town and Country 45,645 to 38,507 for 2012 with the Honda Odyssey holding strong in third place at 38,133.</p></div>
