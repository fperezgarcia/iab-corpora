        <h1 class="subject">What is the difference between art and entertainment?</h1>
                  <div class="content">Art and entertainment are both things that are perceived.  Good art and good entertainment both produce a satisfying experience for the beholder.  What is the difference?</div>
                <ul class="meta">
          <li><abbr title="2006-06-28 06:59:13 +0000">7 years ago</abbr></li>
                                <li class="report-abuse">  
                              <a href="/question/report?qid=20060627235913AADPta6&amp;kid=NbUvWzK1WTgqL4pE66S.&amp;date=2006-06-27+23%3A59%3A13&amp;.crumb=&amp;s=q" title="Report this question"><span class="report-link">Report Abuse</span></a>
                          </li>
                  </ul>
                  <h2 class="additional-details">Additional Details</h2>
                                    <div class="additional-details">Everyone has raised some interesting points but I still haven&#039;t found an answer which really explains it.  <br>
<br>
Some of you have characterized art requiring a creative process but so does entertainment.  Also some have said art is expression but entertainment can also be expression.  Just think of comedians who express their views in often hilarious ways which offer real social commentary just like art might.<br>
<br>
Entertainment has been associated with enjoyment.  If there is no enjoyment, there is no entertainment.  However, not all forms of entertainment are enjoyed by everyone just like not all forms of art are enjoyed by everyone.  Even entertainment within the same genre can be both liked and disliked by different people.  <br>
<br>
The most common theme I see in your answers is that art is for thinking and entertainment is for enjoyment.  I don&#039;t think this holds up because the same thought processes that make art stimulating also makes entertainment stimulating.</div>

            <div class="content">William Schuman, the great American Pulitzer Prize winning composer, educator (Head of the Juilliard School of Music) and administrator (Head of Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts) once provided a distinction which I find meaningful. When asked if popular music could be art, he replied by stating that there is a difference between popular and classical music. In popular music, the goal of the creator is that the music be popular and its success is measured by how popular (or entertaining) it is. For art music, the goal is to satisfy the creative expression of the composer. Its success can be measured in how well the final product satisfies the creative expression of the composer. While the composer of art music might hope for the audience to appreciate his or her work, that is not the goal.<br>
<br>
While such a notion has changed in the history of Western Civilization, the great danger in my mind is to suggest that art must entertain and therefore be judged by its economic viability.</div>

            <div class="content">Art can be studied, but Entertainment can be studied as well. You can even have a passion or a talent for Entertainment, <br>
as in Art. <br>
<br>
The big difference between the two is purpose. <br>
<br>
     In Art, whether it is Painting, Sculpture, Music, Theatre, or even Film (not to mention the many other Art forms in the world), it is created for the individual that created it.  Sometimes Art is created during a learning process, sometimes as an expression of feeling, and other times, just to prove it can be done.<br>
     In Entertainment, which includes pretty much all of the performing arts in addition to Sports and Television, it is created expressly for the enjoyment of others.  No matter what form it takes, it is defined as Entertainment when it is enjoyed in one way or another by other people.<br>
<br>
So I guess the difference is really in who is enjoying it.<br>
If only the creator and maybe a select few others are enjoying it, then it is Art.  If it is enjoyed by many, it is Entertainment.<br>
And if it is enjoyed by many and the creator with his select few others, it is Arts Entertainment.</div>
