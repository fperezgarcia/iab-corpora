</div><p></p>Hinting at the look of the next Murano, Nissan debuted the Resonance Concept at the North American International Auto Show this week.
<p>Much like the original Murano, the Resonance features striking styling that previews the look of future Nissan vehicles, while taking the crossover concept to new levels of aerodynamics and efficiency.</p>
<p>Under the hood is a 2.5-liter gasoline engine, paired to "one motor, two clutch" proprietary hybrid system. Power is put to the road through all four wheels using an Xtronic CVT continuously-variable transmission.</p>	    <div class="ad floating-ad" id="adsense1" style="width:300px;height:250px;">

<p>Behind the concept's five-spoke, 22-inch wheels, regenerative braking energy is harnessed to top up the lithium-ion battery, as with many other hybrids.</p>
<p>As you can see in our live images, the Resonance has a distinctive design, with V-shaped light units front and rear and a large, chrome-lipped grille.</p>
<p>The roof appears to float on glass towards the back of the car, where the traditional C and D pillars seem to disappear. Instead, the side window glass curves seamlessly into the rear light units.</p>
<p>Inside, the Resonance has the sort of clean, modern feel familiar to the Leaf, albeit much more dramatically styled--the center console controls appear to hover science-fiction style in a glass enclosure.</p>
<p>Nissan says the Resonance will appeal to customers whose "fashion and design choices make a statement".</p>
