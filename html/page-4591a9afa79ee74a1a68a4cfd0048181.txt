                                                                                                                        <p>
	The National Highway Traffic Safety Administrations issues recalls affecting millions of vehicles each year, including a total of 16.2 million models in 2012 alone.</p>
<p>
	In June of this year, a stand-off between the NHTSA and Chrysler regarding Jeep recalls shed some light on the recall process. According to the NHTSA, 2.7 million Jeep Grand Cherokee and Jeep Liberty SUVs were at risk of fires caused by fuel leaks. Chrysler initially refused to recall the models, waiting nearly two weeks before giving in.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/autos/hyundai-kia-recalls-1-9m-vehicles-due-issues-air-bags-brake-lights-article-1.1306369">RELATED: HYUNDAI AND KIA RECALL NEARLY 1.9M VEHICLES DUE TO ISSUES WITH AIR BAGS AND BRAKE LIGHTS</a></strong><p>
	The Chrysler-NHTSA standoff remains the largest recall of the year, with the top ten recalls so far this year already affecting nearly 8.6 million vehicles. Below are the top ten largest recalls to hit the auto industry in the U.S. so far this year, according to a <a href="http://autos.yahoo.com/news/top-10-biggest-recalls-of-2013-174809182.html" target="_blank">report</a> from Yahoo!</p>
<p>
	<strong>10. Chrysler</strong>, 442,000 vehicles: Potential problems with the active head restraint affect the 2011-2013 Chrysler Sebring, Chrysler 200, and Dodge Avenger sedans, 2011-2013 Jeep Liberty SUV, and 2011-2012 Dodge Nitro.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/autos/chrylser-2-7-million-jeeps-recalled-defying-government-warning-article-1.1376066">RELATED: CHRYSLER FINALLY COMPLIES WITH JEEP RECALL AFTER DEFYING GOVERNMENT WARNING</a></strong><p>
	<strong>9. BMW</strong>, 500,545 vehicles: A potential problem with the electrical system in 2008-2012 1 Series, 2007-2011 3 Series, and 2009-2011 Z4 models could lead to stalling.</p>
<p>
	<strong>8. Toyota</strong>, 510,000 vehicles: Potentially faulty airbags were the target of this recall that affected Toyota Corolla, Matrix, Sequoia, Tundra, and Lexus SC430 models built between 2001 and 2003.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/autos/general-motors-recall-targets-suvs-catching-fire-parked-article-1.1375521">RELATED: GENERAL MOTORS RECALL TARGETS SUVS CATCHING FIRE WHILE PARKED</a></strong><p>
	<strong>7. Honda</strong>, 561,000 Honda vehicles: Potentially faulty airbags were also cited in this Honda recall that affected 2001-2003 Civic sedans, 2002 Odyssey minivans, and 2002-2003 CR-V crossovers.</p>
<p>
	<strong>6. Kia</strong>, 623,658 vehicles: Malfunctioning brake lights on 2007 Sedona, 2007-2010 Rondo and Sportage, 2007-2011 Sorento, 2010-2011 Soul, and 2011 Optima vehicles.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/autos/chrysler-recall-affects-490-000-vehicles-worldwide-article-1.1397080">RELATED: CHRYSLER RECALL AFFECTS 490,000 VEHICLES WORLDWIDE</a></strong><p>
	<strong>5. Subaru</strong>, 633,842 vehicles: Potentially faulty lamp wiring in 2009-2013 Pilot SUVs and 2011-2013 Odyssey minivans may lead to a short circuit, leading to this recall.</p>
<p>
	<strong>4. Honda</strong>, 748,000 vehicles: The possibility of improper deployment of the driver’s-side airbags led to this recall affecting 2009-2013 Honda Pilot SUVs and 2011-2013 Odyssey minivans.</p>
<p>
	<strong>3. Toyota</strong>, 752,000 vehicles: Toyota led the pack for manufacturer recalls in 2012, with a total of over 5 million vehicles pulled. So far, the manufacturer only makes two appearances in the top ten largest recalls of the year, with this larger recall of 2003-2004 Corolla and Matrix models landing in the top three.</p>
<p>
	<strong>2. Hyundai</strong>, more than 1 million vehicles: A whole slew of Hyundai cars, including 2007-2009 Accents, 2007-2010 Elantras, and 2007-2011 Santa Fe SUVs, were affected by the same brake light recall that affected sister company Kia. Between the two, more than 1.6 million models were affected, which is still more than 1 million less models than the largest recall so far this year.</p>
<p>
	<strong>1. Chrysler</strong>, 2.7 million vehicles: Of course, Chrysler’s giving in to the NHTSA leads the pack so far this year. The automaker recalled 2.7 million Jeep models due to the potential for rear-end crashes to result in fires.</p>
