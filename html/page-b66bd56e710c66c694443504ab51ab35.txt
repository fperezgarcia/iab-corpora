			<p>LOS ANGELES ¬ñ Toyota held the global unveiling of the all-new RAV4 today, designed to get 31 m.p.g. when it goes on sale in January.</p><p>Pricing has not been released.
<p>The new crossover drops the V6, offering only the continuation of the 2.4-liter, four-cylinder but with a new 6-speed automatic transmission, said William Fay, general manager of the Toyota division of Toyota Motor Sales.
<p>&bull; <b>Related story:</b> <A href="http://www.freep.com/article/20121128/BUSINESS01/121128047/Live-updates-from-2012-Los-Angeles-Auto-Show-?odyssey=mod|newswell|text|FRONTPAGE|s" target="_blank">Live updates from the 2012 Los Angeles Auto Show</A>
<p>Also gone is the optional third row of seating, which had a take rate of only 10%.
<p>¬ìAfter researching it, we found someone who wants a third row also wants more room overall,¬î Fay said, making the larger Highlander a better alternative.
<p>Another big change: The mounting of the spare tire on a swinging rack on the back of the RAV4 is gone. Integrating its storage inside the vehicle adds to a cleaner, more modern look in a vehicle with a more premium interior.
<p>"The RAV4 almost literally removed a monkey off its back by doing away with the spare tire hanging off its rear,¬î said Edmunds.com Senior Analyst Jessica Caldwell. 
<p>¬ìWhat's left is a sleeker, modern vehicle that competes much better with rivals in a small SUV segment whose market share has nearly doubled in the last 10 years,¬î Caldwell said. ¬ìAnd since small SUVs are often cross-shopped against midsize sedans, Toyota now has a stronger alternative for hesitant Camry shoppers."
<p>Toyota has sold 145,103 RAV4s so far this year, up 36%. The figures include electric versions that went on sale about 60 days ago and are projected to account for about 1,000 sales a year, Fay said.
<p>The RAV4 EV, which is sold only in California, keeps the old body style until the crossover is redesigned again years from now.
