            <dl><dd><i>This article discusses an aspect of the <a href="/wiki/Re-imagined_Series" title="Re-imagined Series" class="mw-redirect">Re-imagined Series</a> version of the Twelve Colonies. For information on the <a href="/wiki/Original_Series" title="Original Series" class="mw-redirect">Original Series</a> version, see <a href="/wiki/The_Twelve_Colonies_(TOS)" title="The Twelve Colonies (TOS)" class="mw-redirect">The Twelve Colonies (TOS)</a>.</i>
</dd></dl>
<p><br />
The <a href="/wiki/The_Twelve_Colonies_(RDM)" title="The Twelve Colonies (RDM)" class="mw-redirect">Twelve Colonies</a>, like most civilizations, have a rich historical record of <b>arts and literature</b> as well as other works. Their <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_Twelve_Colonies" title="Government of the Twelve Colonies">democratic republic</a> gives its citizens many freedoms of expression in addition to freedom of the press. Colonials have paintings, abstract designs and a variety of musical traditions.
</p><p>Additionally, many functional items produced on the Colonies adhere to an aesthetic, even at a cost to efficiency.

<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Literature">Literature</span></h2>
<h3> <span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span></h3>
<h4> <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Physics_of_Religion_and_Spirituality"> <i>The Physics of Religion and Spirituality</i> </span></h4>
<p>A copy of <i>The Physics of Religion and Spirituality</i> is included in the package of <a href="/wiki/Zoe_Graystone" title="Zoe Graystone">Zoe Graystone</a>'s personal effects that <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Stark" title="Natalie Stark">Natalie Stark</a> gives to <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Graystone" title="Amanda Graystone">Amanda Graystone</a> at <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Park" title="Apollo Park">Apollo Park</a> (<a href="/wiki/CAP" title="CAP">CAP</a>: <a href="/wiki/Rebirth" title="Rebirth">Rebirth</a>).
</p>
<h3> <span class="mw-headline" id="Political">Political</span></h3>
<h4> <span class="mw-headline" id="Tom_Zarek.27s_book"> Tom Zarek's book </span></h4>
<p><b>Tom Zarek's book</b> is a political book of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Zarek" title="Tom Zarek" class="mw-redirect">Tom Zarek</a>'s beliefs, which he wrote while imprisoned. The manuscript is smuggled from his prison and published, but due to its yet-unspecified content, the book became banned in certain places throughout the Twelve Colonies, particularly the fleet academy.
</p><p>During the incident on the <i><a href="/wiki/Astral_Queen" title="Astral Queen">Astral Queen</a></i>, Captain <a href="/wiki/Lee_Adama" title="Lee Adama" class="mw-redirect">Lee Adama</a> tells Zarek that he read the book and that it challenged the way he thought about things on the Colonies (<a href="/wiki/TRS" title="TRS" class="mw-redirect">TRS</a>: <a href="/wiki/Bastille_Day" title="Bastille Day">Bastille Day</a>).
</p>
<h4> <span class="mw-headline" id="My_Triumphs.2C_My_Mistakes"> <i>My Triumphs, My Mistakes</i> </span></h4>
<dl><dd><i>Main article&#58; <a href="/wiki/My_Triumphs,_My_Mistakes" title="My Triumphs, My Mistakes">My Triumphs, My Mistakes</a></i>
</dd></dl>
<p>After returning to the <a href="/wiki/The_Fleet_(RDM)" title="The Fleet (RDM)" class="mw-redirect">The Fleet</a> months after the exodus from <a href="/wiki/New_Caprica" title="New Caprica">New Caprica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gaius_Baltar" title="Gaius Baltar">Gaius Baltar</a> wrote his manifesto, in which he criticizes the Colonial government system and highlights the class factions between the "<a href="/wiki/Caprica_(RDM)" title="Caprica (RDM)">Caprican</a> elite" and the lower classes in the Colonies. Much like Tom Zarek's book, Baltar's manifesto was smuggled out by his <a href="/wiki/Alan_Hughes" title="Alan Hughes">lawyer</a> and published, despite attempts to ban and destroy the book by <a href="/wiki/Laura_Roslin" title="Laura Roslin">Laura Roslin</a> and her supporters (<a href="/wiki/TRS" title="TRS" class="mw-redirect">TRS</a>: "<a href="/wiki/Dirty_Hands" title="Dirty Hands">Dirty Hands</a>", "<a href="/wiki/The_Son_Also_Rises" title="The Son Also Rises">The Son Also Rises</a>").
</p>
<h3> <span class="mw-headline" id="Prose">Prose</span></h3>
<h4> <span class="mw-headline" id="Dark_Day"><i>Dark Day</i></span></h4>
<p>Edward Prima is the author of the novel <i>Dark Day</i>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/William_Adama" title="William Adama">William Adama</a>, who had <i>Dark Day</i> in his personal library on <i><a href="/wiki/Galactica_(RDM)" title="Galactica (RDM)" class="mw-redirect">Galactica</a></i>, gives it to <a href="/wiki/Laura_Roslin" title="Laura Roslin">Laura Roslin</a> as a gift (<a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">Water</a>). She considers it to be "one of those classics I've never gotten around to reading." Given the context of their conversation, notably Roslin's reference to <i>A Murder on Picon</i>, it is likely a mystery.
</p><p>Roslin returns the book, to Adama's consternation, after the prognosis of her illness sharply worsens (<a href="/wiki/TRS" title="TRS" class="mw-redirect">TRS</a>: <a href="/wiki/Flight_of_the_Phoenix" title="Flight of the Phoenix">Flight of the Phoenix</a>).
</p>
<h4> <span class="mw-headline" id="The_French_Lieutenant.27s_Woman"><i>The French Lieutenant's Woman</i></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:144px;"><a href="/wiki/File:The_French_Lieutenant%27s_Woman.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="http://media.battlestarwiki.org/images/2/21/The_French_Lieutenant%27s_Woman.jpg" width="142" height="158" class="thumbimage" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:The_French_Lieutenant%27s_Woman.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="/w/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>A copy of <i>The French Lieutenant's Woman</i>.</div></div></div>
<p><i><b>The French Lieutenant's Woman</b></i><sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0">[1]</a></sup> is part of William Adama's formidable library of books on <i>Galactica</i>.
</p><p>It is located on the shelf behind the couch in Adama's quarters (<a href="/wiki/TRS" title="TRS" class="mw-redirect">TRS</a>: <a href="/wiki/Black_Market" title="Black Market">Black Market</a>). 
</p>
<h4> <span class="mw-headline" id="A_Murder_on_Picon"><i>A Murder on Picon</i></span></h4>
<p><i><b>A Murder on Picon</b></i> is a mystery novel, apparently taking place on <a href="/wiki/The_Twelve_Colonies_(RDM)#Picon" title="The Twelve Colonies (RDM)" class="mw-redirect">Picon</a>. 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Laura_Roslin" title="Laura Roslin">Laura Roslin</a> brought the book with her on the trip to <i><a href="/wiki/Galactica" title="Galactica" class="mw-redirect">Galactica</a></i> prior to its decommissioning ceremonies (<a href="/wiki/TRS" title="TRS" class="mw-redirect">TRS</a>: <a href="/wiki/Miniseries" title="Miniseries" class="mw-redirect">Miniseries</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">Water</a>").
</p>
<h4> <span class="mw-headline" id="Blood_Runs_at_Midnight"> <i>Blood Runs at Midnight</i> </span></h4>
<p>A mystery novel given to William Adama by President Roslin as a gift (<a href="/wiki/TRS" title="TRS" class="mw-redirect">TRS</a>: <a href="/wiki/A_Day_in_the_Life" title="A Day in the Life">A Day in the Life</a>).
</p>
<h4> <span class="mw-headline" id="Love_and_Bullets"> <i>Love and Bullets</i> </span></h4>
<p>A murder mystery written by Nick Taylo that is set in <a href="/wiki/Caprica_City" title="Caprica City">Caprica City</a>. Adama reads part of the book to Roslin when she lies in <i>Galactica'</i>s <a href="/wiki/Sickbay" title="Sickbay">sickbay</a> to receive <a href="/wiki/Doloxan" title="Doloxan">Doloxan</a> treatments (<a href="/wiki/TRS" title="TRS" class="mw-redirect">TRS</a>: <a href="/wiki/The_Ties_That_Bind" title="The Ties That Bind">The Ties That Bind</a>).
</p>

<h3> <span class="mw-headline" id="Tattoos">Tattoos</span></h3>
<h4> <span class="mw-headline" id="Tauron_Tattoos"><a href="/wiki/Tauron" title="Tauron">Tauron</a> Tattoos</span></h4>

The most extensive tattoos are found among members of the <a href="/wiki/Ha%27la%27tha" title="Ha'la'tha">Ha'la'tha</a> crime syndicate, and many Capricans associate the practice with the Ha'la'tha (<a href="/wiki/CAP" title="CAP">CAP</a>: <a href="/wiki/Gravedancing" title="Gravedancing">Gravedancing</a>). <h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Sculpture">Sculpture</span></h2>
<p>In addition to the <a href="/wiki/Arrow_of_Apollo" title="Arrow of Apollo">Arrow of Apollo</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Delphi_Museum_of_the_Colonies" title="Delphi Museum of the Colonies">Delphi Museum of the Colonies</a> contains a large collection of ancient pottery.  Located in numerous display cases in the exhibit halls spanning the museum's levels, this large collection of vases, jars, pots and stone fragments form a rough outline of the Colonials' artistic history.  Many of the artifacts and art pieces are quite elaborate and appear to be in the detailed Corinthian Style.
</p>

<p>Popular Colonial music can be heard in Boomer's apartment on Caprica in the episode "<a href="/wiki/Downloaded" title="Downloaded">Downloaded</a>" and in <a href="/wiki/Joe%27s_bar" title="Joe's bar">Joe's bar</a> in "<a href="/wiki/Taking_a_Break_From_All_Your_Worries" title="Taking a Break From All Your Worries">Taking a Break From All Your Worries</a>". Another song is playing as Lee Adama enters the bar on <i><a href="/wiki/Prometheus_(ship)" title="Prometheus (ship)">Prometheus</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Black_Market" title="Black Market">Black Market</a>) <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[8]</a></sup>.
</p><p>In addition, a popular sing-a-long song is "99 Bottles of Ambrosia"<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[9]</a></sup>, which <a href="/wiki/Marcia_Case" title="Marcia Case">Marcia Case</a> throws out there as a recommendation after <a href="/wiki/Kara_Thrace" title="Kara Thrace">Kara Thrace</a> comments on the humdrum of their up-until-then-uneventful search and rescue mission for a missing <a href="/wiki/Raptor" title="Raptor">Raptor</a> (<a href="/wiki/TRS" title="TRS" class="mw-redirect">TRS</a>: <a href="/wiki/Razor" title="Razor">Razor</a>).
</p><p>Roughly 60 BCH, musical styles emerged on <a href="/wiki/Caprica_(RDM)" title="Caprica (RDM)">Caprica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tauron" title="Tauron">Tauron</a>, themselves later repeated more than 150,000 years later on the second <a href="/wiki/Earth_(RDM)" title="Earth (RDM)">Earth</a>; these include alternative hip-hop, of which the only known artists are Tauron (<a href="/wiki/CAP" title="CAP">CAP</a>: <a href="/wiki/The_Reins_of_a_Waterfall" title="The Reins of a Waterfall" class="mw-redirect">The Reins of a Waterfall</a>) as well as forms of punk rock and dance music (<a href="/wiki/Caprica_pilot" title="Caprica pilot"><i>Caprica</i> pilot</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Aesthetics">Aesthetics</span></h2>
<p>Colonial <a href="http://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aesthetics" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Aesthetics">aesthetics</a> lead to an avoidance of right angles.
</p>
<h3> <span class="mw-headline" id="Octagons">Octagons</span></h3>
<p>Colonial printouts, photographs, videos, and <a href="/wiki/Computers_in_the_Re-imagined_Series#Data_discs" title="Computers in the Re-imagined Series">data discs</a> are rarely rectangular; the corners are trimmed at 45 degree angles, creating snub rectangles, i.e. octagons. The reasoning for this is likely based on their aversion to right angles, as rectangles are, particularly for paper, more efficient<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[11]</a></sup>.
</p><p>This design choice is also evident in Colonial <a href="/wiki/Computers_in_the_Re-imagined_Series" title="Computers in the Re-imagined Series">computer systems</a> with windows and menus often having clipped corners.
</p>
<h3> <span class="mw-headline" id="Hexagons">Hexagons</span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Battlestar_Galactica_(RDM)" title="Battlestar Galactica (RDM)" class="mw-redirect">Re-imagined Series</a>, hexagons feature significantly as <a href="/wiki/Dog_tags" title="Dog tags">dog tags</a>, <a href="/wiki/Triad_(RDM)" title="Triad (RDM)">playing cards</a>, glass bottles and many other objects. This likely reflects both the dislike of right angles and the simplicity of polygons over curved shapes.
</p>
<h3> <span class="mw-headline" id="Pentagons">Pentagons</span></h3>
<p>While not a part of Colonial society <i>per se</i>, pentagons play a large part in the architecture of the lone Thirteenth Tribe structure encountered by the Colonials thus far, the <a href="/wiki/Temple_of_Five" title="Temple of Five" class="mw-redirect">Temple of Five</a>.  Much like the Colonials, it appears at least the ancient members of the Thirteenth Tribe were also wary of right angles.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span></h2>
<h3> <span class="mw-headline" id="Federal_Period">Federal Period</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mini-Caprica_City.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="http://media.battlestarwiki.org/images/thumb/f/f6/Mini-Caprica_City.jpg/180px-Mini-Caprica_City.jpg" width="180" height="99" class="thumbimage" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mini-Caprica_City.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="/w/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>Some Federal Period architecture in Caprica City</div></div></div>
<p>This style was mix of older and newer trends, utilizing traditional stone facades along with glass and steel. This architecture was found throughout <a href="/wiki/Caprica_City" title="Caprica City">Caprica City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delphi" title="Delphi">Delphi</a>, and other <a href="/wiki/Unnamed_cities_of_Caprica" title="Unnamed cities of Caprica">unnamed cities</a> prior to the Cylon attack.
</p><p>Several buildings in Caprica City had the form of a pyramid, but the cultural significance of this design choice is unknown <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[12]</a></sup>.
</p>
<h3> <span class="mw-headline" id="Ancient_Kobol">Ancient Kobol</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a href="/wiki/File:City_of_the_Gods.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="http://media.battlestarwiki.org/images/thumb/5/57/City_of_the_Gods.jpg/180px-City_of_the_Gods.jpg" width="180" height="113" class="thumbimage" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:City_of_the_Gods.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="/w/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>Bird's eye view of the <a href="/wiki/City_of_the_Gods" title="City of the Gods">City of the Gods</a> and its architecture</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Opera_House_ruins.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="http://media.battlestarwiki.org/images/thumb/e/ee/Opera_House_ruins.jpg/180px-Opera_House_ruins.jpg" width="180" height="67" class="thumbimage" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Opera_House_ruins.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="/w/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>The stone ruins of the Opera House</div></div></div> 
<p>In "<a href="/wiki/Kobol%27s_Last_Gleaming,_Part_I" title="Kobol's Last Gleaming, Part I">Kobol's Last Gleaming, Part I</a>", Roslin looks at schematics for the <a href="/wiki/City_of_the_Gods" title="City of the Gods">City of the Gods</a> on <a href="/wiki/Kobol_(RDM)" title="Kobol (RDM)">Kobol</a>. Its buildings, such as the <a href="/wiki/Temple" title="Temple">Temple</a>, <a href="/wiki/Forum" title="Forum">Forum</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Opera_House" title="Opera House">Opera House</a>, were made of stone and range in shape and size, and resemble classical Greco-Roman architecture.
</p><p>Later, the ruins of the Opera House are seen when <a href="/wiki/Gaius_Baltar" title="Gaius Baltar">Gaius Baltar</a> and others crash-land nearby. The building was made of stone and utilized decorative columns and pillars. While there, Baltar has a vision which may have represented the original appearance of the Opera House before it fell into disrepair.
</p>
<h3> <span class="mw-headline" id="Polygonal_Architecture">Polygonal Architecture</span></h3>
<p>Many places and objects within <i>Galactica</i> reflect early aesthetic choices that eschew right-angular forms. Many corridors, including ship bulkhead doors, are hexagonal. <a href="/wiki/CIC" title="CIC">CIC</a> elements, particularly the Command &amp; Control Center table, the tactical station table, and even the alignment of the displays of the <a href="/wiki/DRADIS" title="DRADIS">DRADIS</a> console, form a hexagonal appearance.
</p><p>This aesthetic appears to have waned during the construction of more advanced battlestars such as <i><a href="/wiki/Pegasus_(RDM)" title="Pegasus (RDM)" class="mw-redirect">Pegasus</a></i>, which uses far fewer hexagonal shapes.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p><br />
</p>
