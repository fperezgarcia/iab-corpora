                        <h1>Automotive and Diesel Mechanic Schools</h1>
						<!-- google_ad_section_start -->
<div style="font-size: 12px;"><img align="left" alt="Top Automotive and Diesel Schools" border="0" src="/categories/3/3DIIUBVY.jpg" style="margin: 3px 12px 8px 0px;" /> Formal training in automotive and diesel technology is a great way to enter the field of automotive repair and most auto repair courses only take about a year to complete. You can study to be an auto body technician, automotive service technician, diesel mechanic, motorcycle mechanic, or NASCAR technician. You can even learn to be a service manager for an auto repair shop. If you want to learn professional methods of repairing automobiles and diesel trucks, look for automotive and diesel schools and start driving down the road to success.<br /><br /><center>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Below Are Some Of The Accredited Automotive And Diesel Courses Available On Schoolsgalore.com:</h2>
</center>
<h3>Automotive Training</h3>
SchoolsGalore.com boasts a number of accredited automotive training schools where you can become an expert in repairing cars, trucks, motorcycles and/or diesel equipment. There are several branches of automotive training to consider, including collision repair and refinishing, diesel engine repair, automotive repair, and even NASCAR training. Those who aspire to a higher level may choose to take automotive service manager courses. Without question, the field of automotive technology offers a wide range of educational choices for those who want quick entry into the world of automotive and diesel repair. <br /><br />
<h3>Auto Body</h3>
Accredited auto body schools offer comprehensive training in collision repair and automobile refurbishing. Training in vehicle repair and maintenance takes a little over a year to complete. After an additional year or so of experience in the field, you can apply for Master Collision Repair and Refinish Technician certification. Learning to be a certified auto body technician can be a smart move for anyone who would enjoy working for an automotive dealership or auto body repair shop. You can find several accredited schools on SchoolsGalore.com where you can learn to be a collision repair specialist.<br /><br />
<h3>Automotive Service Management</h3>
A chosen few with leadership ability can rise to the level of <a href="/programs/management-schools.html">manager</a> by enrolling in a course in automotive service management. You can complete automotive service management programs in as little as two years to become an Accredited Automotive Manager (AAM). Training for automotive service managers includes learning to accurately assess mechanical problems and see to it that all necessary repairs are performed to the customers' satisfaction. You will learn how to professionally manage an automotive repair business, including preparing invoices, dealing with insurance claims, ordering parts, personnel issues, and much more. <br /><br />
<h3>Automotive Service Technician</h3>
Gone are the days when auto mechanics were self-taught. Modern automotive service technician training produces highly-skilled auto service professionals who are capable of maintaining and repairing today's complex automotive engines and electrical components. Automotive service technician schools will give you plenty of hands-on, practical training in the uses of electronic diagnostic equipment and computer-based automotive technology, as well as standard hand tools and power tools. The typical automotive service technician course takes about a year to achieve a <a href="/vocational-schools/vocational-certificates.html">certificate of completion</a>; you can also find two-year associate's degree programs in automotive service technology available. <br /><br />
<h3>Diesel Mechanic</h3>
Formal diesel mechanic training can qualify you to repair and maintain diesel engines in a professional environment, and accredited diesel mechanic schools are plentiful across North America. Diesel mechanic courses can take about six months to obtain professional certification; or last up to two years for associate's degrees. Voluntary certification is offered by the <a href="http://www.asecert.org" target="_blank">National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence</a> (ASE) to those who have completed training in automotive and diesel technology and have a minimum of a year's work experience. If your desire is to learn to repair the big rigs, then accredited schools for diesel repair are the obvious choice.<br /><br />
<h3>Motorcycle Mechanic</h3>
The motorcycle mechanic schools will provide certified training in small engine repair and motorcycle servicing. You can easily find accredited motorcycle mechanic training on SchoolsGalore.com to satisfy that urge to take a bike down to its individual parts and put it all back in better condition than before. You can learn to inspect brakes, spark plugs, carburetors and electrical systems to find and fix the sources of mechanical failure. Good training in motorcycle repair can qualify you to work on all types of small engines, from outboard motors to snowmobiles and beyond.<br /><br />
<h3>Nascar</h3>
If you are fascinated by the world of stock car racing, just imagine how exciting it would be to learn to be a member of a NASCAR pit crew! That possibility can become a reality with accredited NASCAR training. The National Association for Stock Car Auto Racing has partnered with NASCAR schools to offer a complete automotive technology program specific to NASCAR. With just over a year of NASCAR education, you will be qualified to join the automotive industry in an entry-level position and begin earning even more valuable experience in the field of stock car racing. What a thrill!<br /><br /><center>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Learn More About Accredited Automotive And Diesel Schools And Courses</h2>
</center>There are quite a few vocational schools, technical institutes and colleges that offer training in automotive and diesel repair. The auto and diesel schools will carry accreditation from a nationally recognized accrediting agency. Accreditation provides a seal of approval for technical training that is of exceptional quality. <br /><br /> For instance, the <a href="http://www.accsct.org" target="_blank">Accrediting Commission of Career Schools and Colleges of Technology</a> (ACCSCT) grants accreditation to more than 65 automotive and diesel programs across the US. So, when you are ready to look seriously into getting good automotive training, be sure to verify accreditation status before you enroll in any particular automotive school.<br /><br /><center>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Find Some Of The Automotive And Diesel Mechanic Schools</h2>
</center><a href="#programselect">Select a specific program of interest</a> from the drop-down menu at the top of the page, and you will soon discover a wealth of accredited schools for automotive and diesel training. Contact as many schools as you like to request more information. Be sure to make the best decision that will drive you down the path to becoming a certified automotive and diesel technician.<br /><br /> <br />ABOUT ACCREDITATION: Official accreditation is your assurance of a quality education. Please be sure to check that your schools of interest have all been fully accredited by professional accrediting organizations that are approved by <a href="http://www.ed.gov/admins/finaid/accred/accreditation_pg8.html" target="_blank">The U.S. Department of Education (USDE)</a> or <a href="http://www.chea.org/default.asp" target="_blank">The Council for Higher Education Accreditation (CHEA)</a>, or both.</div>
