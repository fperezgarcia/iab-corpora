<p>The <a target="_blank" href="http://www.motortrend.com/roadtests/wagons/112_0606_2007_nissan_versa/index.html">Versa</a> has quickly risen to be Nissan&#8217;s most successful vehicle, but after eight years on the market, only five of which the Versa was sold in the U.S., it&#8217;s time for the current generation to retire. Today Nissan revealed its new Versa at the Shanghai motor show.</p>
<p>We got our first glimpse of the next <a href="http://wot.motortrend.com/nissan-releases-teaser-sketch-of-usbound-nextgen-versa-9190.html" target="_blank">Versa in the form of a teaser sketch</a> late last year, but it wasn&#8217;t until today that Nissan fully pulled the wraps off its new global hatchback. Like the current Versa, Nissan focuses on providing good looks with economical packaging.</p>
<p>Exterior designers take the Versa to new levels by completely revamping its sheetmetal. Nissan describes the exterior shape as &#8220;fluid and brisk&#8221; with its flowing, yet sharp lines. New headlights travel up the hood farther than before, while the front fascia receives a taught, new look also.</p>
<p>Class-leading comfort is said to be in play for the interior, and based on the early photos provided, it appears to be the case. Helping to meet that goal, engineers lengthened its wheelbase by nearly 4 inches to give it best-in-class rear leg room according to the automaker. Calm and refined are the words <a href="http://wot.motortrend.com/manufacturer/nissan/" target="_blank">Nissan</a> uses to describe the interior presence, and from what we can gather, the cabin is vastly improved. Cushy armrests and comfortable seats are also highlights of the new model.</p>
<p>Nissan has yet to confirm packaging for United States customers, but the Chinese show car employs the same 1.6-liter I-4 currently found in the compact car here, which is expected to carry over. There&#8217;s no official word on when the new Versa is scheduled to hit American shores, but we wouldn&#8217;t be surprised to see it before the end of the year. The U.S.-spec Versa is expected to be unveiled at the New York<a target="_blank" title=" auto show" href=" http://www.motortrend.com/auto_shows/"> auto show</a> later this week.</p>
