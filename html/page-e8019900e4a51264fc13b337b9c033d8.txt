                                                                                                                        <p>
	In the build-up to the reveal of the 2014 Chevrolet Corvette Stingray convertible at the Geneva Motor Show, General Motors has unveiled a set of photos showing off its sweet new drop-top.</p>
<p>
	<a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/autos/chevrolet-celebrates-60-years-corvette-gallery-1.1192910"><strong>PHOTOS: CORVETTE TURNS 60</strong></a></p>
<p>
	Imagining the Corvette convertible might have seemed easy when it was revealed earlier this year, but these photos show how the car takes advantage of the sleek lines of the car.</p>
<p>
	The roof itself uses a three-ply fabric, while GM reveals that the top features &quot;sound-absorbing padding and a glass rear window,&quot; helping to keep the cabin quiet when the roof is up. Inside, the convertible appears to be identical to the standard hard-top Stingray.</p>
<p>
	With its tonneau cover down, however, the stylish lines of the seventh-generation Corvette steal the show. Taking advantage of its looks, the Corvette Stingray features a &quot;waterfall&quot; design feature which brings the car's exterior color inside, playing up the convertible experience.&nbsp;</p>
<p>
	<a href="http://autos.nydailynews.com/q/2253/10337/How-much-does-a-2014-Chevrolet-Corvette-Stingray-cost-if-purchased-new"><b>READ MORE: HOW MUCH DOES A 2014 CHEVROLET CORVETTE STINGRAY COST IF PURCHASED NEW?</b></a></p>
<p>
	The convertible Corvette, along with its hard-top sister, are both slated to roll off dealers' lots in the third quarter of this year. The hard-top Corvette will start at $49,600 while pricing for the convertible model has not yet been announced.</p>
<div class="story-img" itemid="/polopoly/polopoly_fs/1.1278812!img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/landscape_635/corvette-stingray-convertible-1.jpg" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">

		General Motors</h4>
	<h4 class="caption" itemprop="description">
		The 2014 Corvette Stingray convertible features a three-ply tonneau cover designed to keep the cabin quiet.</h4>
</div>
<p>
	The convertible model is expected to feature the same technologies as the hard-top version, including the same race-ready 450 horsepower engine.&nbsp;</p>
<p>
	<strong><a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/autos/engineering-2014-chevrolet-corvette-stingray-article-1.1270736">READ MORE: ENGINEERING THE 2014 CORVETTE</a></strong></p>
<p>
	Engineering work on the 2014 Corvette Stingray required tearing down the pervious 2013 model and carrying over just two bolts from the previous generation's engine. Every other part on the seventh-generation Corvette is completely new.</p>
<p>
	&nbsp;</p>

<p>
	General Motors is expected to debut the convertible on Tuesday at the Geneva Motor Show.</p>
