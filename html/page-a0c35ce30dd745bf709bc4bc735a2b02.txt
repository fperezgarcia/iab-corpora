							<h1><strong>Bouncy Bouncy</strong></h1>
<p><strong>By Leigh Anne Jasheway<br />
<a href="http://nabbw.com/free-resources/glossary/?Term=NABBW" title="Definition for NABBW: NABBW is the acronym for the &quot;National Association of Baby Boomer Women&quot;." rel="glossary" class="">NABBW</a>&#8217;s <a href="http://nabbw.com/free-resources/glossary/?Term=Baby Boomers" title="Definition for Boomer: Person born during the Post-World War II baby boom between 1946 and 1964. Following World War II, several English-speaking countries  the United States, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand  experienced an unusual spike in " rel="glossary" class="">Boomer</a> Humor Expert</strong></p>
<p><img align="right" hspace="5" src="/photos/associates/54_associate_photo.jpg" >
<p>I bought a mini-trampoline this weekend. I was looking for a piece of home fitness equipment that met one specific requirement &#8211; it had to fit into a 3-foot square area of my bedroom.</p>
<p>All those infomercials that promise muscle toning, core-strengthening, and cardio training neglect to answer the most important question: Will the device fit in your house?</p>
<p>Or will you have to remove a major piece of furniture such as the sofa, requiring your guests to perch along the edge of the AbFlexGlideRowArator whenever they come over for margaritas and tofu chili? </p>
<p>Maybe you have a 3600 square foot house with a room designated as &#8220;the home gym,&#8221; but I&#8217;m lucky to have a room designated as &#8220;the bathroom.&#8221; The rule around here is nothing new comes in without something old being donated to Goodwill.</p>
<p>This is one reason I never have long-term guests &#8211; they worry that I may get up early, hit a few garage sales and come home with a floor lamp that takes up the same amount of space they do. It&#8217;s a legitimate concern; just ask my half-sister. She was exactly the same size as the wicker clothes hamper I found while she was visiting from California.</p>
<p>When I discovered the mini-tramp (love that word, by the way) at a local sporting goods store and realized it would fit in the bedroom without having to take anything out, I was ecstatic. It didn&#8217;t hurt that it was only $39 and came in one sleek box. I&#8217;ve had my fill of exercise equipment that arrives in multiple boxes and requires a screwdriver, wrench, hammer, ratchet set, drill press, circular saw, GPS device, latex gloves, and six rolls of duct tape to assemble. </p>
<p>I managed to put the mini-trampoline together in less than 15 minutes with just a wrench and the enclosed motivational CD featuring a sexy-sounding man with an Italian accent repeating, &#8220;You&#8217;re a smart and talented woman who can put together this product by yourself. And did I mention how attractive you are?&#8221; I got up to my target heart rate just listening to his dulcet tones.</p>
<p>My new &#8220;gym&#8221; is located right in front of a window so that I can look out at the birds and squirrels while I work out. Perhaps twenty-seven years of living with dogs has affected my brain, but I always feel more energetic when I see creatures darting across the yard. And if I yell &#8220;Squirrel!&#8221; my dogs also get a workout. It&#8217;s win-win. The only thing I have to be careful of is not getting overly enthusiastic and hitting my head on my 7-1/2-foot ceiling. I&#8217;m pretty sure a concussion doesn&#8217;t burn extra calories.</p>
<p>Before engaging in my first official bouncing session, I posted the yellow laminated &#8220;Trampoline Use Instructions&#8221; card in a highly visible place as suggested by the manual. I tacked the card to the closest wall and took a moment to read it over.
<ul>
<li><i>Do not allow more than one person on the trampoline at a time</i>. As far as I can tell, the only way that would be possible is if you had a small infant strapped to your chest. So please, if you break into my house with the intent of working out on my mini-trampoline and you happen to have a small infant strapped to your chest, remove said infant prior to getting on the equipment.</li>
<li><i>Use trampoline only with mature, knowledgeable supervision</i>. Well drat! Where am I going to find someone mature? I have no idea where to even start looking. The most mature person nearby is a neighbor in her eighties, but last week she put Spongebob Squarepants stickers on her bicycle helmet and did her hair like Pippi Longstocking, so she&#8217;s out.</li>
<li><i>While keeping the head erect, focus eyes on the trampoline toward the perimeter</i>. I&#8217;m sorry, but if my head is erect, I cannot see the trampoline. I know I&#8217;m supposed to have a third eye, but not in my ankles. Good thing I have the wiener dogs to keep watch on the perimeter for me when they&#8217;re not running for the squirrels.</li>
<li><i>Do not use the trampoline while under the influence of alcohol or drugs</i>. I&#8217;m willing to forgo the booze prior to working out, but what kind of drugs are they talking about? I&#8217;m on the estrogen patch and really think using the equipment while experiencing mood swings and hot flashes may be the more dangerous choice. Perhaps I should ask my doctor.</li>
<li><i>Make sure to use the bathroom before using the device</i>. Okay, it didn&#8217;t say that, but it should. So I scribbled it on the bottom of the list. I don&#8217;t want to take any chances.</li>
</ul>
<p>I&#8217;m ready now for my first real workout. Assuming I can find a mature person somewhere.</p>
<blockquote><p><em>Leigh Anne Jasheway, M.P.H. is a stress management and humor expert who helps women and men manage stress, embrace change, and become healthier by learning to lighten up. She speaks at 50-60 conferences and workshops every year and has been a national Speaking of Womens Health and Healthy Woman keynoter. Shes a member of the Association for Applied and Therapeutic Humor (AATH). Learn more about her at her <strong><a title="AccidentalComic.com" href="Accidental%20Comic.com" target="_blank">website. </a></strong>Leigh Annes new book,</em><em> &#8220;<strong>Confessions of a Semi-Natural Woman</strong>&#8221; </em>(a collection of 99 of her funniest humor columns from the past tenish years  including the one that won the Erma Bombeck Humor Writing Competition  is now available at <a href="http://www.accidentalcomic.com/">www.accidentalcomic.com</a>) She also has a new<a title="Acidental Comic" href="http://accidentalcomic.wordpress.com/" target="_blank"> blog</a>. Of if she says, <strong>&#8220;<em>If you go there and like what you see, you can subscribe by clicking the subscribe button (who knew?)&#8221;</em></strong></p></blockquote>
