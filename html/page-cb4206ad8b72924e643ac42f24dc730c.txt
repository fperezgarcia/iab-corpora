<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://usclassiccars.blogspot.com/2006/07/alabama.html'>ALABAMA</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115396025257409443' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<a href="http://www.barbermuseum.org/">Barber Vintage Motorsports Museum</a><br />
<i>6030 Barber Motorsports Parkway</i><br />
<i>Birmingham AL 35094</i><br />
<i>(205) 699-7275</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> More than 900 vintage and modern motorcycles -- believed to be the largest such collection in North america -- as well as a collection of Lotus and other race cars. Approximately 500 motorcycles on display at any given time. Bikes range from 1904 to current-year production, from 16 countries that represent 143 marques.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://carnutmuseum.blogspot.com/">Car Nut Museum </a><br />
<i>161 Bankhead Highway<br />
Winfield, AL 35594<br />
(205) 329-1505&nbsp;</i><br />
<b>Features:&nbsp;</b> Contains 16 antique cars, a selection of antique motorcycles and bicycles and what purportedly is the largest pinball machine in the world.<br />
<b></b><br />
<i> </i><br />
<a href="http://bp2.blogger.com/_idlYwkc9AhE/Ry0FUDqL-pI/AAAAAAAACVA/Ayr8Ky0tU_k/s1600-h/1919IndyCar.jpg" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5128761392782637714" src="http://bp2.blogger.com/_idlYwkc9AhE/Ry0FUDqL-pI/AAAAAAAACVA/Ayr8Ky0tU_k/s200/1919IndyCar.jpg" style="float: left; margin: 0pt 10px 10px 0pt;" /></a><br />
<a href="http://www.motorsportshalloffame.com/">International Motorsports Hall of Fame</a><br />
<i>Speedway Boulevard, adjacent Talladega Superspeedway</i><br />
<i>Talladega AL</i><br />
<i>(256) 362-5002</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> The IMHOF's Motorsports Museum spans three buildings and an enclosed courtyard. Its collection of racing vehicles and  memorabilia from 1902 to the present is valued at more than $15 million. Among the items on display are the fastest boat in the world, the rare Ford Talladega Torino that Alabama native Donnie Allison raced in the Winston Cup series, the STP Dodge Charger that Richard Petty drove to a career-record 31 victories, and the Budweiser Rocket car that was the first to break the sound barrier on land.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.mbusi.com/pages/vc_home.asp">Mercedes-Benz U.S. International</a><br />
<i>Visitors Center</i><br />
<i>1 Mercedes Drive</i><br />
<i>Tuscaloosa AL 35490</i><br />
<i>(888) 286-8762</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> Tours will resume early next year, when visitors will be able to take a virtual tour of the manufacturing process, and see vintage Mercedes, Benz and Daimler vehicles in Daimler's only U.S. automobile manufacturing plant.<br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://usclassiccars.blogspot.com/2006/07/alaska.html'>ALASKA</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422380370778004' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-HGe6mQM1nUs/TbRqoKWzaVI/AAAAAAAAJso/XfW6OchgENM/s1600/Picture+2.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" height="118" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-HGe6mQM1nUs/TbRqoKWzaVI/AAAAAAAAJso/XfW6OchgENM/s200/Picture+2.png" width="200" /></a></div>
<a href="http://fountainheadauto.blogspot.com/2010/11/vintage-fashion-tour.html">Fountainhead Antique Auto Museum</a><br />
<i>Wedgewood Resort</i><br />
<i>212 Wedgewood Drive<br />
Fairbanks, AK 99701<br />
(907) 450-2100 </i>&#8206; <br />
<b>Features: </b>Fountainhead calls itself "The farthest north automobile museum in America." Tough to argue with that claim. It features 70+ historically significant American cars and emphasizes the heritage of the automobile during  Alaska&#8217;s post-Gold Rush era. The collection includes such rare vehicles as a  1899 Hertel, 1904 Stevens-Duryea, 1917 Owen-Magnetic, 1921 Heine-Velox,  1933 Auburn V-12 dual ratio custom speedster and 1938 Elto Midget. Several of the autos are the only examples of their marques known to  remain, including an 1898 Hay Motor Carriage, 1906 Compound, and 1920  Argonne.Also, huge photos of Alaskan motoring history.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.museumofalaska.org/">Museum of Alaska Transportation &amp; Industry</a><br />
<i>3800 West Museum Drive</i><br />
<i>Wasilla, Alaska 99687</i><br />
<i>(907) 376-1211</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The museum's mission is the collection, conservation, restoration, exhibition, and interpretation of artifacts relating to Alaska&#8217;s transportation and industrial history. Its scope is statewide and the collections reflect that, encompassing Eskimo skin boats to jet aircraft.<br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://usclassiccars.blogspot.com/2006/07/arizona.html'>ARIZONA</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115421928057561005' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<a href="http://www.buddystubbshd.com/custompage3.asp?pg=museum01">Buddy Stubbs Motorcycle Museum</a><br />
<i>13850 North Cave Creek Road<br />
Phoenix, AZ 85022<br />
(602) 971-3400</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This collection is on the grounds of a sprawling Harley-Davidson dealership. More than 100 rare machines are on display, representing several dozen manufacturers.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/Enter1.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/Enter1.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.franklincar.org/body.htm">Franklin Automobile Museum</a><br />
<i>1405 East Kleindale Road</i><br />
<i>PO Box 40444</i><br />
<i>Tucson, AZ 85717-0444</i><br />
<i>(520) 326-8038</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This facility springs from the donation of his adobe home and his car collection by the estate of Thomas H. Hubbard, who died in 1993 at the age of 67. He was a well-known Tucson auto restorer and car history buff widely recognized as a leading authority on the Franklin automobile. His collection, besides 16 Franklins, includes a 1909 REO, a 1939 Lincoln Zephyr and a 1957 Porsche coupe. A '33 Franklin Coupe is shown here next to the museum/home's entrance.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.gammonsgulch.com/">Gammons Gulch Movie-Set Museum</a><br />
<i>331 West Rockspring Lane</i><br />
<i>Benzon, AZ 85602</i><br />
<i>(520) 212-2831</i><br />
<b>Features: </b>On this site where numerous movies, commercials and TV scenes have been shot, the Old Western Town portion is a mix of the 1880s to 1930s containing a collection of antique and vintage cars along with movie memorabilia.&nbsp; It also is a frequent site for collector car club events.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.hallofflame.org/">Hall of Flame</a><br />
<i>6101 East Van Buren St.</i><br />
<i>Phoenix, AZ 85008</i><br />
<i>(602) 275-3473</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> Nearly an acre of fire history exhibits, with more than 90 restored pieces of fire apparatus dating from 1725. Mostly American items, but there are examples from Europe and Japan. Sponsored by the National Historical Fire Foundation.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/penske.0.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/penske.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.penskeracingmuseum.com/">Penske Racing Museum</a><br />
<i>7125 East Chauncey Lane</i><br />
<i>Phoenix, AZ 85054</i><br />
<i>(480) 538-4444</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Celebrates four decades of the famous racing enterprise with an 8,000 square foot facility that includes two floors plus a mezzanine with a collapsible glass wall. Eleven of Penske Racing's winning Indy 500 cars are on display, along with 18 other racing and vintage cars, Team Penske trophies and other memorabilia.<br />

</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422398526687261' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<a href="http://www.fstm.org/">Ft. Smith Trolley Museum</a><br />
<i>100 South 4th Street</i><br />
<i>Fort Smith, AR 72901</i><br />
<i>(479) 783-0205</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The Fort Smith Streetcar Restoration Association is dedicated to the preservation and appreciation of electric powered streetcars, railroad equipment, transportation and other technology that existed during this period in history, and to providing a unique educational experience to the visiting public.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/14creatorspopcorn.0.gif" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/14creatorspopcorn.png" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.museumofautos.com/">Museum of Automobiles</a><br />
<i>Petit Jean Mountain </i><br />
<i>8 Jones Lane </i><br />
<i>Morrilton, AK 72110 </i><br />
<i>(501) 727-5427</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Its collection includes some very unusual vehicles such as a 1904 Oldsmobile French Front, a 1912 Paige Beverly touring car, a 1916 Smith Motor Wheel, a 1933 Auburn V12 Boat Tail Speedster and a 1914 Cretors Popcorn Wagon commercial truck seen here. The museum also serves as headquarters for the <a href="http://www.motaa.com/">Mid-America Old-Time Auto Association</a>.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.texarkaam.org/">Four States Auto Museum</a><br />
<i>217 Laurel Street </i><br />
<i>Texarkana, AR 71854</i><br />
<i>(870) 772-2886</i><br />
<b>Features: </b>This facility, located on a site that once was a gathering place for horse-drawn wagons driven by shoppers, is devoted to renovating and preserving all manner of vehicles and paraphernalia.<br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://usclassiccars.blogspot.com/2006/07/california.html'>CALIFORNIA</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422012269784785' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-mjXEJpvQzYs/TbIZQqGKvsI/AAAAAAAAJqI/AeVLO5MfBCU/s1600/Picture%2B8.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" height="150" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-mjXEJpvQzYs/TbIZQqGKvsI/AAAAAAAAJqI/AeVLO5MfBCU/s200/Picture%2B8.png" width="200" /></a></div>
<a href="http://www.alfordmuseum.com/">Alford Auto Museum</a><br />
<i>599 East Main Street</i><br />
<i>El Centro, CA</i><br />
<i>(760) 353-3920</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Predominately American-Made cars such as Fords and Franklins. The Model &#8220;A&#8221; Ford Rich Alford bought when he was in high school is included in the exhibit outside. All cars are in running condition and have been restored to their original state.  <br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.agsem.com/index.html">Antique Gas &amp; Steam Engine Museum</a><br />
<i>2040 North Santa Fe Avenue</i><br />
<i>Vista, CA</i><br />
<i>760) 941-1791</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Dozens of workhorse machines powered by, obviously, gasoline or steam engines. A visible history of American manufacturing devices.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.automobiledrivingmuseum.org/">Automobile Driving Museum</a><br />
<i>610 Lairport Street</i><br />
<i>El Segundo, CA</i><br />
<i>(310) 909-0950</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> More than 70 antique, classic and special interest cars are on display, including a 1955 Packard Caribbean once owned by the actress Jean Peters (a gift from Howard Hughes) and a 1936 seven passenger Packard Phantom, <br />
purported to be a gift from President Roosevelt to Joseph Stalin. Rides in select vehicle4s are given on Sundays. <br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.blackhawkauto.org/">Blackhawk Museum</a><br />
<i>3700 Blackhawk Plaza Circle</i><br />
<i>Danville, CA 94506 </i><br />
<i>(925) 736-2280</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> Two spacious galleries display about 90 historically significant and artistically inspired automobiles, mostly one-of-a-kind, dating from the turn of the 20th Century. <br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.answers.com/topic/chandler-vintage-museum-of-transportation-and-wildlife">Chandler Vintage Museum of Transportation &amp; Wildlife</a><br />
<i>Oxnard, CA</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This was home to the late newspaper mangate Otis Chandler's extensive collection of vintage and rare automobiles, motorcycles and trains as well as fine art and wildlife. The inventory was sold off in 2008. See an update <a href="http://www.answers.com/topic/chandler-vintage-museum-of-transportation-and-wildlife#The_collection">here</a>.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://emporium.turnpike.net/%7Ewalk/mg-world/Museums/cooley.htm">J.A. Cooley Museum</a><br />
<i>North Park</i><br />
<i>San Diego, CA</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This is a small facility in one of the city's older neighborhoods. It has 15 cars from 1886 through 1933 plus 25 categories of antiques represented by collections such as model trains, cast iron toys, spittoons, tools, cuckoo clocks, license plates, World War I posters, phonographs, typewriters, and cameras.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.truckmuseum.org/">Hays Antique Truck Museum</a><br />
<i>1962 Hays Lane</i><br />
<i>Woodland, CA</i><br />
<i>(530) 666-1044</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The collection includes more than 100 makes of old trucks, representing 94 different manufacturers such as Fageol, Freightliner, Mack, Sterling, Oshkosh, Peterbilt, Chevrolet, Dodge, and the one and only 1916 Breeding Steam Truck.     <br />
<br />
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/Ferrari%20FX.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/320/Ferrari%20FX.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.marconimuseum.org/">Marconi Automotive Museum</a><br />
<i>1302 Industrial Drive</i><br />
<i>Tustin, CA 92780</i><br />
<i>(714) 258-3001</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> A $30 million collection includes exotic cars, auto memorabilia, and convention and event space. Included are cars, motorcycles and such special groupings as "Ferrari Row," a line of Ferraris that includes a sapphire-blue Ferrari FX (seen here),  the only one in the world. Many of the event proceeds help fund the Marconi Foundation for Kids.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.murphyautomuseum.org/">Murphy Auto Museum</a><br />
<i>2230 Statham Boulevard</i><br />
<i>Oxnard, CA 93033</i><br />
<i>(805) 487-4333</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This facility was opened in 2002 as a non-profit collection of more than 50 cars in the classic, milestone and special interest niches, including a collection of Packards from 1927-1958.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://museum.nhra.com/">NHRA Motorsports Museum</a><br />
<i>Fairplex Gate 1</i><br />
<i>1101 West McKinley Avenue</i><br />
<i>Pomona, CA</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The Wally Parks NHRA Motorsports Museum opened in 1998. The 28,500 square foot building on the edge of the Los Angeles County Fairplex is intended to highlight the impact of motorsports on our culture. Hot rods, customs and race cars are featured.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/nethercutt.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/nethercutt.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.classics.com/nthct.html">Nethercutt Collection and Museum</a><br />
<i>15151 Bledsoe Street</i><br />
<i>Sylmar, CA 91342</i><br />
<i>(818) 367-2251</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This is an upscale facility with a dress code. No jeans or shorts, for example. Besides classic cars, the collection has more than 1,100 hood ornaments, many crafted from Lalique crystal. Among its 200 showpiece cars are a 1933 Duesenberg SJ Arlington Torpedo "Twenty Grand." a 1932 Maybach Zeppelin Sport Cabriolet and a 1934 Packard 1108 Dietrich Convertible Sedan, the "Orello." <br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.petersen.org/">Petersen Automotive Museum</a><br />
<i>6060 Wilshire Boulevard</i><br />
<i>Los Angeles, CA 90036</i><br />
<i>(323) 930-2277</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The museum covers four stories. Permanent exhibits, including dioramas and experience settings, are on the first. The second is comprised of five large changing exhibition galleries with displays of race cars, classic cars, vintage motorcycles, concept cars, celebrity and movie cars, and automotive design and technology. The May Family Children's Discovery Center is located on the third floor. It's an interactive hands-on learning center that teaches children basic scientific principles by explaining the fundamental functions of a car. An all-glass penthouse conference center, founder's lounge and kitchen cover the fourth floor. Changing exhibits cover such topics as "Presidents, Popes &amp; Potentates." <br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.sdautomuseum.org/">San Diego Automotive Museum</a><br />
<i>Balboa Park</i><br />
<i>2080 Pan American Plaza</i><br />
<i>San Diego, CA  92101-1636</i><br />
<i>(619) 231-2886</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features: </span>An exotic and extensive collection that ranges from  the first Fiberglas dune buggy to the Nash Metropolitan -- America's smallest-ever production car, three-wheelers, desert racing vehicles and motorcycles.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://thesdfirehousemuseum.org/">San Diego Firehouse Museum</a><br />
<i>1572 Columbia Street </i><br />
<i>San Diego, CA</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The museum, located in the city's Little Italy section, occupies the former home of Fire Station No. 6. It has a wide range firefighting gear, including such exhibits as an 1841 Rumsey &amp; Co. piano box hand pumper that was retired during the Civil War, and a 1928 Seagrave pumper, equipped with front-fender holes for "blackout lights" used on the streets of La Jolla, CA, during WWII. <br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.simpsonsnursery.com/">Simpson's Nursery &amp; Auto Museum</a><br />
<i>13925 Highway 94</i><br />
<i>Jamul, CA</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This sprawling 25-acre plant nursery and garden center is located 20 minutes from downtown San Diego. In addition to being a horticultural center, it offers two barns full of vintage and antique cars for visitors to visit for free. One barn contains Ford Model T's and Model A's, and the other hot rods, classics and muscle cars from the 1940's, '50s, '60s and early '70s. <br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.toweautomuseum.org/index.html">Towe Auto Museum</a><br />
<i>2200 Front Street </i><br />
<i>Sacramento, CA 95818-1107</i><br />
<i>(916) 442-6802 </i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This museum stems from local collector Edward Towe's passion for collecting Fords. At one time, he had amassed 240 if them, which became the core of the facility. By the mid-1980s, his array was displayed in two separate museums, the Towe Ford Museum in Deer Lodge, MT, and the Towe Ford Museum in Sacramento. Local enthusiasts drummed up municipal and industry funding for a new, consolidated facility which opened in 1987.<br />

<a href='http://usclassiccars.blogspot.com/2006/07/colorado.html'>COLORADO</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422410101190585' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/x/blogger/6012/1261/1600/963517/stutz.jpg"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/x/blogger/6012/1261/320/26309/stutz.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.cusslermuseum.com/">Clive Cussler Museum</a><br />
<i>14959 W. 69th Avenue</i><br />
<i>Arvada CO 80007</i><br />
<i>(303) 420-2795</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Cussler, author of multiple best-selling novels, frequently includes exotic cars and other transportation in his books. This seasonal museum is a collection of cars he owns, some of which have been described in his books. Among the prizes: a 1932 Stutz town car (seen here), a 1953 Allard J2X sports car, and a 1936 Pierce Arrow Berline with a Pierce Arrow  Travelodge house trailer.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.co.boulder.co.us/openspace/dougherty">The Dougherty Museum</a><br />
<i>U.S. 287</i><br />
<i>Longmont, CO</i><br />
<i>(303) 776-2520</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The collection includes restored cars powered by steam, electricity and early internal combustion engines. Most of the cars are in running order. Some unusual cars include a 1908 Silent Waverly (seen here), a 1909 Fuller, a 1910 Lozier, several Ford Model A's and several horse-drawn carriages. <br />
<br />
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/Renault1912operacoupe.3.jpg"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/Renault1912operacoupe.2.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.forneymuseum.com/">Forney Museum of Transportation</a><br />
<i>4303 Brighton Boulevard</i><br />
<i>Denver, CO 80216</i><br />
<i>(303) 297-1113</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Amelia Earhart's "Gold Bug" Kissel, Aly Khan's Rolls Royce Silver Ghost, plus "Anything On Wheels," such as the Forney Locomotive, the Big Boy and other train engines, as well as dining cars, cable cars and more as part of a 500-item collection of antique cars -- such as the 1912 Renault opera coupe seen here, trains, tractors, carriages, cars, buggies and bicycles. The exhibits also include wax figures of the likes of W.C. <br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.shelbyamericancollection.org/index.html">Shelby American Collection</a><br />
<i>5020 Chaparral Court</i><br />
<i>P.O. Box 19228</i><br />
<i>Boulder, CO 80308-2228</i><br />
<i>(303) 516-9565</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The collection represents the race heritage of Shelby American and has on display some of the most famous cars it ever built. The museum also is host to displays of period autos, tools and memorabilia.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.stanleymuseum.org/co.html">Stanley Museum</a><br />
<i>Lower Stanley Village</i><br />
<i>P.O. Box 788</i><br />
<i>Estes Park, CO 80517</i><br />
<i>(970) 577-1903</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This is a sister facility to one in <a href="http://www.stanleymuseum.org/maine.html">Kingfield, ME</a>. Its exhibits range from history and ghost story tours to virtual tours in a presentation format. With the addition of the museum's 1909 Model R Stanley Roadster, the museum ramped up the significance of the Stanley steam car, its place in automotive and transportation history and an introduction to how it works.<br />

<a href='http://usclassiccars.blogspot.com/2006/07/connecticut.html'>CONNECTICUT</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422422520119821' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/firehouse.gif" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/firehouse.png" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.ci.manchester.ct.us/fire/CtHxFSc/CtHxFSc2.htm">Connecticut Firemen's Historical Society</a><br />
<i>230 Pine Street</i><br />
<i>Manchester, CT 06040</i><br />
<i>(860) 649-9436</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The museum is located in a 1901 firehouse and exhibits a wide variety of firefighting equipment and memorabilia ranging from leather fire buckets used by the first settlers, to early motorized fire apparatus. Also on display, a horse-drawn hose wagon and a steam-operated pumper.<br />
<br />
<b>Gasoline Alley Automotive Museum</b><br />
<i>58 Buckley Highway</i><br />
<i>Stafford Springs, CT 06076</i><br />
<i>(860) 684-2678</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> (No website.) All sorts of automobilia is on display -- gas pumps, oil cans, signage, etc. -- plus such cars as a 1946 Fordf Sportsman, one of only about 700 manufactured. <i><br />
</i><br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.goldenagetruckmuseum.com/">Golden Age of Trucking Museum</a><br />
<i>1101 Southford Road</i><br />
<i>Middlebury, CT</i><br />
<i>(203) 577-2181       </i><br />
<b>Features:</b> In addition to continually changing exhibits on trucking, the museum has a display of Crosley cars and collectibles, a 35-minute movie about moving the Edaville Railroad from Carver, MA, to Portland, ME, art displays, stock cars from the now-closed Danbury Race Arena, antique trucks ranging from the early 1900s to 1974, plus a trio of cars made in Connecticut -- a Locomobile, a Trumbull and a Barker.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.artcom.com/Museums/nv/mr/06281.htm">Photomobile Model Museum</a><br />
<i>1728 Highway 198</i><br />
<i>Woodstock, CT</i><br />
<i>(203) 974-3910</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Dedicated to solar electric small-scale car, boat, plane, train and maglev vehicles. Highlight: New England solar car.<br />

</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422426219346681' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/stanley.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/stanley.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.steamtraction.com/archive/3425/">Magic Age of Steam</a><br />
<i>Route 82</i><br />
<i>Yorklyn, DE 19736</i><br />
<i>(302) 239-4410</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Steam-powered cars, locomotives and other vehicles are represented in this facility, dedicated to the "Age of Steam" in America from the end of the Civil War until the early 1920s. It includes the largest collection of steam-powered autos in the world, an exhibit of miniature stationary steam engines and tiny railroad locomotives, and the 7" gauge coal-burning Auburn Valley Railroad, all located on the grounds. Seen here is the 30-horsepower 1912 Stanley, a 7-passenger touring car that holds the record for the longest trip ever made in a steam car -- 8,328 miles during the summer of 1972.<br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://usclassiccars.blogspot.com/2006/07/florida.html'>FLORIDA</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422431783342091' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<a href="http://www.elliottmuseumfl.org/">Elliott Museum</a><br />
<i>825 N.E. Ocean Boulevard</i><br />
<i>Hutchinson Island</i><br />
<i>Stuart, FL</i><br />
<i>(772) 225-1961</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Founded by inventor Harmon Elliott as a tribute to his father, Sterling, also a prolific inventor. Collectively, they were awarded 222 patents. Many of them had to do with vehicle design, an dthe collection includes Sterling&#8217;s quadricycle and the Elliott family&#8217;s Stanley Steamer car. Plus, the facility holds a transportation gallery and life-size dioramas of turn-of-the-century shops and residences. The museum is owned and operated by the Historical Society of Martin County.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.antiquecarmuseum.org/">Ft. Lauderdale Antique Car Museum</a><br />
<i>1527 SW 1st Avenue (Packard Avenue)</i><br />
<i>Ft. Lauderdale, FL 33315 </i><br />
<i>(954)779-7300</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The museum is a reproduction of a Packard showroom from the 1920s. On display are 22 Packards from the 1900s to the 1940s, all in working order. The 18,000 square foot building is also stocked with thousands of pieces of memorabilia as well as a gallery dedicated to President Franklin D. Roosevelt.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.garlits.com/">Don Garlits Museum of Drag Racing</a><br />
<i>13700 SW 16th Avenue</i><br />
<i>Ocala, FL 34473</i><br />
<i>(877) 271-3278</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> This is drag racing's Mecca, devoted to the legendary namesake dragster driver and the sport as a whole.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/familyxmascard.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/familyxmascard.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.sarasotacarmuseum.org/">Sarasota Classic Car Museum</a><br />
<i>5500 North Tamiami Trail</i><br />
<i>Sarasota, FL 34243</i><br />
<i>(941) 355-6228</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> This 53-year-old facility recently underwent a renovation. The 60,000 square foot facility sits on four acres near the  Ringling Museum of Art. It houses more than 100 automobiles, including classic models, one of only five Cadillac station wagons ever made, John Lennon&#8217;s Mercedes Roadster, and one of Don Garlits' dragsters. Also on premises is an antique game arcade with machines that are operable and cost just a few cents.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.tacm.com/">Tallahassee Antique Car Museum</a><br />
<i>3550A Mahan Drive</i><br />
<i>Tallahassee, FL 32308</i><br />
<i>(850) 942-0137</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> A large collection of classics from around the world. Perhaps the most prized possession is an 1894 Duryea, perhaps the oldest known surviving fully manufactured pre-production model. It is in running condition. A forensic examination of the underside of the original floorboard reveals that the pencil writing of the words &#8220;flywheel&#8221; and &#8220;Sprocket&#8221; were by J. Frank Duryea, the original designer of the Duryea automobile.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.tbauto.org/">Tampa Bay Auto Museum</a><br />
<i>3301 Gateway Centre Boulevard</i><br />
<i>Pinellas Park, FL</i><br />
<i>(727) 579-8226</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> If you want to see some very unusual marques, models and memorabilia, try this facility. Examples? How about Peugeot Darl 'mat, Salmson S4E, Talbot Lago T 15 Q L 6, Avion Voisin-C7 Chastness, Tracta A, Hotchkiss Gregoire, Allard P1, Fardier de Cugnot, Kubelwagen Type 82 ... . Convinced?<br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://usclassiccars.blogspot.com/2006/07/georgia.html'>GEORGIA</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422435890867160' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-R3Z-UA_RM4E/TbIdV9x0u5I/AAAAAAAAJqQ/nCUu0jXJbMA/s1600/Picture+9.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" height="120" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-R3Z-UA_RM4E/TbIdV9x0u5I/AAAAAAAAJqQ/nCUu0jXJbMA/s200/Picture+9.png" width="200" /></a></div>
<a href="http://www.thunderroadusa.com/Museum.html">Georgia Racing Hall of Fame Museum</a><br />
<i>415 Highway 53 East<br />
Dawsonville, GA 30534 </i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The museum recognizes prominent members of the state's racing heritage. It includes the Elliott Family Legacy Room and a display of vintage and NASCAR vehicles.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.stonemountainpark.com/">Stone Mountain Antique Car and Treasure Museum</a><br />
<i>Stone Mountain Memorial Park</i><br />
<i>2542 Young Road</i><br />
<i>Stone Mountain, GA 30088 </i><br />
<i>(404) 981-0194</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Among 40 display cars are a Tucker, a 35K series Lincoln and a handmade car from a Buck Rogers sci-fi film, the body of which is a shortened '37 Ford.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/micro.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/micro.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.microcarmuseum.com/">Bruce Weiner Microcar Museum</a><br />
<i>2950 Eatonton Road</i><br />
<i>Madison, GA 30650</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Open by <a href="mailto:2006@microcarmuseum.com">e-mail appointment</a> only. This an unusual collection, devoted to tiny autos spurred by post-World War II desires to get Europe back on its feet. The collection is primarily focused on cars of the late 1940s-pre-1964 range with engine sizes of 700cc or less (many are 250cc and 50cc) and two doors or less. The microcar or "bubble car" came to symbolize this period of renewed energy and pulling together.<br />

<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-VsTtMoyOids/TbId-uHvtTI/AAAAAAAAJqU/JRGZi50uoZE/s1600/Picture%2B10.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" height="159" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-VsTtMoyOids/TbId-uHvtTI/AAAAAAAAJqU/JRGZi50uoZE/s200/Picture%2B10.png" width="200" /></a></div>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://usclassiccars.blogspot.com/2006/07/indiana.html'>INDIANA</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422463677279638' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<a href="http://www.acdmuseum.org/">Auburn Cord Duesenberg Museum</a><br />
<i>1600 South Wayne Street</i><br />
<i>Auburn, IN 46706</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Some of America's most artistically classic vehicles are housed in an Art Deco gallery that was a world-famous 1920s-'30s showroom. Besides the marques in the establishment's name there are examples of Packards, Cadillacs, Rolls Royces and other high-end makes.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.automotiveheritagemuseum.org/">Automotive Heritage Museum</a><br />
<i>1500 North Reed Road (US 31 North)</i><br />
<i>Kokomo, IN</i><br />
<i>(765) 454-9999</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> More than 100 antique and classic vehicles built in the period 1895 to the mid 1970s, including Packards, Hupmobiles and the Haynes autros built in Kokomo.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/--6GaQ5MoEYo/TbIi4E5tyPI/AAAAAAAAJqg/NHm3E77GlXU/s1600/Picture+13.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" height="108" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/--6GaQ5MoEYo/TbIi4E5tyPI/AAAAAAAAJqg/NHm3E77GlXU/s200/Picture+13.png" width="200" /></a></div>
<a href="http://www.brickyard.com/museum">Indianapolis Motor Speedway Hall of Fame Museum</a><br />
<i>4790 West 16th Street</i><br />
<i>Indianapolis IN 46224</i><br />
<i>(317) 492-6784 </i><br />
<b>Features:</b> About 75 cars are on display at any one time in the 30,000 square feet of exhibition space. Highlights: <br />
The Marmon Wasp that won the inaugural Indianapolis 500 in 1911 with Ray Harroun at the wheel, and was featured on a postage stamp in the U.S. Postal Service's Transportation Series; four two-time winning cars, the Boyle Maserati (Wilbur Shaw 1939-40), the Blue Crown Spark Plug Special (Mauri Rose 1947-48), the Fuel Injection Special (Bill Vukovich 1953-54) and the Belond Special (Sam Hanks 1957 and Jimmy Bryan 1958), plus more than 30 Indy 500-winning cars.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.automotiveheritagemuseum.org/">Kokomo Auto Heritage Museum</a><br />
<i>The Johanning Civic Center</i><br />
<i>1500 North Reed Road, US 31N</i><br />
<i>Kokomo, IN 46901 </i><br />
<i>(765) 454-9999 </i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The city is the home of the Haynes auto, first built in 1893 by inventor Elwood Haynes. It is regarded as America's first gasoline-powered car. The car, named the Pioneer was successfully road tested on July 4, 1894. The Pioneer is now housed in the Smithsonian Institute in Washington, D.C. This museum displays more than 100 antique and classic autos representing dozens of makes and models. Included are displays of automobile components and accessories manufactured in Kokomo since 1894.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.kccmuseum.org/">Kruse Automotive &amp; Carriage Museum</a><br />
<i>5634 CR 11-A</i><br />
<i>Auburn, IN 46706</i><br />
<i>(260) 927-9144</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The classics on display range from a Duesenberg to custom hot rods built by Carl Casper, winner of more than 100 car show and competition awards. Also, several novelty cars such as one of the Batmobiles of TV and movie fame.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.laportecountyhistory.org/">LaPorte County Historical Museum</a><br />
<i>2405 Indiana Avenue, Suite 1</i><br />
<i>La Porte, IN 46350</i><br />
<i>(219) 324-6767</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Several dozen vintage and classic autos that were part of the now-closed Door Prairie Auto Museum. Located in an expanded facility that opened in August 2006.  <br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.natmus.org/">National Automotive and Truck Museum of the U.S.</a><br />
<i>1000 Gordon M. Buehrig Place</i><br />
<i>Auburn, IN 46706</i><br />
<i>(260) 925-9100</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This is a non-profit museum with emphasis on post-World War II cars and trucks, although it does have vehicles on display from 1908 high wheelers to current production pickups. The facility also houses the NATMUS Model Car/Truck &amp; Toy Museum.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.ragtopsmuseum.com/">Rag Tops Museum</a><br />
<i>209 West Highway 12</i><br />
<i>Michigan City, IN 46360</i><br />
<i>(219) 878-1514 </i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This tribute to the convertible covers 65 cars displayed as a &#8220;tour through time,&#8221; with vehicles being shown amid mannequins and memorabilia. Primarily cars from the 1950s and '60s, although it sports a '70 Plymouth Superbird.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-itBkyWtSMYU/TbIjJ6-yUtI/AAAAAAAAJqk/KishYl5ufmQ/s1600/Picture+14.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-itBkyWtSMYU/TbIjJ6-yUtI/AAAAAAAAJqk/KishYl5ufmQ/s1600/Picture+14.png" /></a></div>
<a href="http://www.studebakermuseum.org/">Studebaker National Museum</a><br />
<i>201 South Chapin Street</i><br />
<i>South Bend, IN 46601</i><br />
<i>(574) 235-9714</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This actually is two museums in one -- the Studebaker National Museum and the Center for History -- available for the price of one ticket. The Bullet Nose Gallery houses a Raymond Loewy-designed 1950 Champion convertible, distinctive for its front-end &#8220;Bullet Nose&#8221; treatment that was new for 1950. Hydraulic lifts in the lower level hold vehicle storage in double-decker fashion that  makes all parts of the vehicles observable. The museum also is home to the largest presidential carriage collection known to exist, with carriages belonging to Presidents Lincoln, McKinley, Harrison and Grant, the latter seen here.<br />

<a href="http://www.iowa80truckstop.com/halloffame.htm">Iowa 80 Truckstop Trucking Hall of Fame</a><br />
<i>755 West Iowa 80 Road</i><br />
<i>I-80, Exit 284</i><br />
<i>Walcott, IA</i><br />
<i>(563) 284-6961</i><br />

<i>(800) 874-4488</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Between <a href="http://www.knoxvilleraceway.com/">Knoxville Raceway</a> and the Hall of Fame &amp; Museum, this town claims to be the capital of sprint car racing in America. Numerous events are held at the Hall, which has displays recording sprint car history.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.nod-valley.k12.ia.us/community/automuseum/index.html">Schildberg's Antique Car Museum</a><br />
<i>East Iowa Street</i><br />
<i>Greenfield, IA</i><br />
<i>(641) 743-8444</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This small but varied collection is from the estate of Dennis Schildberg, a local auto collector and restorer.. The cars are used for community events, and the exhibit is open by appointment by calling the local Chamber of Commerce at the number listed here.<br />

<b>Features:</b> (No Web site.) Displays 35 vehicles representing 10 different marques, ranging from a 1914 Ford touring car to a 1937 Cord, with most of the autos from the 1920s.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/millionthvette.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/320/millionthvette.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.corvettemuseum.com/">National Corvette Museum</a><br />
<i>350 Corvette Drive</i><br />
<i>Bowling Green, KY 42101</i><br />
<i>(270) 745-8019</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> Every Corvette is made in the plant here, which is open for tours, and the museum is adjacent to it. The 68,000 sq. ft. building houses more than 50 Corvette models and one-of-a-kind concept cars spanning the history of Corvette since its inception in 1953. Also includes thousands of related photos, movies, videos, advertisements, scale models and rare memorabilia. Inside includes full-scale dioramas showing Corvettes in period settings. The one-millionth 'Vette ever built (seen here) also is on display.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.prestonsburgky.org/ranier_racing_museum.html">Ranier Racing Museum</a><br />
<i>Prestonburg, KY</i><br />
<i>(606) 886-1341</i><br />

<br />
<a href="http://www.swopemuseum.com/">Swope's Cars of Yesteryear Museum</a><br />
<i>1100 North Dixie Avenue </i><br />
<i>Elizabethtown, KY</i><br />
<i>(270) 765-2181</i><br />

<i>601 Spring Street</i><br />
<i>Shreveport, LA 71101-3645 </i><br />

<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-NFSe9PgLgT4/TbIlwcDaXgI/AAAAAAAAJqo/UrygRdpk-hk/s1600/Picture+15.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><br />

<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-xl4knz3zP-M/TbIm_MR54lI/AAAAAAAAJqs/nbdbsUOmHrA/s1600/Picture+15.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-xl4knz3zP-M/TbIm_MR54lI/AAAAAAAAJqs/nbdbsUOmHrA/s1600/Picture+15.png" /></a><br />

The museum has the bullet-riddled death car, a collection of photos, one of Clyde's Remington  shotguns pulled from the car, some items of clothing, replicas of Bonnie and Clyde's tombstones set in a simulated  graveyard, a large mural showing the death scene, and other icky stuff.<br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://usclassiccars.blogspot.com/2006/07/maine.html'>MAINE</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422508916091872' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/packardpapforrest.jpg"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/packardpapforrest.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.railwayvillage.org/">Boothbay Railway Village</a><br />
<i>Route 27, P.O. Box 123</i><br />
<i>Boothbay, ME 04537</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> What began as a village restoration has morphed into an eclectic collection of transportation memorabilia and village history. The car collection includes such attractions as a circa 1830 one-horse shay, an 1860 carriage built in Farmington, ME, an  1897 Haynes-Apperson Surrey, a 1904 curved dahboard Oldsmobile, a 1923 Ford Model T depot hack, a  1926 Franklin Victoria sport touring car, a  1929 Packard Model 640 limousine, a  1939 Lincoln Zephyr 12 cylinder,  a 1941 Willys Americar Speedway Sedan and a 1962 Rolls Royce.  <br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.colemuseum.org/">Cole Land Transportation Museum</a><br />
<i>405 Perry Road</i><br />
<i>Bangor ME 04401</i><br />
<i>(207) 990-3600</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This is a repository of vehicles and memorabilia that chronicle all areas of transportation in Maine's history. That includes a collection of more than  2,000 photographs, the largest display of snow removal equipment under one roof in America, and 10 fire trucks ranging from a 1910 hand tub to a 1948 Ahrens Fox. <br />
<br />
<b>Jay Hill Auto Museum</b><br />
<i>Route 4,  Jay Hill </i><br />
<i>Jay , ME 04239</i><br />
<i>(207) 897-5257</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> (No Web site.) Small exhibit of antique vehicles.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.ohtm.org/">Owls Head Transportation Museum</a><br />
<i>Route 73 adjacent Knox County Airport</i><br />
<i>Owls Head, ME</i><br />
<i>(207) 594-4418</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The facility deals with more than 100 historic aircraft, automobiles, bicycles, carriages and engines on permanent display. The aircraft collection contains replicas and originals representing the first century of flight, from Cayley&#8217;s unmanned glider (1804) to the legendary Curtiss Jenny of the barnstorming era. The auto collection spans the late 19th and early 20th centuries, and includes the 1963 Prototype Mustang and a 1935 Stout Scarab, called the world&#8217;s first mini-van. Only six were made.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/1907chadwick.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" height="156" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/320/1907chadwick.jpg" style="float: right; margin: 0pt 0pt 10px 10px;" width="200" /></a><a href="http://www.sealcoveautomuseum.org/">Seal Cove Auto Museum</a><br />
<i>PO Box 190</i><br />
<i>Pretty Marsh Road</i><br />
<i>Camden, ME 04843</i><br />
<i>(207) 244-9242</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> One of the largest, if not <span style="font-style: italic;">the</span> largest, collections of brass cars, containing more than 100 rare autos -- such as the 1907 Chadwick seen here -- and about 30 antique motorcycles.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.skylinefarm.org/">Skyline Farm Carriage Museum</a><br />
<i>95 The Lane</i><br />
<i>North Yarmouth, ME 04097</i><br />
<i>(207) -829-9908</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Housed in one of Maine's first indoor riding rings, the horse-drawn carriages and sleighs represent the types of vehicles used for personal tranportation prior to the 1920s.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.stanleymuseum.org/">Stanley Museum</a><br />
<i>40 School Street</i><br />
<i>P.O. Box 77</i><br />
<i>Kingfield, ME04947</i><br />
<i>(207) 265-2729</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This is a sister entity to the Stanley in <a href="http://www.stanleymuseum.org/co.html">Estes Park, CO</a>, established in 1981 and located in the Georgian-style Stanley School built in 1903. Kingfield is the birthplace of the Stanleys &#8212; important to note because the steam car for which they're most often remembered was built in Watertown, MA. The Museum commemorates and preserves the heritage of the Stanley family that created the famous Stanley Steamer automobile. The exhibits cover all facets of Stanley family history and memorabilia &#8212; airbrush painting and photography, violins and examples of Stanley steam cars from 1905, 1910, and 1916.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.wellschamber.org/Museums%20in%20Wells.htm">Wells Auto Museum</a><br />
<i>Route 1</i><br />
<i>Wells, ME</i><br />
<i>(207) 646-9064</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> More than 80 cars covering 1900-1963. Some of the gems are a 1963 Studebaker Avanti, a 1949 Cadillac Fleetwood, and a 1907 Stanley Steamer. Also, a collection of nickelodeons, antique arcade games, license plates and toys.<br />

Several marques long gone from the automotive scene are represented, including a '26 Essex, a '49 Packard, a '51 Willys Jeep station wagon and a '55 Hudson Metropolitan (seen here).<br />

<br />
<a href="http://www.heritagemuseumsandgardens.org/collections_auto.asp">Josiah K. Lilly III Automobile Collection</a><br />
<i>Heritage Museums &amp; Gardens</i><br />
<i>Sandwich, MA</i><br />
<i>(508) 888-3300, ext. 121</i><br />
<b> Features:</b> This collection is housed in a two-story replica of a Shaker Round Barn on the grounds of an extensive campus of history and horticultural attractions on the west end of Cape Cod. Among the vehicles on display: actor Gary Cooper's 1930 Duesenberg, a 1932 Auburn Boattail Speedster model 8-100A, a 1922 Rolls-Royce Silver Ghost Pall Mall Phaeton, a 1912 Mercer Raceabout and a 1909 White Steam Car Model M, one of the first official cars of the White House, owned by President William Howard Taft.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://world.nycsubway.org/us/boston/lowell.html">National Streetcar Museum</a><br />
<i>25 Shattuck Street</i><br />
<i>Lowell, MA</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This is an offshoot of the Seashore Trolley Museum in Kennebunkport, ME. It's an exhibit describing the history of urban rail transportation and how its rebirth is helping to revitalize American cities.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/1965MorganRoadster.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/1965MorganRoadster.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0pt 0pt 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.toadhallcars.com/">Toad Hall</a><br />
<i>Simmons Homestead Inn</i><br />
<i>288 Scudder Avenue</i><br />
<i>Hyannis Port, MA 02647</i><br />
<i>(508) 778-4934</i><br />
<b> Features:</b> A collection of more than 50 classic sports cars, mostly English with some Japanese and European ones thrown in -- and all of them are red. There are a dozen Lotuses, a half-dozen MGs, Triumphs and Jaguars, and a smattering of Austin Healeys, TVRs, Datsuns, and Ferrari, Mazda, Morgan, AC, Daimler, Sunbeam, Mini Cooper, etc.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://64.242.254.49/new_index.asp">Walker Transportation Collection</a><br />
<i>Beverly Historical Society and Museum</i><br />
<i>117 Cabot Street</i><br />
<i>Beverly, MA 01915-5196 </i><br />
<i>( 978) 922-1186</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> As the collection curators put it, "If it moved or existed within, around, or above New England,  the collection most likely has a photo of it." The collection covers trains, streetcars, trolleys, trucks, buses, cars, ships, and planes &#8212; all  forms of transportation.<br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://usclassiccars.blogspot.com/2006/07/michigan.html'>MICHIGAN</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422518617173243' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-36xN0lnLAHk/TbIoyTq47QI/AAAAAAAAJqw/SfGJLPnfgeM/s1600/Picture%2B16.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" height="128" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-36xN0lnLAHk/TbIoyTq47QI/AAAAAAAAJqw/SfGJLPnfgeM/s200/Picture%2B16.png" width="181" /></a></div>
<a href="http://www.automotivehalloffame.org/">Automotive Hall of Fame</a><br />
<i>21400 Oakwood Boulevard</i><br />
<i>Dearborn, MI</i><br />
<i>(313) 240-4000 ext. 228</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This visitor attraction and educational resource is a repository for automotive history shown through visual and interactive exhibits, automobiles and artifacts. School groups are admitted free of charge with advance arrangements.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.ypsiautoheritage.org/">Automotive Heritage Museum</a><br />
<i>100 East Cross Street</i><br />
<i>Ypsilanti, Mi 48198</i><br />
<i>(734) 482-5200</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> Located in the nation's last Hudson dealership, with records dating back to 1927. Apex Motors, which produced the "ACE" car from 1920-1922, was located on South River Street and Preston Tucker whose family owned the Ypsilanti Machine and Tool Co. developed and built the prototype for his "Tucker Torpedo." <br />
<br />
&#8226; <a href="http://www.corvair.org/museum.php">Corvair Museum</a><br />
Part of the Ypsilanti Automotive Heritage Collection.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.thebaycitymotorcompany.com/">Bay City Motor Co. Museum</a><br />
<i>201 Second Street</i><br />
<i>Bay City, MI 48708</i><br />
<i>(866) 822-2216</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Vintage, classic, neoclassic, muscle, and specialty vehicles abound. Among the marques represented are BMW, Buick, Cadillac, Chevrolet, Chrysler, Dodge (2), Ferrari, Ford, Hudson, Jaguar, Lincoln, Mercedes-Benz, Mercury and Studebaker.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.chryslerheritage.com/">Walter P. Chrysler Museum</a><br />
<i>One Chrysler Drive</i><br />
<i>Auburn Hills, MI 48326-2778</i><br />
<i>(888) 456-1924 </i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This facility named for the auto pioneer is located on the DaimlerChrysler Headquarters campus. Although much of the collection deals with Chrysler Corp. marques including Plymouth, DeSoto, Dodge, Imperial and Chrysler, there are others dotted here and there -- such as Willys, Rambler Hudson and more.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.detroithistorical.org/collections/index.asp?ID=8">Detroit Historical Society</a><br />
<i>5401 Woodward Avenue</i><br />
<i>Detroit, MI</i><br />
<i>(313) 833-7935</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This all-things-Detroit organization includes, naturally enough, numerous exhibits and artifacts concerning the auto industry.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/rosaparksbus.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/rosaparksbus.jpg" style="float: right; margin: 0pt 0pt 10px 10px;" /></a></div>
<a href="http://www.henryfordmuseum.com/">Henry Ford Museum</a><br />
<i>20900 Oakwood Boulevard.</i><br />
<i>Dearborn, MI 48124-4088</i><br />
<i>(800) 835-5237</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> "The Henry Ford," as its members call it, prides itself on reflecting major parts of American history with five distinct attractions spread over the 90 acres of Greenfield Village.  It includes such support activities as the Benson Ford Research Center and the Henry Ford IMAX Theater. In addition to explaining the history of Ford's innovative automaking techniques, there are numerous special displays such as the Montgomery, AL, city bus (seen here) on which Rosa Parks refused to sit in the segregated area in 1955, helping touch of the civil rights movement.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.gilmorecarmuseum.org/">Gilmore Car Museum</a><br />
<i>6865 Hickory Road</i><br />
<i>Hickory Corners, MI 49060</i><br />
<i>(269) 671-5089</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> This is not the sort of place you'll see in a half-hour. Nearly 200 vehicles are on display in restored historic barns. Exhibits range from a 1899 Locomobile, to the classic Duesenberg or the rare Tucker '48, and from the Model T to the muscle cars of the 60s.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.tplex.org/1_whatyouwillsee.html">Model T Automotive Heritage Complex</a><br />
<i>411 Piquette Avenue</i><br />
<i>Detroit, MI</i><br />
<i>(313) 872-8759</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The home of the Ford Model T was in this Piquette Avenue plant, now well preserved and largely unchanged from its original 1904 appearance. The third floor has never been painted since Ford Motor Company left in 1910. It houses the secret Experimental Room where Henry Ford developed the innovative Model T. Another exhibit spans five bays, showing the Model T during various stages of assembly. This exhibit explains the stationary assembly process with which cars were produced before the moving assembly line. Various Model Ts and other Piquette-era Fords are on display throughout.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.mshf.com/">Motorsports Hall of Fame of America</a><br />
<i>Novi Expo Center</i><br />
<i>I-96 &amp; Novi Road (exit 162)</i><br />
<i>P.O. Box 194</i><br />
<i>Novi, MI</i><br />
<i>(800) 250-7223</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The hall's board of directors has temporarily suspended public operations at its temporary location in the Expo Center to allow staff and volunteers to concentrate on moving to an appropriate new home. A site selection task force is working on site acquisition and development. Updates will be posted on the Web site.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/1911_olds_limited_w750.jpg"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/1911_olds_limited_w750.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.reoldsmuseum.org/home.htm">R.E. Olds Museum</a><br />
<i>240 Museum Drive</i><br />
<i>Lansing, MI  48933</i><br />
<i>(517) 372-0529</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This facility is named for Ransom E. Olds, founder of REO and, later, Oldsmobile. Displays range from the 1880s through the curved-dash Olds era to today's concept cars. The museum., named one of the nation's 10 best by Car Collector magazine, also houses noth the oldest and the fastest Oldsmobiles, and rare models from REO, Star, Durant, and Viking.  Includes buggies, bicycles, trucks, engines and memorabilia.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.sloanmuseum.com/">Alfred P. Sloan Museum</a><br />
<i>1221 E. Kearsley Street</i><br />
<i>Flint, MI 48503</i><br />
<i>(810) 237-3450</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> The Sloan deals with science, local history and genealogy. But its Buick Gallery deals with classic autos -- more than 25 classic and concept Buicks, Chevrolets, and other locally built automobiles. Featured vehicles on exhibit are the 1910 Buick Bug race car, 1951 Buick XP-300 and 1954 Buick Wildcat II concept cars, and a Hellcat Tank Destroyer built by Buick during World War II.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://willsautomuseum.com/">Wills Ste. Claire Auto Museum</a><br />
<i>2408 Wills Street</i><br />
<i>Marysville, MI</i><br />
<i>(810) 987-2854</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This former WWII munitions factory houses an evolving collection of vintage autos and memorabilia and serves as a center for hobbyists. The collection includes such Wills models as a 1926 seven-passenger sedan and a '26 roadster.<br />

<a href='http://usclassiccars.blogspot.com/2006/07/mississippi.html'>MISSISSIPPI</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422525465286710' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<b>Antique Car Museum</b><br />
<i>Highway 82</i><br />
<i>Carrollton, MS 38917</i><br />
<i>(662) 237-9301</i><br />
<b>Features: </b>(No website) This has many antique cars and antique memorabilia. Some of the cars have been in movies, such as "My Dog Skip."<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.mississippihills.org/index.php?option=com_content&amp;view=article&amp;id=156%3Amike-brown-racing-a-classic-cars-museum&amp;Itemid=96">Mike Brown Racing &amp; Classic Car Museum</a><br />
<i>Routes 306 and 109</i><br />
<i>Iuka, MS 38852</i><br />
<i>(662) 424-3343</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> A display of restored classic antique automobiles. Some unusual items: a 1936  Auburn Boatail Speedster, the '84 Pontiac Richard Petty drove to his 200th NASCAR win, Indianapolis 500 and NASCAR pace cars, and a 1920 Ford Model A.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/bluecar2.jpg"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/bluecar2.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.tupeloautomuseum.com/">Tupelo Automobile Museum</a><br />
<i>Otis Boulevard<br />
Tupelo, MS <br />
(662) 842-4242</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Featured are 120,000 square feet of automobile displays and open viewing restoration bays. More than 100 antique, classic and collectible automobiles are chronologically displayed, beginning with an 1886 Benz and ending with a never-driven 1994 Dodge Viper. Also on view is a Lincoln once owned by Tupelo native Elvis Presley. Elvis was known for giving cars as gifts. The 1976 Lincoln Mark IV on display (seen here) was given to Jerry Kennedy, captain of the Denver Police Vice and Drug Control Bureau, who was in charge of security for Elvis when he appeared in Denver. Elvis purchased the care for $13,386.69 and gave it to the police official.<br />

</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422535567424353' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<b>Archie's Car Museum</b><br />
115 South Garfield Avenue<br />
Terry, MT<br />
<b>Features: </b>(No website) Founded in September 2010 by Doug Braun to house much of his 100-vehicle vintage car collection as well as a large collection of vintage U.S. license plates.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://bp1.blogger.com/_idlYwkc9AhE/RlIc_GNRLcI/AAAAAAAABL8/hijbT_uDMXE/s1600-h/Picture+4.png"><img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5067144401068830146" src="http://bp1.blogger.com/_idlYwkc9AhE/RlIc_GNRLcI/AAAAAAAABL8/hijbT_uDMXE/s320/Picture+4.png" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><br />
<a href="http://www.miracleofamericamuseum.org/">Miracle of America Museum</a><br />
<i>36094 Memory Lane</i><br />
<i>Polson MT </i><br />
<i>(406) 883-6804</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This multi-faceted museum is devoted to all sorts of Montana memorabilia. Among its transportation artifacts are a rare 1943 motorized toboggan, an alcohol-fueled ice racer, a 1930 Model A Ford "Snowbird" (seen here) used for mail delivery and other vintage seasonal vehicles.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.pcmaf.org/auto.htm">Montana Auto Museum</a><br />
<i>Old Prison Museums</i><br />
<i>1106 Main Street</i><br />
<i>Deer Lodge, MT 59722</i><br />
<i>(406) 846-3111</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> More than 150 cars are on display, from classic Chevys and muscle cars to a Schacht high wheeler and a 1903 Ford with tonneau and wicker side baskets. Also, see a DeSoto Airflow, Model T's, V-8 Fords, a Chevy Nomad, a Mustang Mach I,  a Chevelle Super Sport, a Seagrave fire truck, a Diamond T logging truck, an Indian motorcycle, and a Ferrari.<br />
<br />
<b>Oscar's Dreamland Yesteryear Museum</b><br />
4745 Hesper Road<br />
Billings, MT 59106<br />
(406) 656-0966 <br />
<b>Features:</b> (No web site.) Founded by the late Oscar Cooke to house his immense collection of tractor seats (300), antique cars and trucks (30), original frontier buildings (10), construction and military equipment, airplanes and airplane engines, boat motors, hand tools, antique watches, steam engines and tractors, wagons, drilling equipment and much more. The collection now exceeds 10,000 items and requires 152,000 square feet of indoor display space as well as 18 acres outdoors.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://nps.gov/yell">Yellowstone National Park</a><br />
<i>Park Heritage &amp; Research Center</i><br />
<i>Gardiner, MT</i><br />
<i>Yellowstone National Park</i><br />
<i>(307) 344-7381</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> A collection of 30 horse-drawn and motorized vehicles, ranging from stagecoaches operated by the park to the first car &#8212; a Ford Model T &#8212; allowed in the park.<br />

<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422539680916812' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/chevyland.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/chevyland.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.kearneycoc.org/Visiting_Kearney/attractions/Attr_Chevyland.htm">Chevyland USA</a><br />
<i>Exit 257 of I-80</i><br />
<i>Elm Creek, NE</i><br />
<i>(308) 856-4208</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features: </span>More than 100 Chevrolets, all restored or in original condition, are shown in this collection located about 10 minutes west of Kearney.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.roadsideamerica.com/attract/NEMINwarp.html">Harold Warp's Pioneer Village</a><br />
<i>138 East Highway 6</i><br />
<i>Minden, NE</i><br />
<i>(308) 832-1181</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This is a collection of 25 buildings offering everything from a money wrench exhibit to "the world's second oldest Buick," the world's largest collection of restored farm tractors, several hundred autos and other memorabilia.<br />
<br />
<b>Sawyer's Sandhills Museum</b><br />
<i>440 Valentine Street </i><br />
<i>Valentine, NE 69201 </i><br />
<i>(402)376-3293 </i><br />
<b>Features:</b> (No website) Exhibits include a 1907 Cadillac roadster, 1911 Austro-Daimler, 1909 Buick, 1909 Flanders and 1916 Paterson touring car.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.stuhrmuseum.org/tourantique.htm">Stuhr Museum</a><br />
<i>3133 West Highway 34</i><br />
<i>Grand Island, NE 68801</i><br />
<i>(308) 385-5316</i><br />
<b>Features: </b> This complex is called "The Stuhr Museum of the Prairie Pioneer" and covers many aspects of prairie life. The 200-piece Antique Auto and Farm Machinery exhibit includes an 1880 threshing machine, early steam engines, tractors, and numerous examples of horse-drawn and tractor-drawn farm implements, hay rakes, cultivators, plows, potato planters, and more. It also includes 15 antique automobiles, including buggies, a 1909 Model T, a 1913 Overland and a 1903 American.<br />

<a href='http://usclassiccars.blogspot.com/2006/07/nevada.html'>NEVADA</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422548029287021' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/imperial.png" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/imperial.png" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.autocollections.com/">Imperial Auto Collections</a><br />
<i>Imperial Palace</i><br />
<i>Las Vegas, NV</i><br />
<i>(702) 794-3174</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> More than 250 cars are displayed in the 125,000 square foot showroom. Among some of the models on display: Elvis Presley's 1929 Duesenberg J Derham Phaeton, Marilyn Monroe's 1959 Cadillac Fleetwood, and such rarities as a 1933 Pierce Arrow Silver Arrow and a 1961 Aston Martin DB4/GT Zagato valued in excess of $3 million. <br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.riversideresort.com/Html/Museum.asp">Don Laughlin's Classic Car Collection</a><br />
<i>Riverside Resort &amp; Casino</i><br />
<i>500 South Casino Drive</i><br />
<i>Laughlin, NV</i><br />
<i>(702) 298-2622</i><br />
<b>Features: </b>This free-admission collection includes a changing array of rare and unusual vehicles, from limos once owned by notorious historic figures, to movie cars, to the battered motorcycle Steve McQueen rode to get away from it all.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.automuseum.org/">National Auto Museum</a><br />
<i>10 South Lake Street</i><br />
<i>Reno, NV 89501-1558</i><br />
<i>(775) 333-9300</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> This is the famous Harrah Collection, which includes period street settings and clothing to enhance the classic auto displays. In addition to a large number of classic cars, there also are numerous celebrity-owned models (such as James Dean's 1949 Mercury Series 9CM 6-passenger coupe seen here) and vehicles featured in various films.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.fabulousracers.com/aboutus.html">Nevada Vintage Race Car Museum</a><br />
<i>250 SunPac Avenue</i><br />
<i>Henderson, NV 89015</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The museum contains vintage races cars from different eras and includes sprint cars, midgets, Indy Race League (IRL) cars and engines, as well as aircraft models depicting various eras in aviation history. It also has an extensive gallery of motor sports and aviation photos, artifacts, trophies and memorabilia.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.carrollshelby.com/">Carroll Shelby Museum</a><br />
<i>Las Vegas Motor Speedway</i><br />
<i>Las Vegas, NV</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This facility, located at the entrance to the <a href="http://www.lvms.com/">speedway</a>, honors the racing legend who set record as a driver, owner and race car developer. Shelby set land speed records in 1954 at Bonneville, won the 24-hour Le Mans in 1959, created the legendary Shelby Cobra, and then, in 1991 after heart transplant surgery, created the Carroll Shelby Children's Foundation dedicated to providing assistance for acute coronary and kidney care for children in need.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/DualGhia.0.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" height="133" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/320/DualGhia.0.jpg" style="float: right; margin: 0pt 0pt 10px 10px;" width="200" /></a><a href="http://www.sunbeltcars.com/">Sunbelt Classic &amp; Antique Automobile Museum</a><br />
<i>Bruce Street &amp; Washington Avenue</i><br />
<i>Las Vegas, NV</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> An esoteric collection of such unusual display models as Frazer, Willys, Jeep, LaSalle, Hudson, Crosley, Dual Ghia (seen here) and other models along with the usual Ford, Chevy, Chrysler and other familiar makers' works.<br />

<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422552410683101' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-uTZ3I6HA_Q8/TbMm2OqSeJI/AAAAAAAAJq8/PxdBJIlUQCI/s1600/Picture+3.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" height="136" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-uTZ3I6HA_Q8/TbMm2OqSeJI/AAAAAAAAJq8/PxdBJIlUQCI/s200/Picture+3.png" width="200" /></a></div>
<a href="http://www.altonmotorsports.com/">Alton Motorsports Company</a><br />
<i>12 DOT 3 Street (off of Route 28 North)</i><br />
<i>PO Box 60</i><br />
<i>Alton, NH 03809&nbsp; </i><br />
<i>(603) 875-7575 </i><br />
<b>Features:</b> True, this is not really a museum, but in a state with a lack of auto museums it deserves mention in this guide because of its 10,000 square foot showroom that houses an extensive  inventory of owned consigned motorcycles, rods, classics and muscle cars. Obviously, open to the public.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.carrollstauto.com/site/about.htm">Carrol St. Auto </a><br />
<i>68 Carroll Street</i><br />
<i>Manchester, NH 03102</i><br />
<i> (603) 627-5595</i><br />
<b>Features: </b>Like Alton Motorsports (above), this is not really a museum but it gets a mention because of its large browse-around inventory of classic and muscle cars such as Mustangs, GTOs, Chevelles, Chargers,                Road Runners, Camaros, Novas, etc.&nbsp; <br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.wrightmuseum.org/abou.html">Wright Museum</a><br />
<i>77 Center Street</i><br />
<i>Wolfeboro, NH</i><br />
<i>(603) 569-1212</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This is a museum complex with three separate and distinct buildings. The Home Front and theater, the two-story educational building or Visitor's Center and the military building which houses memorabilia from all branches of service from WWII including a large collection of military vehicles from this era -- tanks, halftracks, jeeps, an ambulance, motorcycles and the only existing operational American tank to cross the Rhine River in Germany.<br />

</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422558216237813' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-0Mflz4V06WA/UYqwQt3uq_I/AAAAAAAAYDg/LxbNxOU36rI/s1600/Picture+3.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-0Mflz4V06WA/UYqwQt3uq_I/AAAAAAAAYDg/LxbNxOU36rI/s1600/Picture+3.png" /></a></div>
<a href="http://www.hemmings.com/hmn/stories/2007/01/01/hmn_feature5.html">CWJ Classic Automobile Museum</a> <br />
<i>18 Claremont Road </i><br />
<i>Bernardsville, NJ </i><br />
<i>(908) 221-0908</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The museum organizes rotating installations of American automobiles and art exhibitions that illustrate the role of the car in defining the social and economic climate of the country. It also has a collection of American convertibles from the 1940s, '50s and '60s that frequently are on display.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.spacefarms.com/">Space Farms Zoo and Museum</a><br />
<i>218 Route 519</i><br />
<i>Sussex, NJ 07461</i><br />
<b>Features: </b>Despite its name, this museum and 
zoo dedicated to conserving wildlife and preserving American history also has
 50 vintage cars and motorcycles as well as antique wagons and 
carriages. Open daily May 1 through October 31 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. <br />
 <br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.vintageautomuseum.org/">Vintage Automobile Museum of New Jersey</a><br />
<i>18 Bay Avenue</i><br />
<i>Point Pleasant, NJ 08722</i><br />
<i><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-family: inherit;">732-899-0012
</span></span></i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Currently closed due to damage by Hurricane Sandy. The website will update potential visitors as repairs are made.<br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://usclassiccars.blogspot.com/2006/07/new-mexico.html'>NEW MEXICO</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422561902998655' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<b>Antique Auto Barn</b><br />
<i>National Parks Highway 16-180</i><br />
<i>Carlsbad, NM 88220</i><br />
<i>(505) 885-2437</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> (No website)<br />
<br />
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/1948_Diamond_T_4.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/1948_Diamond_T_4.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.jrvintageautos.com/">J&amp;R Vintage Auto Museum</a><br />
<i>3650 NM Highway 528</i><br />
<i>Rio Rancho, NM 87124</i><br />
<i>(505) 867-2881</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> A collection of about 70 vehicles, including a Packard Gallery, several cars that competed in The Great American Race, and a large array of die-cast automotive-related toys. Overall, cars range from a 1902 Oldsmobile to a '48 Diamond-T truck (seen here) to a '69 Mercury Cougar.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.route66automuseum.com/">Route 66 Auto Museum </a><br />
<i>2866 Will Rogers Avenue (Historic Route 66)<br />
Santa Rosa, NM 88435</i><br />
<i>(575) 472-1966</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> More than 30 street rods, classic cars and vintage vehicles are on display. Curio shop sells car-related souvenirs.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tzmHNXxvo7g">Russell's Truck &amp; Travel Plaza and Museum</a> <br />
<i>Exit 369 of I-40 at the Texas-New Mexico border</i><br />
<i>Glenrio, NM</i><br />
<b>Features: </b>You won't find this collection on many museum lists, but it's worth stopping in. In addition to being a large truck plaza, it has a nicely-arranged free display of classic cars, trucks, automotive paraphernalia and model cars. Clicking on the title above will take you to a video shot by a trucker who says she was struck by the collection the first time she stopped in.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.unserracingmuseum.com/">Unser Racing Museum</a><br />
<i>1776 Montao NW </i><br />
<i>Los Ranchos de Albuquerque, NM 87107 </i><br />
<i>(505) 341-1776</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Memorabilia and race cars documenting the competitive history of one of the major family dynasties of the field.<br />
<br />
<b>YesterDave's Auto Museum</b><br />
<i>10601 Montgomery Boulevard NE<br />
Albuquerque, NM 87111</i> <br />
<i>(505) 293-0033&nbsp;</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> (No website) A small but nicely varied collection that includes several cars once owned by Elvis Presley and a 428 Cobra autographed by Carroll Shelby.<br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://usclassiccars.blogspot.com/2006/07/new-york.html'>NEW YORK</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-115422565855409926' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<a href="http://www.buffaloharley.com/miscpage_003.asp?sid=0355633X5K18K2005J8I09I14JPMQ1553R0">Buffalo Harley-Davidson Museum</a><br />
<i>4220 Bailey Avenue<br />
Amherst, NY 14226<br />
(716) 832-7159</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> All things Harley are on display here, including rare and special-edition bikes from across the years, plus gear and memorabilia.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-kSosbiWUJGg/TbMwEqsUZdI/AAAAAAAAJrA/V1GS7UmSQGM/s1600/Picture+4.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-kSosbiWUJGg/TbMwEqsUZdI/AAAAAAAAJrA/V1GS7UmSQGM/s1600/Picture+4.png" /></a></div>
<a href="http://www.pierce-arrow.com/">Buffalo Transportation Pierce Arrow Museum</a><br />
<i>263 Michigan Avenue</i><br />
<i>Buffalo, NY 14203</i><br />
<i>(716) 853-0084</i><br />
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Features:</span> The latest phase of this project is a replica of a circa 1927 gas station that was designed, but never built, by<br />
Frank Lloyd Wright. Next to the gas station will be a re-creation of a 3,500-square-foot greasing station that architecturally fits with the Wright design.  <br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.cvtmuseum.com/">Champlain Valley Transportation Museum</a><br />
<i>12 Museum Way</i><br />
<i>Plattsburgh, NY</i><br />
<i>(518) 566-7575</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This onetime autos-only collection of Lozier marque cars made in Pittsburgh now covers numerous modes of land and maritime transportation. Plus, of course, Loziers. <br />
<br />
<b>Collectors Cars Auto Museum</b><br />
<i>56 West Merrick Road </i><br />
<i>Freeport, NY 11520</i><br />
<i>(516) 378-6666</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> (No Web site.)<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.cooperclassiccars.com/gallery.asp">Cooper Classic Cars</a><br />
<i>137 Perry Street (Greenwich Village)<br />
New York, NY 10014<br />
(800) 719-3909 or (212) 929-3909</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> This is a classic car dealership with a display gallery and a changing lineup of classic, vintage and exotic vehicles.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.cayugacountyfairspeedway.com/index.php?option=com_contact&amp;task=view&amp;contact_id=1&amp;Itemid=72">DIRT Motorsports Hall of Fame &amp; Museum</a><br />
<i>1 Speedway Drive<br />
Cayuga County Fair Speedway <br />
Weedsport, NY 13166 <br />
(315) 834-6606</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Wide variety of racing and antique cars as well as racing memorabilia, particularly from the worldl of dirt track competition.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.fasnyfiremuseum.com/">FASNY Museum of Firefighting</a><br />
<i>117 Harry Howard Avenue<br />
Hudson, NY 12534<br />
(877) 347-3687</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> FASNY (Firemen's Association of the State of New York) owns this facility, which houses rare and antique fire trucks and apparatus, equipment, gear and memorabilia, including a large collection of artworks.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.geocities.com/MotorCity/Downs/6115">Himes Museum of Motor Racing Nostalgia</a><br />
<i>15 O'Neil Avenue</i><br />
<i>Bayshore, NY 11706</i><br />
<i>(516) 666-4912</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> Owner/curator Marty Himes prides himself on never refusing any donation. Thus, a very unusual and eclectic museum. Himes openly confesses he has "things most museums would have considered junk" but that he's restored for display. The vehicle collection includes a handful of stock cars,, midget racers and TQ (three-quarter) racers, equipment, and memorabilia.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.racingarchives.org/">International Motor Racing Research Center</a><br />
<i>610 South Decatur Street</i><br />
<i>Watkins Glen, NY 14891-1613</i><br />
<i>(607) 535-9044</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The center maintains archives for the personal papers of drivers, journalists and authors; rare books, serial publications, and reference books pertaining to drivers, cars, races and the history of motor racing; the historical records and newsletters of clubs and motor racing teams and corporations; and historical photographs, movie films, videos, oral histories, and memorabilia.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://mta.info/mta/museum/index.html">New York Transit Museum </a><br />
<i>130 Livingston Street, 10th Floor</i><br />
<i>Brooklyn, NY 11201</i><br />
<i>(718) 694-3451 </i><br />
<b>Features: </b>The museum, operated by the Metropolitan Transit Authority, is devoted to all forms of public transportation throughout the history of the city -- subways, above-ground trains, buses, taxis, trolleys, etc. The museum also operates a gallery annex in Grand Central Terminal in Manhattan that presents changing exhibitions. <br />
<br />
<a href="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/1600/stutz.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://photos1.blogger.com/blogger/6012/1261/200/stutz.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: right; margin: 0 0 10px 10px;" /></a><a href="http://www.classiccarmuseum.org/">Northeast Classic Car Museum</a><br />
<i>24 Rexford Street (NYS Rt. 23)</i><br />
<i>Norwich, NY 13815</i><br />
<i>(607) 334-2886</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> More than 120 cars are displayed in seven exhibits, including what is believed to be the largest collection of Franklin luxury cars in the world. Included are Duesenbergs, Packards, Cords, Auburns and Pierce-Arrows.<br />
<br />
<a href="http://www.oldrhinebeck.org/">Old Rhinebeck Aerodrome Collection</a><br />
<i>Old Rhinebeck Aerodrome</i><br />
<i>PO Box 229 </i><br />
<i>Rhinebeck, NY 12572</i><br />
<i>(845) 752-3200 </i><br />
<b>Features:</b> The aerodrome is widely known for its antique airplanes and airshows, but it also has an extensive and eclectic auto, truck and motorcycle collection that contains such highlights as a 1911 Baker Electric (fams of Donald Duck comics will remember this as Grandma Duck's car), a 1922 American La France fire truck, a 1930 Austin Seven, a 1908 Brush Runabout, a 1922 Cleveland Speedster, a 1917 Columbia Ambulance, a 1917 Indian Motorcycle, a 1915 Maxwell armored car,  a 1916 Packard Moving Van, a 1918 Renault light tank and a 1916 Willys Overland Opera Coupe.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-LAD72RX7JXM/TbM4Bh9GbuI/AAAAAAAAJrE/7PaUCMdgMOs/s1600/Picture+5.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" height="150" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-LAD72RX7JXM/TbM4Bh9GbuI/AAAAAAAAJrE/7PaUCMdgMOs/s200/Picture+5.png" width="200" /></a></div>
<a href="http://www.saratogaautomuseum.com/">Saratoga Automobile Museum</a><br />
<i>110 Avenue of the Pines</i><br />
<i>Saratoga Springs, NY 12866</i><br />
<i>(518) 587-1935</i><br />
<b>Features:</b> In addition to exhibiting classic and vintage cars from around the world, this museum emphasizes the role of New York State and the East in general in the development of the passenger, commercial and racing sectors of the automobile industry. The facility, located in the picturesque Saratoga State Park, also is headquarters for the New York State Stock Car Association Hall of Fame.<br />
