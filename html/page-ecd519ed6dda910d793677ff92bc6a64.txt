<a href='http://automotiveandmotorcycles.blogspot.com/2009/10/lorenzo-cuts-rossis-title-advantage.html'>Lorenzo cuts Rossi&#8217;s title advantage with Estoril triumph</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
<div style='text-align: right;'>
<a href='http://twitter.com/home?status=Check this Out: http://automotiveandmotorcycles.blogspot.com/2009/10/lorenzo-cuts-rossis-title-advantage.html |Motorcycles' target='_blank' title='Click to Tweet this page'>

<p class="date"><span><span style="color:#007cc6;">Sunday</span></span>, 04 October 2009</p><h2 class="newsItem">A great win for Jorge Lorenzo at the bwin.com Grande Premio de Portugal saw him reduce the gap to Valentino Rossi in the standings to 18 points. </h2><div class="imgRelated"><a title="Estoril 2009 - MotoGP Race Highlights" href="http://www.blogger.com/en/videos/2009/Estoril+2009+MotoGP+Race+Highlights"><img title="Estoril 2009 - MotoGP Race Highlights" alt="Estoril 2009 - MotoGP Race Highlights" src="http://resources.motogp.com//files/videos/xy/2009/MotoGP/FP2/09_Estoril_MotoGP_Race.preview_wm.jpg" /></a></div><p class="imgRelated"><em><strong>Estoril 2009 - MotoGP Race Highlights</strong></em></p><div id="newsContent"><p><strong></strong><p>Jorge Lorenzo repeated his 2008 Estoril victory from pole on Sunday to significantly boost his title chances as Valentino Rossi could only manage fourth place at the challenging Portuguese circuit.</p><p>The Fiat Yamaha pair got away in first and second places on the grid but Rossi was unable to match the pace of Lorenzo as he pulled away quickly at the front. On his return to action Ducati&#8217;s Casey Stoner rode brilliantly to follow Lorenzo across the line in second place, whilst Repsol Honda&#8217;s Dani Pedrosa was third.</p><p>Lorenzo will now feel that with three races to go and an 18 point deficit to Rossi the championship is far more achievable than before the visit to Portugal. The Spaniard was in unstoppable form in the Atlantic Coast sunshine and his fourth win of the year was eventually secured by a 6.3s margin.</p><p>Stoner performed admirably on his return to action after two months away due to illness, achieving his first podium since Assen in June &#8211; and he now has his home race at Phillip Island to look forward to in a fortnight&#8217;s time.</p><p>Pedrosa rode smoothly from fourth on the grid, taking the holeshot with one of his traditionally rapid starts onboard the factory RC212V and briefly holding the lead before Lorenzo slipped through. Pedrosa&#8217;s third place was his eighth podium of 2009.</p><p>World Champion Rossi appeared to be off-colour throughout the race, finishing off the podium for the first time at Estoril in the premier class. The championship leader will undertake a comprehensive inquest into his lack of competitiveness with his Fiat Yamaha crew after finishing 23 seconds behind Lorenzo.</p><p>Behind the MotoGP big guns at the front, Colin Edwards (Monster Yamaha Tech 3) was 10 seconds back on Rossi in fifth place, whilst Toni El√≠as (San Carlo Honda Gresini) held off Andrea Dovizioso (Repsol Honda) for sixth on the line.</p><p>The top ten also featured Nicky Hayden (Ducati Marlboro), James Toseland (Monster Yamaha Tech 3) and Chris Vermeulen (Rizla Suzuki), with Mika Kallio (Pramac Racing) crashing out early on and Loris Capirossi (Rizla Suzuki) and Alex de Angelis (San Carlo Honda Gresini) retiring due to technical problems.</p><p><a href="http://www.motogp.com/en/news/2009/motogp+race+estoril">http://www.motogp.com/en/news/2009/motogp+race+estoril</a></p></div>

<a href='http://automotiveandmotorcycles.blogspot.com/2009/10/race-is-on-between-elly-and-zulfahmi.html'>Race is on between Elly and Zulfahmi for wildcard spot</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
<div style='text-align: right;'>
<a href='http://twitter.com/home?status=Check this Out: http://automotiveandmotorcycles.blogspot.com/2009/10/race-is-on-between-elly-and-zulfahmi.html |Motorcycles' target='_blank' title='Click to Tweet this page'>
<img alt='Twitter submit button' src='http://img220.imageshack.us/img220/6449/twitterlogon.png'/>

<div class='post-body entry-content'>
Wednesday September 30, 2009<br /><br />By LIM TEIK HUAT<br /><br />KUALA LUMPUR: It&#8217;s down to Elly Idzlianizar Ilias and Mohd Zulfahmi Khairuddin to get the wildcard to ride for the Malaysian 125cc team in the Shell Advance Malaysian Motorcycle Grand Prix at the Sepang Circuit from Oct 23-25.<br /><br />The 25-year-old Elly and 18-year-old Zulfahmi are the final two candidates shortlisted from the list of four hopefuls, who are all competing in the Malaysian Cub Prix Championships.<br />The other two were Mohd Iskandar Raduan and Mohd Farid Ahmad and they were selected last month from a list of 17 riders identified for the Malaysian wildcard programme, which is a collaboration between Sepang International Circuit, Motorsports Association of Malaysia, Automobile Association of Malaysia and Two Wheels Motor Racing.<br /><br />Shahrol Yuzy, who was the last Malaysian to ride on the world stage seven years ago and is now the coach for the riders, said that the two candidates will take part in two days more of testing on the Yamaha bike on Oct 6 and 7 before a final decision is made.<br /><br />Prime Minister Datuk Seri Najib Tun Razak will unveil the Malaysian team and the rider for the GP in Sepang the following day.<br /><br />&#8220;There is not much difference between the two riders. Although Elly is the more experienced one, Zulfahmi is giving him a tough fight and he is physically strong as well,&#8221; said Shahrol, who last rode for Team Petronas Sprinta TVK in the 250cc class of the world motorcycle championship in 2002.<br /><br />The Malaysian wildcard rider will be astride an Aprilia race bike provided by the full-time GP 125cc side, Ongetta ISPA, who are based in Italy.<br /><br />The Sepang International Circuit (SIC) chairman, Datuk Mokhzani Tun Mahathir, said that the penultimate round of a 17-leg championships this year in Malaysia would be more meaningful because of there would be a local interest again.<br /><br />&#8220;The wildcard rider is finally turning into a reality after so many years without one. It is also going to be an exciting weekend as we have other interactive activities planned,&#8221; he said.<br />&#8220;You can also get a grandstand ticket from as low as RM30. It is about five Euros and I think you cannot get a better bargain in any other countries hosting the championships.&#8221;<br />Among the programmes planned for the fans are autograph-signing sessions, stunt shows, guided garage tours and special go-kart races with the riders.<br /><br /><a href="http://thestar.com.my/sports/story.asp?file=/2009/9/30/sports/4808695&amp;sec=sports">http://thestar.com.my/sports/story.asp?file=/2009/9/30/sports/4808695&amp;sec=sports</a>

<a href='http://automotiveandmotorcycles.blogspot.com/2009/10/kallio-pleased-with-performance-on.html'>Kallio pleased with performance on factory machine</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
<div style='text-align: right;'>
<a href='http://twitter.com/home?status=Check this Out: http://automotiveandmotorcycles.blogspot.com/2009/10/kallio-pleased-with-performance-on.html |Motorcycles' target='_blank' title='Click to Tweet this page'>

Tuesday, 08 September 2009<br /><br />Riding for Ducati Marlboro on Sunday Mika Kallio achieved his best MotoGP result to date. <a title="" href="http://www.motogp.com/en/photos/2009/Mika+Kallio+in+action+in+Misano+1"></a><br />In what is scheduled to be his last factory Ducati ride for this season at least, with Casey Stoner due back on track in Portugal at the next round of the MotoGP World Championship, Mika Kallio seized his opportunity well on Sunday to sign off from the Ducati Marlboro team with his best premier class result to date.<br /><br />The MotoGP rookie crossed the Misano finishing line in seventh position, and was less than four seconds behind fourth placed Andrea Dovizioso at the end of the race, adding to his eighth place at Indianapolis, as he bounced back from his Brno DNF on the factory Desmosedici.<br />Reviewing the round 13 race, Kallio commented, &#8220;I think we can be quite happy, because that was the best result so far this season and also fourth place was not so far away, just a few seconds ahead of me. I got away ok but the first two corners were not so good as a few guys crashed in front of me and I lost a few positions there so it was difficult to catch the guys ahead of us.&#8221;<br /><br />On his experience with the factory team as a whole over the three Grands Prix he spent with them, he stated, &#8220;It was more or less how I expected, of course I hoped that we could get better results, because in Brno I crashed with Marco Melandri and at Indianapolis I didn&#8217;t have too much confidence on the bike so we weren&#8217;t that fast there, but here at Misano everything was already at a better level, so the confidence was coming back lap by lap. Then in the race we were quite fast so I think that without the incident on the first lap we would have been able to fight for fourth.&#8221;<br /><br /><a href="http://www.motogp.com/en/news/2009/Kallio+pleased+with+performance+on+factory+machine">http://www.motogp.com/en/news/2009/Kallio+pleased+with+performance+on+factory+machine</a>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://automotiveandmotorcycles.blogspot.com/2009/10/kallio-renews-contract-with-pramac.html'>Kallio renews contract with Pramac Racing for 2010</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
<div style='text-align: right;'>
<a href='http://twitter.com/home?status=Check this Out: http://automotiveandmotorcycles.blogspot.com/2009/10/kallio-renews-contract-with-pramac.html |Motorcycles' target='_blank' title='Click to Tweet this page'>

<div class='post-body entry-content'>
Wednesday, 30 September 2009<br /><br />Finnish rider Mika Kallio has been confirmed as a Ducati satellite rider in MotoGP for another year.<br /><br />The steady progress and solid performances of MotoGP rookie Mika Kallio have earned him another season in the premier class with his current employers Pramac Racing, who have confirmed that he will ride for them again in 2010.<br /><br />Returning to the team this weekend at the bwin.com Grande Premio de Portugal, after standing in for three rounds in the factory Ducati team as a replacement for Casey Stoner, Kallio now goes into the final four races of the 2009 World Championship totally focused on the job at hand.<br />He stated, &#8220;Now that we have agreed for next season we can exclusively concentrate on our work on track. I am very happy to be able to continue with the Pramac Racing Team, with Paolo Campinoti and with Ducati. This season is going in a positive direction. We have had some highs and lows, but considering that it is my rookie season, that&#8217;s normal. We trust in our ability improve next year.&#8221;<br /><br />Paolo Campinoti, Pramac Racing&#8217;s Team Principal, stated, &#8220;We are very happy to agree this deal with Mika and to continue to work together next season.
