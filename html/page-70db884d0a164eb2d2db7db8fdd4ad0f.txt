					<div class="post-content"><p>It&#8217;s official: Green car madness has taken over. After seeing more electric and hybrid vehicle startups than we could keep track of, we finally decided to start keeping count.</p>
<p>We&#8217;ve compiled a list, below, of 27 (<strong>update</strong>: the list has reached 30; thanks for the comments) startups, listed according to their release date, with additional information on fuel type, range, top speed and price. Most haven&#8217;t yet taken venture funding, but where applicable, we&#8217;ve listed financial backing.</p>
<p>While we&#8217;ve got some overall favorites (Miles, Tesla, Think) and a few favorite oddballs (Aptera, Commuter Cars, Eliica), we&#8217;ve for the most part withheld judgement. Still, if you have any of your own predictions about which companies will succeed or &#8212; far more likely &#8212; fail, we&#8217;d encourage you to make them known in the comments.</p>
<p>A note on our method: While most manufacturers are planning more than one model, we chose the one that seemed either most commercially viable or closest to release, depending on our own (discretionary) formula. We didn&#8217;t included well-known consumer models like the <a href="http://www.toyota.com/prius/"id="oed_" title="Toyota Prius"  target="_blank">Toyota Prius</a> or <a href="http://www.chevrolet.com/electriccar/"id="o1u3" title="Chevy Volt"  target="_blank">Chevy Volt</a>, or startups like <a href="http://www.acpropulsion.com/"id="u.lt" title="AC Propulsion"  target="_blank">AC Propulsion</a> that only do battery conversions for consumer vehicles. We also rounded the price to the nearest thousand.</p>
<p>All details are taken from the companies, so we haven&#8217;t independently confirmed things like range and top speed details. In case we missed any, mention them below and we&#8217;ll add them to the list.</p>

These tiny, odd little vehicles are powered <a href="http://www.goodcleantech.com/2008/01/the_air_car_gets_closer_to_pro.php"id="faq0" title="entirely by compressed air"  target="_blank">entirely by compressed air</a>. You won&#8217;t be likely to see one Stateside just yet, but India&#8217;s Tata Motors has plans to begin production on them this year.<br />
Fuel type: Air!<br />
Price: TBA<br />
Range / top speed: 120 miles / 70mph<br />
Release date: 2008 (month TBA)</p>
<p><a href="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/porteon10.png"title="porteon10.png"  target="_blank"><img style="float:right;" src="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/porteon10.thumbnail.png" alt="porteon10.png" /></a><strong><a href="http://www.porteon.net/"id="lb:-" title="Porteon"  target="_blank">Porteon</a></strong> &#8212; Porteon<br />
Reportedly to be sold alongside houses in small Mexican communities. Like other companies, this one plans to roll out higher-speed models later, but they haven&#8217;t gotten back to us yet with more details, including the official name.<br />
Fuel type: All-electric<br />
Price: $7K &#8211; $9K<br />
Range / top speed: TBA / 25mph<br />
Release date: 2008 (month TBA)</p>
<p><a href="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/spyder.jpg"title="spyder.JPG"  target="_blank"><img style="float:right;" src="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/spyder.thumbnail.jpg" alt="spyder.JPG" /></a><strong><a href="http://www.universalelectricvehicle.com/"id="n94b" title="Universal Electric Vehicles"  target="_blank">Universal Electric Vehicles</a></strong> &#8212; Spyder<br />
UEV&#8217;s Spyder has a sporty design, but the company itself doesn&#8217;t seem as slick and polished as some of the competitors. We&#8217;re not sure whether that means anything, though.<br />
Fuel type: All-electric<br />
Price: $70K<br />
Range / top speed: 150 miles / 100+mph<br />
Release date: 2008 (month TBA)</p>
<p><a href="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/aptera.jpg"title="aptera.JPG"  target="_blank"><img style="float:right;" src="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/aptera.thumbnail.jpg" alt="aptera.JPG" /></a><strong><a href="http://www.aptera.com/"id="vyf9" title="Aptera"  target="_blank">Aptera</a></strong> &#8212; Typ-1e / Type-1h<br />
The Aptera Typ-1 is a three-wheeler (two front, one back) available in two configurations. We&#8217;ve covered the vehicle <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2007/12/14/would-you-drive-this-car-aptera-unveils-the-typ-1/"id="a71m" title="here" >here</a>.<br />
Fuel type: All-electric or hybrid, depending on configuration<br />
Price: $27K electric, $30K hybrid<br />
Range / top speed: 120 miles electric, 600 miles hybrid; top speed TBA<br />
Funding: Backed by Idealab and an angel investor, the CEO of Esenjay Petroleum, Michael Johnson.<br />
Release date: Late 2008</p>
<p><a href="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/obvio.jpg"title="obvio.JPG"  target="_blank"><img style="float:right;" src="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/obvio.thumbnail.jpg" alt="obvio.JPG" /></a><strong><a href="http://www.obvio.ind.br/"id="h--w" title="Obvio"  target="_blank">Obvio</a></strong> &#8212; 828e<br />
Sold in the United States by Zap, the diminutive but high-powered Obvio could almost race a Tesla. It&#8217;s designed and manufactured by a Brazilian company that is also working on a cheap combustion engine version.<br />
Fuel type: All-electric<br />
Price: $49k<br />
Range / top speed: 240 miles / 120 mph<br />
Release date: Late 2008 or early 2009</p>
<p><a href="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/fisker.jpg"title="fisker.JPG"  target="_blank"><img style="float:right;" src="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/fisker.thumbnail.jpg" alt="fisker.JPG" /></a><strong><a href="http://www.fiskerautomotive.com/"id="s7mg" title="Fisker Automotive"  target="_blank">Fisker Automotive</a></strong> &#8212; &#8220;Eco-Chic&#8221; (Working name TBA)<br />
Fisker will be showing off the first productions models of its cars on January 13th, at the Detroit Auto Show. Created by a world-renowned auto designer, these will compete in Tesla&#8217;s high-end market niche.<br />
Fuel type: Hybrid<br />
Price: $80K<br />
Range / top speed: 620 miles / TBA<br />
Backing: Kleiner Perkins Caufield &amp; Byers has invested more than $10 million in Fisker.<br />
Release date: Mid-2009</p>
<p><a href="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/flytheroad.jpg"title="flytheroad.JPG"  target="_blank"><img style="float:right;" src="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/flytheroad.thumbnail.jpg" alt="flytheroad.JPG" /></a><strong><a href="http://www.flytheroad.com/"id="ehaa" title="Venture Vehicles"  target="_blank">Venture Vehicles</a></strong> &#8212; VentureOne e50 / Q100 / EV<br />
We previously suggested that some might call this vehicle a &#8220;glorified scooter.&#8221; The company does have supporters, though, who gave us plenty of heat &#8212; for a chuckle, check out the comment thread below <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2007/08/20/venture-vehicles-maker-of-nifty-electric-car-with-100mpg"id="aq0y" title="the story" >our story</a>. The three models listed are actually one car with different configurations.<br />
Fuel type: Two hybrids, the e50 and Q100, and the all-electric EV<br />
Price: $20K &#8211; 25K<br />
Range / top speed: e50, Q100 and EV respectively: 350 miles / 100mph; 300 miles / 120mph; 120 miles / 75mph<br />
Backing: Backed by NGEN Partners.<br />
Release date: Second quarter 2009</p>
<p><a href="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/miles.jpg"title="miles.JPG"  target="_blank"><img style="float:right;" src="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/miles.thumbnail.jpg" alt="miles.JPG" /></a><strong><a href="http://www.milesev.com/#aboutus.swf"id="lqdc" title="Miles Electric Vehicles"  target="_blank">Miles Electric Vehicles</a></strong> &#8212; XS500<br />
Founded in 2004, this company already makes and sells two low-speed electric vehicles which are manufactured in China. We chose to list details for its upcoming highway-speed model, which should have a mass market appeal, if the price is low enough.<br />
Fuel type: All-electric<br />
Price: TBA<br />
Range / top speed: 120 miles / 80mph<br />
Release date: 2009 (month TBA)</p>
<p><strong>Bonus: Concept cars</strong></p>
<p>We&#8217;ve picked out a few companies that represent next-generation thinking in EVs. As noted below, there are many more.<a href="http://www.eliica.com/"id="dhe1" title="Eliica"  target="_blank"></a></p>
<p><a href="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/eliica.jpg"title="eliica.JPG"  target="_blank"><img style="float:right;" src="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/eliica.thumbnail.jpg" alt="eliica.JPG" /></a><strong><a href="http://www.eliica.com/"id="dhe1" title="Eliica"  target="_blank">Eliica</a></strong> (The Electric Lithium-Ion Car)<br />
A concept car created by Keio University in Japan, the Eliica has an odd eight-wheeled design and has come close to breaking speed records for combustion-engine cars. The team plans to sell a couple hundred eventually.<br />
Fuel type: All-electric<br />
Price: $255K<br />
Range / top speed: TBA / 230mph</p>
<p><a href="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/velozzi.jpg"title="velozzi.JPG"  target="_blank"><img style="float:right;" src="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/velozzi.thumbnail.jpg" alt="velozzi.JPG" /></a><strong><a href="http://www.velozzi.org/"id="gz6-" title="Velozzi"  target="_blank">Velozzi</a></strong><br />
An X-Prize contestant, Velozzi is most notable for claiming that their car will have a 0-60mph acceleration of under 3 seconds. Other details are pretty thin, though.<br />
Fuel type: Hybrid<br />
Price: TBA<br />
Range / top speed: TBA / 200mph</p>
<p><a href="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/wrightspeed.jpg"title="wrightspeed.JPG"  target="_blank"><img style="float:right;" src="http://venturebeat.files.wordpress.com/2008/01/wrightspeed.thumbnail.jpg" alt="wrightspeed.JPG" /></a><strong><a href="http://www.wrightspeed.com/"id="k9i6" title="Wrightspeed"  target="_blank">Wrightspeed</a></strong> &#8212; X-1<br />
Wrightspeed admits that its scarily fast prototype is unsafe, and will never be mass-produced. The company is planning a commercial model with the same high performance, but hasn&#8217;t given any real details yet.<br />
Fuel type: All-electric<br />
Price: Probably high<br />
Range / top speed: &lt;100 miles / 112mph w/ electronic limiter (but it&#8217;s still in first gear when it hits that speed)</p>

Most are concept cars.</p>
