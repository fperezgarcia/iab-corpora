	<h1 class="posttitle">Luxury Tax On Cars</h1>
</header>
			<div class="postdata fix">
			</div>
		<span class='post-format-icon'>&nbsp;</span><span class='updated' title='2012-09-19T08:46:14+00:00'></span>			<div class="entry-container fix">
				<div class="entry fix">
					<h1>(&#8220;LCT&#8221;)</h1>
<p>Theluxurycartax on taxable supplies and importations of luxury carsapplies to supplies of cars which are over the luxury car tax thresholds.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;">&gt;&gt; Note: <a title="Car depreciation limits" href="http://atotaxrates.info/tax-deductions/work-related-car-expenses/luxury-car-limits" target="_blank">If you are looking for the luxury car<em>depreciation</em> limit go here</a> &lt;&lt;</p>
<p><strong>The luxury car tax rate is 33%.</strong></p>
<p>The LCT applies in addition to GST. Itisdetermined by taking the GST inclusive excess over the threshold, andthen excluding GST on thatexcess amountbefore applying the tax percentage. The calculated LCT is then reduced by the amount of LCT already paid (if any).</p>
<p>The LCT only applies to supplies by entities<strong>registered for GST</strong>.</p>
<p>A<strong>&#8216;luxury car&#8217;</strong>is a motor vehicle with a carrying capacity of less than 2 tonnes and 9 passengers. It includes four wheel drives, passenger cars, station wagons, light trucks, limousines (regardless of the number of passengers).</p>
<p>A &#8216;luxury car&#8217; does<em>not</em>include trucks and vans designed to carry a load of more than two tonnes, vehicles such as buses, designed to carry nine or more passengers, motorcycles or similar vehicles, unregistered racing and rally cars, prescribed emergency vehicles, cars more than 2 years old, campervans and mobile homes.</p>
<h3>People With Disabilities</h3>
<p>Cars used for transportingpeople with a disability are excluded only if GST-free. See more information for<a href='http://www.ato.gov.au/Individuals/People-with-disability/In-detail/Cars/GST-and-LCT-on-cars-you-buy---people-with-disabilities/' onclick='return popitup(this.href, 950, 500);' class='simple_popup_link '>people with disabilities here.</a><br />
<a title="LCT on transport for the disabled" href="http://www.ato.gov.au/businesses/content.aspx?menuid=0&amp;doc=/content/13288.htm&amp;page=8&amp;H8" target="_blank"><br />
</a></p>
<h2><a name="lctthresholds"></a>Luxury Car Tax Thresholds</h2>
<p><strong><span style="color: #800000;">Update</span></strong>: Thresholds from 1 July 2013 &#8211; for 2013-14</p>
<ul>
<li><span style="line-height: 13px;">The indexed LCT threshold for the 2013-14 financial year is $60,316</span></li>
<li>
<p style="display: inline !important;">The fuel-efficient car limit for the 2013-14 financialyear remains at $75,375.(<a class="fancybox-iframe" href="http://law.ato.gov.au/pdf/pbr/lcd2013-001.pdf&quot;target">LCTD 2013/1</a>)</p>
</li>
</ul>
<a href='http://www.ato.gov.au/Business/Activity-statements/In-detail/Instructions/Luxury-car-tax---how-to-complete-your-activity-statement/' onclick='return popitup(this.href, 950, 500);' class='simple_popup_link '><img class="alignleft  wp-image-561" style="margin: 5px;" title="LCT - Activity Statement" alt="" src="http://atotaxrates.info/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/lctinstructions1.png" width="177" /></a>
<p>For<strong> 2012-13 </strong>the luxury car tax threshold is indexed to $59,133.<br />
For fuel efficient cars the thresholdremains at $75,375.(<a class="fancybox-iframe" href="http://law.ato.gov.au/pdf/pbr/lcd2012-001.pdf&quot;target">LCTD 2012/1</a>)</p>
<p>For the year <strong>2011-12</strong> the LCT rate is 33% of the GST-excluded amount of GST inclusive excess over the threshold amounts of $57,466 or $75,375 for fuel efficient cars.</p>
<p><em>Fuel efficient cars</em>are<em>v</em>ehicles with fuel consumption below 7 litres per 100 kms</p>
<p>Deferralsof LCT payment are available for eligible dealers andimporters.</p>
<p>Eligibleprimary producers and tourism operatorscan within limits obtain refunds of LCT up to a maximum of $3,000.</p>
<p>The LCT collection and payment processes are administered through the GST and<a href='http://atotaxrates.info/businesses/bas-statement' onclick='return popitup(this.href, 950, 500);' class='simple_popup_link '>BAS</a>returns systems at the ATO, which provides clear and comprehensive guidance on what to do. See the ATO&#8217;s <a href='http://www.ato.gov.au/Business/Bus/Luxury-car-tax/' onclick='return popitup(this.href, 950, 500);' class='simple_popup_link '>luxury car tax guide here.</a></p>
<p><strong>Activity Statements</strong>-<a href='http://www.ato.gov.au/Business/Luxury-car-tax/Registering,-reporting-and-paying/' onclick='return popitup(this.href, 950, 500);' class='simple_popup_link '>Luxury Car Tax Registering Reporting and Paying</a></p>
<p><a title="Luxury Car Tax Activity Statement Guide" href="http://atotaxrates.info/PDFS/LuxuryCarTax-ActivityStatementInstructions-nat7391.pdf" target="_blank"><br />
</a></p>
<h3>GST onLuxury Cars</h3>
<p>Generally,the maximum amount of GST credit available isone-eleventh of the non-fuel efficient luxury car limit (whether or not the car isfuel efficient). No GST credit applies to any part of the Luxury Car Tax, regardless of business use.</p>
<p>Exceptional circumstances in whicha full GST credit may be claimed, include:</p>
<ul>
<li>car held as trading stock (other than hire or lease)</li>
<li>you carry out research and development for the car manufacturer</li>
<li>emergency vehicle</li>
<li>non-passenger (principle purpose) commercial vehicles</li>
<li>motor home or camper van</li>
<li>fitted out for wheelchair disabled (unless acquired GST-free)</li>
<li>vehicle lease payments (to the extent of GST included)</li>
</ul>
