<blockquote><center>
<h1>How To Get The Best Deals<br> On New Or Used Cars<br> ... Even Wholesale</h1>
</center>
<br>

<div style="width:153; height:102; float:left;"><img src="images/ford-fusion.jpg" width="150" height="100" border="0" align="left" alt="Ford Fusion"></DIV>

<p align=justify>
<font face=verdana color=#000000 SIZE=3>Hi, Josh Rosenberg here.  I'm a recently-retired Auto Broker who's still devoted to helping the average person get a great deal on the new or used car of their choice.  Most people hate the whole car buying process.  On the other hand, I actually love it and somehow became quite good at it.

<br><br>So, I'm here to simply pass along my knowledge and experience to, in effect, help you become your own Auto Broker and to take the mystery, distrust and stress out of car buying ... and all while getting just the very best out-the-door prices possible.

<br><br>Ready to get started?  Are you looking for a new car or a used one?

</font></p>

<hr>

<p align=justify>
<font face=verdana color=#000080 SIZE=5><b><u>New Cars</u></b>:
</font></p>

<div style="width:153; height:102; float:left;"><img src="images/chevy-cruze.jpg" width="150" height="100" border="0" align="left" alt="Chevrolet Cruze"></DIV>

<p align=justify>
<IMG SRC=images/grip201.gif>  <font face=verdana color=#000000 SIZE=3><A HREF="http://www.auto-broker-magic.com/buy-new-car.html"><b><u>How To Always Get The Lowest Price</u></b></A>

<br><br><IMG SRC=images/grip201.gif>  <A HREF="http://www.auto-broker-magic.com/carwoo-review.html"><b><u>The CarWoo Anonymous Quote System</u></b></A>

<br><br><IMG SRC=images/grip201.gif>  <A HREF="http://www.auto-broker-magic.com/truecar-review.html"><b><u>TrueCar's "Prices Paid" Advantage</u></b></A>

<br><br>My target prices for either new and used cars are almost always "wholesale".   For new cars, this can be a little hard to pinpoint because the readily available "invoice price" numbers on the web no longer truly reflect a dealer's cost.  But you'll get a much better feel for "wholesale" on a specific new car by learning the lowest prices already paid in your area (more on this in the above articles).
</font></p>

<hr>

<p align=justify>
<font face=verdana color=#000080 SIZE=5><b><u>Used Cars</u></b>:
</font></p>

<div style="width:153; height:102; float:left;"><img src="images/toyota-corolla.jpg" width="150" height="100" border="0" align="left" alt="Toyota Corolla"></DIV>

<p align=justify>
<IMG SRC=images/grip201.gif>  <font face=verdana color=#000000 SIZE=3><A HREF="http://www.auto-broker-magic.com/usedcarbuying.html"><b><u>Best Used Car Buying Method</u></b></A>

<br><br><IMG SRC=images/grip201.gif>  <A HREF="http://www.auto-broker-magic.com/car-auction.html"><b><u>Wholesale Auto Auction Opportunities</u></b></A><br><br>

<IMG SRC=images/grip201.gif>  <A HREF="http://www.auto-broker-magic.com/used-car.html"><b><u>Wholesale Opportunities On Dealer Lots</u></b></A>

<br><br>For used cars, wholesale represents a purchase at or below a vehicle's trade-in value.  And what is a vehicle's true trade-in value?  Well, this can get a little tricky.  But essentially, it's the price a Dealer would pay for the same vehicle at any given moment in time at a Dealer-Only car auction. (Here's more on <A href="http://www.auto-broker-magic.com/car-value.html"><b><u>"wholesale" and "trade-in" values</u></b></A>.)
</font></p>

<hr>

<p align=justify>
<font face=verdana color=#000080 SIZE=5><b><u>Car Loans</u></b>:
</font></p>

<div style="width:153; height:102; float:left;"><img src="images/ford-f150.jpg" width="150" height="100" border="0" align="left" alt="Ford F-150"></DIV>

<p align=justify>
<IMG SRC=images/grip201.gif>  <font face=verdana color=#000000 SIZE=3><A HREF="http://www.auto-broker-magic.com/car-loans-used.html"><b><u>How To Get The Lowest Rate Car Loans</u></b></A>

<br><br>Try to stay away from traditional Car Dealership loans.  They're simply the <i>most likely</i> to put a much bigger dent in your wallet.  Most car buyers don't realize that while Car Dealers are obviously trying to make a nice profit on the car, they can also ring up big extra profits on the financing as well.  It can be a real double whammy on the unaware and you can likely do much better elsewhere.

</font></p>

<hr>

<p align=justify>
<font face=verdana color=#000080 SIZE=5><b><u>Car Insurance</u></b>:
</font></p>

<div style="width:153; height:102; float:left;"><img src="images/jeep-wrangler.jpg" width="150" height="100" border="0" align="left" alt="Jeep Wrangler"></DIV>

<p align=justify>
<IMG SRC=images/grip201.gif>  <font face=verdana color=#000000 SIZE=3><A HREF="http://www.auto-broker-magic.com/car-insurance.html"><b><u>How To Save On Car Insurance</u></b></A>

<br><br>It seems like the vast majority of people take little interest in their auto insurance.  Well, they're very likely flushing away a significant amount of very hard-earned dollars.  Insurance may be boring, but spending a few minutes to review the key factors as well as where to get quick and easy quotes from competing insurers can rack up thousands of dollars in savings over time.

</font></p>

<hr>

<p align=justify>
<font face=verdana color=#000080 SIZE=4><b><u>A Message From Josh</u></b>:
</font></p>

<div style="width:153; height:102; float:left;"><img src="images/honda-crv.jpg" width="150" height="100" border="0" align="left" alt="Honda CR-V"></DIV>

<P ALIGN=JUSTIFY>
<FONT FACE=VERDANA COLOR=#000000 SIZE=3>I know alot of people who love the thought of driving around in a new car, but hate the thought of going through the agonizing, stress-filled and distrustful process of buying one.  Why does buying a new or used car have to be so unpleasant, anyway?

<br><br>Sadly, that's simply the way the auto industry is structured to operate.  And until that changes to where all car prices are fixed and are no longer negotiated, like buying nearly everything else, the process will remain muddled and distasteful, often exceedingly so.

<BR><BR>Well, let's change that right now, okay?  In addition to being somewhat of a car fanatic, for years before my retirement I was an Auto Broker, buying cars for customers on their behalf at great prices and without any hassle.  Somehow, the business spread far and wide and we had clients from all over the country, even internationally (here's some <A href="http://www.auto-broker-magic.com/Testimonials.html"><b><u>customer testimonials</u></b></A> from those days ... just a few years ago, actually).

<br><br>But I guess I'm not quite fully retired afterall.  You see, I'd still very much like to share my experiences in hopes of helping many more car buyers help themselves.  So, I've simply decided to teach everyday consumers everything I know about how to buy either a new or used car at the best possible price by basically acting as their own Auto Broker ... and it's not difficult at all.

 <BR><BR>There's also more here as well to help car shoppers.  In addition to car buying and money-saving advice, you'll also find information on both new and used car models to help narrow down your wish list.  

<BR><BR>Welcome, hope you enjoy the site and let me know about your great deal.

<br><br>All the best,

<br><br>Josh
