<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
2014 Mercedes E-Class Coupe
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-3983388584683220512' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-ms-ZvUwqgfk/Uaa8uRMvPUI/AAAAAAAAGqw/W8SKIGDXkY0/s1600/628x417x01-2014-mercedes-benz-e-class-coupe-fd-opt.png.pagespeed.ic.38-2zW-8sp.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="424" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-ms-ZvUwqgfk/Uaa8uRMvPUI/AAAAAAAAGqw/W8SKIGDXkY0/s640/628x417x01-2014-mercedes-benz-e-class-coupe-fd-opt.png.pagespeed.ic.38-2zW-8sp.png" width="640" /></a></div>
<br />
<br />
Though the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/mercedes-benz/s-class/">S-Class</a> is the profit-per-unit world champion, Mercedes' stalwart <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/mercedes-benz/e-class/">E-Class</a> is still the company's most reliable producer of profits. The latter tends to represent the model that most humans on Earth think of when they think of <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/mercedes-benz/">Mercedes-Benz</a>. The taxi version of the E-Class sedan used in Europe and elsewhere is the usual reason given for this familiarity we all have with the E, but c'mon, it also happens to be a pretty stunning piece of work in its market segment.<br /><br />Today we're trying the upgraded and massaged fourth-generation<a href="http://www.autoblog.com/mercedes-benz/e-class+coupe/">Mercedes E-Class Coupe</a> (known internally as C207). Specifically we're driving the 329-horsepower E400 coupe that arrives in the US next summer (2014) to eventually replace &#8211; in all body variants &#8211; the current V8-powered E550. Starting price for a base E350 coupe should scare up $51,500 when it arrives a little later this year, while this E400 two-door will hit US soil at around $56,000 as a futuristic-sounding 2015 model.<div>
