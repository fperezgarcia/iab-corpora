		<div class="article-content" itemprop="articleBody">
		  <p>For an electric car, the 2013 <a title="Ford" href="http://blogs.automotive.com/manufacturer/ford/">Ford</a> Focus Electric delivers an ample amount of acceleration while maintaining the quietness in the cabin one expects from electric vehicles, prompting us to say, &#8220;For better or worse, it&#8217;s one of the best electric vehicles you can buy today.&#8221;</p>
<p>With an interior just like any other Ford Focus, the Focus EV delivers the same navigation with SYNC and MyFord Touch, but the cargo room is cut by almost a third, due to the battery placement directly behind the second row seats.</p>
<p>Coming with a base price of $30,692, if you live in California, you can take $2,500 off that price, thanks to government incentives, making this car more desirable. However, even though the price may be a bit steep for what is ultimately a second car, &#8220;The Focus Electric marks the most advanced electric car we have today for the price.&#8221;</p>
<p>Read more of what Jason had to say on the 2013 Ford Focus Electric at his <a target="_blank" href="http://www.automotive.com/ford/focus-electric/2013/road-test/">Road Test</a>.</p>		</div>
