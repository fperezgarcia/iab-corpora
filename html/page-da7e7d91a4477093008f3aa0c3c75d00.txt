	<p>HIGHLIGHTS FOR 2013 SANTA FE XL<br>&#8232;&#9642; Bold ''Fluidic Sculpture'' styling&#8232;<br>&#9642; Lambda II 3.3-litre GDI V6 engine&#8232;	<br>- 290 horsepower, 252 lb-ft of torque&#8232;	<br>- 2,268 kg (5,000 lb) maximum towing capacity&#8232;	<br>- The only mid-size CUV with standard Gasoline Direct Injection (GDI)<br>&#8232;&#9642; 6-speed automatic transmission with SHIFTRONIC® manual mode<br>&#8232;&#9642; 215 mm longer than the 5-passenger Santa Fe&#8232;&#9642; Driver Selectable Steering Mode (DSSM) system&#8232;&#9642; Advanced All-Wheel Drive (AWD) system with Active Cornering Control (ACC)<br>&#8232;&#9642; Seven airbags including driver's knee airbag<br>&#8232;&#9642; Long list of available premium features&#8232;	<br>- Heated steering wheel, heated front and rear seats, ventilated front seats&#8232;	<br>- Power-adjustable front seats with Integrated Memory System (IMS)&#8232;	<br>- Panoramic sunroof&#8232;	<br>- Power liftgate&#8232;&#9642; Seven-passenger seating with 40:20:40 split-folding, sliding, and reclining second-row bench seat or six-passenger seating with second row captain's chairs<br>&#8232;&#9642; 50:50 flat folding third row seats<br>&#8232;&#9642; 145 kg lighter than the outgoing Veracruz, 108 kg lighter than the all-new Nissan Pathfinder<br><br>NO LINES STANDING STILL<br>Hyundai designers crafted the two-row Santa Fe and three-row Santa Fe XL together, with the signature differentiating element being the side window shape. The larger Santa Fe XL features a shape that highlights the increased passenger and cargo room behind the third-row seat. Santa Fe XL also features its own bodyside character lines from the B-pillar back, a unique grille design, new alloy wheels, dual chrome asymmetrical exhaust outlets integrated into the rear bumper, and a flush-mounted tow hitch design.<br><br>LUXURY CAR FEATURES, THREE-ROW PRACTICALITY&#8232; <br>Wth an exhaustive list of standard and optional premium features, the 2013 Santa Fe line-up represents an unprecedented value relative to competitors. Standard equipment includes heated front seats, power windows, driver's seat power lumbar support, a six-speaker audio system, Bluetooth®, a tilt and telescoping steering wheel with audio controls, cruise control, and much more.<br>Also available are luxury features completely unexpected in the segment and unavailable in competing crossovers. Items such as a leather-wrapped heated steering wheel, heated rear seats, leather seating surfaces, and a panoramic sunroof, among others, ensure that all occupants ride in comfort. Also new for 2013 - and standard on all Santa Fe models with cloth seating - is a YES Essentials seat fabric treatment that provides soil-resistant, anti-odour and anti-static properties for added longevity and liveability. For easier parking manoeuvres, a rear-view camera and rear parking sensors are offered. Other high-tech luxury features available on Santa Fe include proximity keyless entry with push-button ignition, approach lighting, dual-zone fully automatic climate control, as well as power driver and passenger seats.<br><br>The Santa Fe XL increases second-row legroom by 50 millimetres and cargo capacity by 156 litres relative to its five-passenger sibling. Also added are standard rear-passenger HVAC controls and vents. Santa Fe XL incorporates a standard 50:50 split-folding third row bench seat with 800 millimetres of legroom. Customers can opt for a 40:20:40 split-folding second row bench seat for seven-passenger versatility or a six-passenger layout with second row captain's chairs. Convenient features like rear under-floor storage and a power-operated liftgate can be found throughout the vehicle.<br><br>THIRD-GENERATION AUDIO AND ENTERTAINMENT &#8232;<br>Santa Fe XL is available with three audio systems. Standard is an AM/FM/Satellite Radio/CD/MP3 audio system with six-speakers and iPod®/USB/auxiliary connectivity. The upgraded audio system boasts a 4.3-inch full colour LCD screen and rear view camera. Finally, the premium navigation system adds a 550-watt Infinity Logic 7® audio system with 12 speakers, including an eight-inch subwoofer, and external amplifier. An XM® satellite radio interface is integrated into each head unit, as is a Bluetooth hands-free phone system with voice recognition, address book download, and audio streaming. Both LCD screen-equipped systems integrate rear-view camera output.<br><br>The optional navigation system includes an 8-inch touch-screen display. This third-generation display features a simpler user interface, enhanced voice recognition commands, and improved navigation screens. The system is more intuitive to use for commonly used tasks. Simple pop up messages now appear to help pair a phone, for example. The voice recognition software also now understands street addresses and cities all in one sentence. The route screens display speed limits and details the next three required manoeuvres. Most functions can now be engaged with only one or two button pushes.<br><br>HIGHLY ADVANCED LAMBDA II 3.3-LITRE GDI V6 ENGINE&#8232;<br>The Santa Fe XL is powered an advanced 3.3-litre GDI V6 engine. Producing 290 horsepower and 252 lb-ft of torque, the XL's new V6 engine provides an ideal combination of power, refinement, and fuel efficiency. The high-pressure Gasoline Direct Injection (GDI) system (over 2,200 psi) allows for a higher-than-normal compression ratio, dramatically increasing power and torque while reducing fuel consumption and emissions. This potent V6 provides a maximum towing capacity of 2,268 kilograms (5,000 pounds), perfect for pulling boats or weekend toys. The three-row Santa Fe's powertrain was tuned at the Hyundai America Technical Center in Ann Arbor, Michigan to ensure it suits the preferences of North American drivers.<br><br>&#8232;SIX-SPEED AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION&#8232; <br>All Santa Fe models come equipped with a six-speed automatic transmission with SHIFTRONIC® manual mode that can adapt quickly to deliver smooth shifts and fuel efficient operation throughout the rev range. An automatic transmission warmer is included to keep the transmission fluid at optimal temperature, improving real-world fuel economy. Additionally, all models feature an ActiveECO system that modifies engine and transmission control to smooth out throttle response and increase real-world fuel economy by five to seven percent.<br><br>ALL-WEATHER CONFIDENCE <br>&#8232;While front-wheel drive is standard equipment, an advanced active All-Wheel Drive (AWD) system is available. This sophisticated electronic AWD system automatically activates under any driving condition as needed, apportioning power in order to optimize traction, and can even anticipate traction requirements. In normal driving, the system distributes power only to the front wheels, helping to reduce fuel consumption. All AWD Santa Fe models feature a control unit that continuously analyzes data from the vehicle controller and actuates the system through a multi-plate clutch. The coupling is fully controllable via an electro-hydraulic actuation system with torque distribution.<br>The AWD system in Santa Fe models incorporates Active Cornering Control (ACC), a new element within the Vehicle Stability Management (VSM) system which helps the vehicle manage acceleration during a turn. To improve traction and cornering performance, braking force is applied to the inside rear wheel when accelerating through a turn.<br><br>SOPHISTICATED CHASSIS, BALANCED RIDE AND HANDLING <br>&#8232;For enhanced handling performance and ride comfort, all Santa Fe models employ a compact MacPherson strut front suspension and a fully-independent multi-link rear suspension. A 26 mm front stabilizer bar increases roll control while reducing weight. A rear stabilizer bar, 21 mm in front-wheel drive models and 19 mm in all-wheel drive models, is also included. The Santa Fe XL rides on 18- or 19-inch alloy wheels.<br><br>Behind the wheel, drivers benefit from Hyundai's all-new Driver Selectable Steering Mode (DSSM) system. With three operating modes - Comfort, Normal and Sport - this innovative system allows drivers to adapt Santa Fe Sport's steering characteristics to varying driving preferences and road conditions. Comfort mode is ideal for city and parking environments, offering the greatest ease of steering with a 10 percent decrease in effort from Normal mode. Normal mode is ideal for a mix of driving conditions. Meanwhile, Sport mode is optimized for higher-speed freeways or winding roads and increases steering effort by 10 percent from Normal mode.<br><br>OVERALL DIMENSIONS <br>&#8232;The Santa Fe XL rides on a 2,800 millimetre wheelbase, 100 millimetres longer than that of the five-passenger Santa Fe. Other key dimensional differences are found throughout.<br><br>IMPROVED RIGIDITY AND ULTRA HIGH TENSILE-STRENGTH STEEL <br>&#8232;The Santa Fe XL's all-new architecture incorporates a new structural design that not only improves rigidity by 16% relative to the previous-generation Santa Fe, but also incorporates a 30-percent increase in the use of ultra high tensile-strength steel to improve collision energy management. Ultra high tensile-strength sheet metal has also been applied to the outer door panels for the first time in the company's history. In addition, anti-corrosion steel was applied to 70 percent of the outer frame and floor structure.<br><br>New hot stamping and roll-forming methods have also been applied to impact members, improving strength while reducing the number of body parts and overall weight. The amount of structural adhesives has been significantly increased for enhanced body rigidity, crashworthiness, and durability.<br><br>WORLD-CLASS WEIGHT EFFICIENCY&#8232; <br>World-class weight efficiency is primary development target for all Hyundai models. Base curb weight for front-wheel drive Santa Fe XL models is 1,790 kilograms, with all-wheel drive models weighing 1,858 kilograms. At 108 kilograms lighter than the all-new Pathfinder and 145 kilograms lighter than the outgoing Veracruz, the Santa Fe XL is able to offer car-like handling, rapid acceleration, and excellent fuel efficiency.<br><br>ADVANCED STANDARD SAFETY TECHNOLOGIES &#8232;<br>Santa Fe models are equipped with an exceptional level of standard safety features for the segment. A Vehicle Stability Management (VSM) system incorporates a Rollover Protection System and optimally manages the ESC (Electronic Stability Control) and the Motor-Driven Power Steering (MDPS) to help the driver maintain control of the vehicle in slippery conditions or during fast cornering. Should the vehicle lose traction, VSM can deliver up to eight Newton-meters of counter-steering effort to help keep it on the road. A Traction Control System (TCS) is also included. <br><br>All Santa Fe models feature seven airbags including driver's knee, dual front-impact, seat-mounted front-side impact, and side curtains. A four-wheel disc braking package provides confident stopping ability. The Anti-lock Braking System (ABS) features Electronic Brake-force Distribution (EBD) and Brake Assist (BA). EBD evaluates the impact of the vehicle's load on weight distribution and adjusts braking force accordingly between the front and rear axles. In a panic stop situation, Brake Assist ensures maximum braking force is applied. As noted, all of these safety features are standard equipment.&#8232;<br><br>Additionally, Santa Fe offers family-friendly passive safety features, including a Lower Anchors and Tethers for Children (LATCH) system for children's seats on the second row and three-point seatbelts for all seating positions.<br></p></td>
