			</div><div style="clear:both"></div><div style="padding-bottom:4px;"></div><p>Ever wish you could bring one of those iconic <a href="http://www.hotwheels.com/index_hwkids.aspx" target="_self">Hot Wheels</a> toys to life? In the case of Jim and Melinda Cooper of Laguna Hills, they have.</p>
<p>That&#8217;s because they own not only a Totally Hot Car, but one that is to be made into a die-cast model by Mattel.</p>
<p>Theirs is a 1956 Chevrolet Handyman Wagon. They entered the car in the PPG/Hot Wheels &#8220;Paint your ride contest,&#8221; and won the grand prize of having the car selected to be made into a toy model.</p>
<p>Underneath the radical paint job is a blown 496-cubic-inch engine and suspension by Air Ride. The couple has owned the car for 15 years, and about a decade ago started an extensive renovation that lasted until this past year.</p>
<p>Their hard work certainly paid off. Be sure to look for this car at local shows &#8212; and on a shelf near you in the future.</p>
<p>Congrats, Jim and Melinda: Your car is Totally Hot!</p>
