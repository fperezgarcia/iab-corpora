<h1>How to Start Buying And Selling of Cars in Nigeria
</h1>
<div class='post-meta'>
<span class='post-author'>

          By:
          <a>Darlinton Omeh</a>
</span> -

        <span class='post-labels'>

          In:
          
<a href='http://www.wealthresult.com/search/label/Buying%20%26%20Selling' rel='tag'>Buying &amp; Selling</a>,
<a href='http://www.wealthresult.com/search/label/Import%20and%20Export' rel='tag'>Import and Export</a>
</span> -
  
<span class='post-comment-link'>
<a class='comment-link' href='http://www.wealthresult.com/2013/08/Buying-Selling-Cars-Nigeria.html#comment-form' onclick=''>0
comments</a>
</span>
</div>
<div class='post-body'>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">If you are looking for really good and cool business you can do in Nigeria, Buying and Selling of Cars should be on top of your list. There will always be opportunity in this business in Nigeria for many reasons -</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">1. Nigeria is and will always be a big market for cars, both used and new ones. I mean what do you expect of a nation of over one hundred and sixty million people with almost everyone working towards owning his own car one day. Those who already have are also working to get additional new ones or change the ones they're having currently.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">Nigerian likes cars a lot, everyone wants to cruise no matter how poor he is. Car is always on top of the list of every family's purchase priority. It is always a dream come true once you're able to acquire your own car and your status will change immediately. Family and friends will respect you, that is why everyone is working towards it.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">2. With the ongoing banning of okada in most states of the federation, even those without the means are being forced to find means of getting their own car.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">3. Transporters are becoming more and more greedy in Nigeria, making public transportation very costly. Therefore, having ones own car has becomes more cost effective and who likes to stand on the bus stop all day waiting for buses is stead of getting to where he is going?</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">4. The profit margin is good too. I can tell you that dealers are making good money in this business, though they always claim of not making enough profit but that is not true, there is huge profit in this business.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<h3>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: large;">This is How The Importation Works</span></span></h3>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">1. Lets say you are buying a 2009 Toyota Camry Leather, you will get good one with low mileage from Maryland for $12,000.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">2. Then you pay the auction fee of $300. Auction fee may be more or less depending on the auction.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">3. You have it towed to the closest port - Baltimore is closer to Maryland, you tow it to Baltimore for onward shipping to Nigeria.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">4. Shipping cost to Nigeria will cost about $1,200 or less, just around that price.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">5. Clearing in Lagos and Courier Bill of Lading to Lagos will stand at N250,000</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><b>Total cost that will land the Toyota Camry 2009 leather for you to Nigeria</b>:</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">USA buying and shipment cost = $13,500.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">Nigeria Clearing Total cost = N250,000.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">That will put the final price of the car to $13,500 x N160 = N2,160,000 + N250,000 clearing = <b>N2,410,000</b>.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;"><tbody>
<tr><td style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-CMx3YwzFHGg/UhoIqRcX36I/AAAAAAAAFJk/ERIPzZdXyXU/s1600/2009-Toyota-Camry.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"><img alt="2009-Toyota-Camry" border="0" height="480" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-CMx3YwzFHGg/UhoIqRcX36I/AAAAAAAAFJk/ERIPzZdXyXU/s640/2009-Toyota-Camry.jpg" title="2009 Toyota Camry" width="640" /></a></span></td></tr>

<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><i><b>Market:</b></i> Search your cars to buy on <a href="http://autos.yahoo.com/" target="_blank">Yahoo Autos</a> there you will get good cars prices. You can as well search at eBay Auction and other <a href="http://wwwa.autotrader.ca/car-buying-and-selling-tips.aspx" target="_blank">Buying and Selling of Cars</a> websites. </span><br />
<h3>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: large;">Selling and Your Profit in Nigeria:</span></span></h3>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">I have gone round and the final cost of getting such Clean Toyota Camry with leather seats and low mileage (under 50,000 mile) anywhere is Nigeria is <b>N2,900,000</b>. Some sell as high as <b>N3,500,000</b></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">Therefore, your profit for the car will be from N500,000 to as much as N1.M depending on how much you finally sells it.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">Toyota Camry is one of the fastest selling cars in Nigeria, so much that you can sell the car within weeks of landing it, though it can stay for months sometimes, but when you finally sell, you are sure of minimum profit of <b>N500,000.</b></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">For Nigerians oversea, this is one of the smartest way of bringing your money back home instead of money transfer that may cost you some charges and may put you in danger of money laundering.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">Use the money you wants to save to buy cars and ship to Nigeria, have a trusted person sell it for you and deposit the money into your account! You will see yourselves in another line of business completely and another stream of income.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">For Nigerian aspiring dealers, this is a great business for you. You can start small with one or two cars and gradually expand into having your own car mart.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><i><b>Note:</b></i> Prices (buying, shipping, and clearing) are not the same. Sometimes you get it cheaper, sometimes you get it higher. Your final sells depend on how much you get your car.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></span>
