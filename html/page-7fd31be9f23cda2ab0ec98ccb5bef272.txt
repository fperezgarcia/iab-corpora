<p><big><strong>Acura NSX Concept</strong></big></p>
<p><strong></strong>Acura excited its fans last year with the debut of the <a href="http://inhabitat.com/naias-2013-acura-brings-its-awesome-nsx-concept-sports-hybrid-back-to-detroit/">Acura NSX concept</a>, which previews the return of Acura&#8217;s supercar. Unlike the first model, which only used a V6 engine, the next NSX will be powered by a hybrid powertrain that combines a 3.5L V6 with three electric motors. One electric motor is integrated with the V-6 engine and two motors driving the front wheels.We&#8217;re still two years away from the debut of the NSX, but <a href="http://www.acura.com">Acura</a> continues to evolve the concept, which was shown at the show with updated styling and a possible design direction for the interior.</p>
<p><a href="http://inhabitat.com/naias-2013-seven-sizzling-hybrid-and-electric-cars-from-the-detroit-auto-show/2014-cadillac-elr-naias-0008/" rel="attachment wp-att-476278"><img class="alignnone size-large wp-image-476278" title="2014-Cadillac-ELR-NAIAS-0008" src="http://assets.inhabitat.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/1/files/2013/01/2014-Cadillac-ELR-NAIAS-0008-537x359.jpg" alt="Acura, Acura NSX Concept, Cadillac, Cadillac ELR, Infiniti, Infiniiti Q50, Lexus, Lexus IS 300h, Nissan Leaf, Nissan Resonance Concept, VW, VW CrossBlue Concept, 2013 Detroit Auto Show" width="537" height="359" /></a>.</p>
<p><big><strong>2014 Cadillac ELR</strong></big></p>
<p><strong></strong>The <a href="http://inhabitat.com/2014-cadillac-elr-extended-range-ev-to-debut-today-at-detroit-auto-show/">2014 Cadillac ELR</a> was one of the biggest debuts at the Detroit Auto Show. The ELR borrows its electric powertrain from the <a href="http://www.chevy.com">Chevy</a> Volt, but gets sexier coupe styling. The 2014 <a href="http://www.cadillac.com">Cadillac</a> ELR was also won the &#8220;Eyes On Design Best Production Vehicle Award&#8221; at the show. It arrives in early 2014 and will be able to travel up to 35 miles in full electric mode. A 1.4L range extender engine kicks in once the lithium-ion battery is depleted, giving the ELR a total driving range around 300 miles.</p>
