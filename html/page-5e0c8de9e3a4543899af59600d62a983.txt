<FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">
<FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: bold 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">
<FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: bold italic 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">There's no business like show business</FONT>, and with a Lubin BBA in Arts and Entertainment Management, you will gain the skills you need to succeed in the <FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: bold italic 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">business</FONT> of arts and entertainment.</BR>
You can:
</FONT>
<UL STYLE="list-style-type: disc">
<FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">
<LI> launch an <FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: bold 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">exciting and fulfilling career</FONT> with opportunities in performing arts and visual arts organizations
<LI> <FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: bold 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">manage</FONT> in theatre companies, museums, dance companies, contemporary and classical music organizations, art galleries, talent representation and management companies, and television production firms
<LI> see Broadway shows, visit headquarters of entertainment companies, and <FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: bold 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">meet leaders in arts organizations</FONT>
</UL>
</FONT>
Lubin's Arts and Entertainment Management concentration:
<BR>
<UL STYLE="list-style-type: disc">
<FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">
<LI> is one of only two <A HREF="http://www.aacsb.edu/aacsb-accredited/" target="_blank">AACSB-accredited programs</A> in New York State
<LI> leverages our <FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: bold 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">location</FONT> in New York City and <FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: bold 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">industry contacts</FONT> to bring in stellar guest lecturers including
<BR><BR>
<UL STYLE="list-style-type: disc">

<LI> Katie Couric, ABC News special correspondent and syndicated talk show host

<LI> Doug Herzog, President, Viacom Entertainment Group
<LI> Rachel Moore, Executive Director, American Ballet Theater
<LI> Arlene Shuler, President and CEO, New York City Center
<LI> Robert Wankel, Co-CEO and President, The Shubert Organization
</FONT>
</UL>

</FONT>
</UL>
And Pace is home to Bravo TV's long-running <FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: bold italic 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">Inside the Actors Studio</FONT>, a 15-time Emmy nominated series that is broadcast in 125 countries around the world reaching 89 million homes. Recent guests interviewed by host James Lipton include Jennifer Aniston, Jim Carrey, George Clooney, Colin Firth, James Franco, Brad Pitt, and members of the casts of <FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: italic 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">Glee</FONT>, <FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: italic 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">Mad Men</FONT>, and <FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: italic 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">Modern Family</FONT>.

15,000<BR></FONT>
</BLOCKQUOTE>

There are over 15,000 media and entertainment companies headquartered in New York City</FONT>
(<FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: italic 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">Source: New York City Economic Development Corporation</FONT>). In 2010, the nonprofit arts and culture industry in the United States generated $135.2 billion in economic activity, supporting 4.1 million jobs, according to <A HREF="http://www.americansforthearts.org/pdf/information_services/research/services/economic_impact/aepiv/AEP4_NationalHighlightsBrochure.pdf" target="_blank">Americans for the Arts</A>.

<BR>
<UL STYLE="list-style-type: disc"><FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">

<LI> <FONT style="FILTER: ; FONT: bold 16px Arial,Geneva,sans-serif; COLOR: #000000">Formal and systematic</FONT> <span id="qtip-link-2" class="qtip-link qtip-link-odd" title="Gain methods of directing and motivating group efforts toward achieving the organization's primary objective: the optimal allocation of resources.">management methods.</span></FONT>
</UL>
