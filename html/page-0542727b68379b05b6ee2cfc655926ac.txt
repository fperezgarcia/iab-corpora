<div class="entry-content">
<div class="entry_widget_large entry_widget_left">
<form class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-photo" style="display: inline;" mt:asset-id="8906541"><span class="adv-photo-large"><img class="adv-photo" alt="Coda.JPG" src="http://media.cleveland.com/business_impact/photo/codajpg-a14f0db9082dacb7_large.jpg"><span class="photo-data"><span class="byline">Coda Automotive</span><span class="caption">Coda Automotive's 2012 electric car is getting closer to production. If it can get federal loans, the California automaker will build a plant in Columbus to make batteries for its vehicles.</span></span><span class="photo-bottom-left"><!-- --></span><span class="photo-bottom-right"><!-- --></span></span></form></div>
<p>CLEVELAND, Ohio -- California's <a href="http://www.codaautomotive.com/">Coda Automotive</a>, a company that may eventually make batteries in Columbus for its electric cars, is getting closer to selling its vehicles. </p>
<p>"We believe it's crucial for the world to move away from oil for transportation," Coda Chief Executive Kevin Czinger said Tuesday during a conference call. He added that making cars in California could be good for the U.S. economy. </p>
<p>"Almost all of the electric drive and safety components, excluding the battery system which we design and manufacture, are coming from U.S. suppliers," Czinger said. </p>
<p>Suppliers in Northeast Ohio could get some of the benefit from Coda's vehicles. If it can win federal loans, Coda plans to build a plant in Columbus to make batteries for its electric cars, and that could mean work for some of the smaller plastics and metals companies in this area. </p>
<p>"I would be very surprised if there wasn't some impact in this part of the state," said Greg Krizman, spokesman for <a href="http://www.magnetwork.org/">Magnet, an advocacy group for Northeast Ohio manufacturers</a>. </p>
<p>"Any automotive company that locates in Ohio would be beneficial to the" companies that make the parts that bigger suppliers such as Lear, Continental and Delphi sell to automakers, Krizman said. "They're going to need things produced that will go into their batteries. </p>
<p>Coda plans to enter the <a href="http://www.cleveland.com/business/index.ssf/2010/06/in_electric_era_auto_industry.html">increasingly competitive electric car market </a>by the end of next year, selling initially in California before spreading out to other states. </p>
<p>For the first year, at least, the company will import its batteries from a plant in China owned by one of its business partners. But if the company can win U.S. Department of Energy loans, Czinger said Coda would build a 1,000-person plant in Columbus. </p>
<p>If it gets the grant and builds that factory, Czinger said Coda would import battery cells from China to Columbus for a year and have employees in Ohio integrate those cells into vehicle battery packs. </p>
<p>"In the next year, we would do the entire manufacturing of the cell in the factory and the finishing of the pack there as well," Czinger said. </p>
<p>The state approved about $100 million in tax incentives for Coda in May. The city of Columbus approved a set of local incentives earlier this week. Still, the project won't get started without the federal loans. </p>
<p>Approved at the urging of President George W. Bush's administration in 2007, the $25 billion loan program is intended to help companies build plants and upgrade existing facilities to build more fuel-efficient cars. </p>
<p><a href="http://www.cleveland.com/business/index.ssf/2009/06/ford_to_get_59_billion_to_deve.html">Ford Motor Co. has been the biggest recipient of loans </a>at about $6 billion. Much of that went to the company's engine plants in Brook Park and Lima and a transmission plant near Cincinnati. <a href="http://www.cleveland.com/business/index.ssf/2010/05/nissan_breaks_ground_on_batter.html">Nissan </a>and electric car company Tesla also received federal loans. </p>
<p>General Motors and Coda have applied, but the government is still considering their proposals. </p>
<p>Czinger said company officials had to sign confidentiality agreements with the Energy Department, so he could not say how far along the process is for the loans. </p>
<p>Coda's plan to make both its battery cells and battery packs is more ambitious than many of its competitors. General Motors, for example, plans to assemble its own battery packs for the Chevrolet Volt at <a href="http://www.cleveland.com/business/index.ssf/2009/08/gm_to_assemble_volt_battery_pa.html">a plant south of Detroit</a>, but the company is buying its sells from Korea's LG Chem. </p>
<p>Ford plans to assemble its battery packs, but it plans to get the cells from suppliers who are <a href="http://www.cleveland.com/business/index.ssf/2010/09/a123_opens_new_battery_factory.html">building plants near Detroit</a>. </p>
<p>Czinger said the Coda is still about a year away. That could put it at a disadvantage to competitors such as the Volt, which will be out by the end of this year and <a href="http://www.cleveland.com/business/index.ssf/2010/03/nissans_electric_car_the_leaf.html">Nissan's Leaf electric car</a>, also expected by year's end. </p>
<p>The Leaf will also be cheaper than the Coda, but Czinger said he's not worried. His vehicle can go farther on a charge, and it will be able to operate well in extremely cold climates, a problem for many electric vehicles. </p>

function emailCheck (emailStr) {

   		,emailPat=/^(.+)@(.+)$/
		,specialChars="\\(\\)><@,;:\\\\\\\"\\.\\[\\]"
		,validChars="\[^\\s" + specialChars + "\]"

		,atom=validChars + '+'
		,word="(" + atom + "|" + quotedUser + ")"
		,userPat=new RegExp("^" + word + "(\\." + word + ")*$")
		,domainPat=new RegExp("^" + atom + "(\\." + atom +")*$")
		,matchArray=emailStr.match(emailPat)
		;

	if (matchArray==null) return false;
   
	var user=matchArray[1]
   		,domain=matchArray[2];

		if (user.charCodeAt(i)>127) return false;
	}
	for (i=0; i<domain.length; i++) {
		if (domain.charCodeAt(i)>127) return false;

   return true;
}
