<h1>Aftermarket, Performance and Add-On Parts </h1><p id="updated">This page last reviewed April 15, 2010</p>
<img src="http://www.arb.ca.gov/msprog/aftermkt/parts.jpg" class="right" alt="parts sign" height="170" width="197" />
<h3><a name="background"></a>Background:</h3>
<strong>What's under the hood is
important to air quality. &nbsp;</strong>Automotive emissions
account for over 50 percent of all smog-forming pollutants in
California. To improve air&nbsp;quality, the California Air
Resources Board (ARB) requires vehicle manufacturers to develop engine
and emission equipment systems that&nbsp;reduce
the&nbsp;specific pollutants that cause California's severe air
quality problem. These emission control systems are
also&nbsp;required to be proven durable and reliable.
<br />
<br />
To ensure that these systems operate as designed, California Vehicle Code
Section 27156 and the Federal Clean Air Act prohibit modifications that
increase motor vehicle emissions. Since if properly designed, most
performance modifications do&nbsp;not&nbsp;increase vehicle
emissions, these same laws also allow the installation of parts or
modifications proven by their manufacturers and the ARB not to increase
vehicle emissions.
<br />
<br />
<h3>All aftermarket parts sold in
California belong to one of the following four groups:</h3>
<strong>1. &nbsp; Replacement Parts
-&nbsp;</strong><a href="replace.htm">Replacement Parts
Guide</a><br /><strong>2. &nbsp; Legal Add-On or
Modified Parts (Executive Order Parts) - &nbsp;</strong><a href="http://www.arb.ca.gov/msprog/aftermkt/devices/amquery.php"> Aftermarket Parts
Database</a><br /><strong>3. &nbsp; Competition or Racing
Use Only Parts - &nbsp;</strong><a href="replace.htm#racing">Exemptions for Uncontrolled Vehicles</a><br /><strong>4. &nbsp; <a href="/msprog/aftermktcat/aftermktcat.htm">Catalytic Converters</a></strong>
<br />
<br />
<h3><a name="additional"></a>Additional Information for
Manufacturers of Aftermarket Parts:</h3>
An exemption from Vehicle Code Section 27156 (VC
27156), California's anti-tampering law, is required before any add-on
or modified part can be sold in California.&nbsp;<span style="color: red;"></span>&nbsp;Manufacturers interested in
obtaining a VC&nbsp;27156 exemption for an add-on or a modified
part should fill-out and submit an <a href="./forms/forms.htm">application form</a> to the ARB for
evaluation. Detailed information on how to do this can be obtained from
the document, "Procedures for Exemption of Add-On and Modified Parts,"
amended June 1, 1990. <a href="vc27156procedures.pdf">(PDF - 2.54MB)</a>
<br />
<br />
<h3><a name="heavyduty"></a>Heavy-Duty Diesel Retrofit Device
Emission Reduction Verification:</h3>
Recently, the Air Resources Board initiated a
number of incentive programs to offer funds for
retrofitting&nbsp;existing heavy-duty engines in public and private
fleets to reduce PM and NOx emissions. Retrofit systems to be used in
any PM or NOx incentive&nbsp;program must be evaluated and
certified for their&nbsp;emission&nbsp;reducing capabilities.
Applicants who plan to participate in&nbsp;any of the ARB's
retrofit programs may obtain additional information by viewing the
ARB's <a href="http://www.arb.ca.gov/diesel/mobile.htm">Diesel Mobile Programs</a> website<span style="text-decoration: underline;">.</span>
<br />
